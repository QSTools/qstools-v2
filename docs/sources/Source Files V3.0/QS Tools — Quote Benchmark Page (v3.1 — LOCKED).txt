# QS Tools — Quote Benchmark Page (v3.1 — LOCKED)

STATUS: CORE SYSTEM MODULE (REAL-WORLD VALIDATION LAYER)

---

## PURPOSE

The Quote Benchmark page compares a real quote against the business model.

It exists to:

* check whether a quoted job aligns with the business baseline
* show whether the quote supports or weakens the business model
* connect real-world pricing decisions back to the business reality
* give the user visibility before work is accepted

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

> The quote is entered in the user’s normal format.
> The system translates it and shows what it means.

---

## SYSTEM ROLE

Quote Benchmark is the **real-world validation layer**.

Position in system:

Rate Models
↓
Production Capacity
↓
Recovery Outcome
↓
**Quote Benchmark**

---

## INPUTS (LOCKED)

### 1. Required Rates

From:

* Rate Models

Includes:

* required earning rates
* rate structure

---

### 2. Business Reality

From:

* Recovery Outcome

Includes:

* overall business status
* pressure level
* whether the model is working, tight, or not working

---

### 3. Production Reality

From:

* Production Capacity

Includes:

* time pressure
* delivery capacity
* whether additional work increases strain

---

### 4. User Quote Input

The user enters the quote in the way they already think about it.

Examples:

* total job value
* hourly rate
* m² rate
* unit rate

---

## SYSTEM BEHAVIOUR

The system must:

* convert the quote into a comparable format
* compare it against the required rate model
* show what the quote means to the business
* show whether taking the job improves or worsens the position

---

## OUTPUTS (LOCKED)

### 1. Quote Position

* Above baseline
* Near baseline
* Below baseline

---

### 2. Pricing Impact

* whether the quote meets the required rate
* whether it under-recovers or over-recovers

---

### 3. Capacity Impact

* whether the quote adds manageable work
* whether it increases pressure on production capacity
* whether it pushes the system into a tight or unsustainable state

---

### 4. Business Impact

* helps the business
* keeps the business tight
* weakens the business

---

## KEY FUNCTION

This page answers:

> “Given my business model, is this quote helping me or hurting me?”

---

## WHAT THIS PAGE DOES NOT DO

* does not create the quote
* does not replace the user’s quoting process
* does not give instructions
* does not decide whether the user should take the job

---

## INTERPRETATION RULE

The page must show:

* what the quote means financially
* what the quote means operationally
* what the quote means for the business overall

It must NOT:

* prescribe actions
* override judgement

---

## UX RULE

The user must not be required to:

* do mental maths
* manually convert units
* interpret raw model outputs alone

The system must do the translation and present the result clearly.

---

## STATUS TYPES

### ✔ Supports the Model

The quote aligns with required rates and fits within realistic production capacity.

---

### ⚠ Tight

The quote is possible, but leaves limited room for error financially or operationally.

---

### ❌ Weakens the Model

The quote under-recovers, increases pressure, or pushes the business beyond realistic delivery conditions.

---

## DEFINITION

The Quote Benchmark page is the real-world validation layer of QS Tools. It compares actual quotes against the business model and shows whether a piece of work supports or weakens the business financially and operationally.

---

## FINAL LINE

> This is where your quote meets your business reality.
