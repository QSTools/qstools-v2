# QS Tools — Rate Models Page (v3.1 — LOCKED)

STATUS: CORE SYSTEM MODULE (PRICING LAYER)

---

## PURPOSE

The Rate Models page converts the business model into real-world earning requirements.

It exists to:

* define how the business must generate revenue
* translate financial requirements into usable rates
* ensure pricing aligns with the business model
* create a clear connection between costs and how work is charged

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

> Rate Models defines how you must earn — not whether you can deliver it.

---

## SYSTEM ROLE

Rate Models is the **pricing layer** of the system.

Position in system:

Cost Summary
↓
Business Model
↓
Recovery Summary
↓
**Rate Models**
↓
Production Capacity

---

## INPUTS (LOCKED)

### 1. Required Revenue

From:

* Cost Summary

Definition:

* total revenue required for the business to operate correctly

---

### 2. Cost Structure

From:

* Cost Modules

Includes:

* labour
* materials
* overheads
* assets

---

### 3. Business Model Settings

From:

* Business Model
* Recovery Summary

Includes:

* labour vs material structure
* recovery approach
* margin targets

---

## OUTPUTS (LOCKED)

Rate Models must produce clear, usable earning metrics such as:

---

### 1. Primary Rates

Examples:

* $ per hour
* $ per m²
* $ per unit
* $ per crew week

---

### 2. Revenue Distribution Insight

* how revenue is generated across:

  * labour
  * materials
  * other components

---

### 3. Recovery Alignment

* whether the defined rates:

  * meet required revenue
  * align with cost structure

---

## KEY FUNCTION

This page answers:

> “When I go out and do work, what do I need to charge for my business to work?”

---

## CRITICAL DISTINCTION

### Rate Models vs Production Capacity

* Rate Models → defines **pricing requirements**
* Production Capacity → defines **delivery capability**

---

## WHAT THIS PAGE DOES NOT DO

* does not check time availability
* does not assess workload or capacity
* does not model job delivery
* does not validate quotes

---

## INTERPRETATION

The outputs from this page must be:

* practical
* usable in real-world pricing
* directly relatable to how the user charges work

---

## RULES

### 1. No Capacity Logic

This page must NOT include:

* time calculations
* utilisation
* delivery constraints

---

### 2. No Prescriptive Advice

This page must NOT:

* tell the user what to charge as a decision
* enforce a strategy

It must only:

* reflect what the business requires

---

### 3. Direct Output

Rates must be:

* clear
* simple
* actionable

---

## DEFINITION

The Rate Models page converts the financial and structural requirements of the business into clear, real-world earning rates that define how the business must generate revenue.

---

## FINAL LINE

> This is how your business needs to earn.
