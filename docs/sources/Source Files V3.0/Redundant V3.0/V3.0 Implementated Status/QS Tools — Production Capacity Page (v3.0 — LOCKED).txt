# QS Tools — Production Capacity Page (v3.0 — LOCKED)

STATUS: CORE SYSTEM MODULE (OPERATIONAL REALITY LAYER)

---

## PURPOSE

The Production Capacity page converts required revenue into time and checks whether the business can realistically deliver that level of work.

It exists to:

* translate revenue into required time
* compare required time to available time
* reveal whether the business is constrained, balanced, or underutilised
* expose pressure and sustainability risk

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

> Revenue becomes time. Time reveals the truth.

---

## SYSTEM ROLE

Production Capacity is the **operational reality layer**.

Position in system:

Cost Summary
↓
Rate Models
↓
Production Capacity
↓
Recovery Outcome

---

## INPUTS (LOCKED)

### 1. Required Revenue

Source:

* Cost Summary

Definition:

* total revenue required for the business to operate correctly

---

### 2. Production Rate

Definition:

* how quickly the business converts time into revenue

Examples:

* $ per week
* $ per hour
* $ per crew week
* $ per production unit (normalised)

Notes:

* this must reflect real-world output, not theoretical output
* it inherently includes labour, materials, subcontracting, and efficiency

---

### 3. Available Time

Definition:

* total working time available in the selected period

Examples:

* weeks per year
* hours per month

---

## CALCULATIONS (LOCKED)

### 1. Time Required

time_required = required_revenue ÷ production_rate

---

### 2. Time Gap

time_gap = available_time − time_required

---

### 3. Utilisation Ratio

utilisation = time_required ÷ available_time

---

## OUTPUTS (LOCKED)

### 1. Time Required

* total time needed to achieve required revenue

---

### 2. Time Available

* total working time available

---

### 3. Capacity Status

* ✔ Capacity Available → time_required < available_time
* ⚠ Tight → time_required ≈ available_time
* ❌ Constrained → time_required > available_time

---

### 4. Intensity (Pressure Level)

* Comfortable → low utilisation
* High Effort → elevated utilisation
* Unsustainable → extreme utilisation

---

## KEY INSIGHT

This page answers:

> “Can this business realistically produce the revenue it needs over time?”

---

## WHAT THIS PAGE DOES NOT DO

* does not model individual jobs
* does not allocate assets
* does not simulate workflows
* does not provide solutions

---

## CRITICAL RULE

> If capacity can be bought (subcontracting / hire), it is not a constraint — it is part of the production rate.

---

## DEFINITION

The Production Capacity page converts required revenue into time and compares it against available time to reveal whether the business can realistically achieve its target output.
