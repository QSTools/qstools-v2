---

## OBJECTIVE

The Cost Summary module defines the **true internal cost baseline of the business**.

It answers:

* What does the business cost to run?
* What revenue is required to sustain it?
* What is the required recovery rate per productive hour?

This module is **not analytical** and does not drive decisions.
It provides a **clear, structured financial baseline**.

---

## CORE ROLE (LOCKED)

Cost Summary = **WHAT the business costs**

It does NOT:

* validate performance (P&L does this)
* allocate cost (Cost Allocation does this)
* determine pricing strategy

---

## FINAL PAGE STRUCTURE (LOCKED)

Single full-width card. No right rail.

### 1. Header

* Kicker: Cost Baseline
* Title: Cost Summary
* Supporting line:

  * Insight (if available)
  * Else: "Defines the current cost burden and required recovery rate."

---

### 2. Total Cost Composition (PRIMARY BLOCK)

Top-level cost breakdown.

Displays:

* Labour / People Cost (value + %)
* Assets (value + %)
* General Overheads (value + %)
* Total Cost Burden (value only — strong emphasis)

Below this:

#### Revenue per Productive Hour (PRIMARY METRIC)

* Large display:

  * `$ / hr`
* Supporting:

  * "Based on X productive hours"

Rules:

* This is the **primary commercial signal**
* Must be the most visually dominant number
* No additional logic introduced
* Derived from:

  * total_cost_burden
  * total_productive_output

---

### 3. People Cost

Displays:

* Total People Cost (primary)
* Gross Wages
* Entitlements
* Employer KiwiSaver
* ESCT
* Employer Contribution Total
* Employee Overheads

Includes:

* Staff drilldown (toggle)
* Sorted by highest cost first

Rules:

* Table layout only
* No duplication of totals from other sections
* No new calculations

---

### 4. Business Cost

Displays:

* Total Business Cost (primary)
* Asset Cost
* General Overheads

Includes:

* Drilldown (Assets + Overheads)

Rules:

* Label standardised to:
  → "Non-people cost burden"
* Table layout consistent with People Cost

---

### 5. Commercial Baseline (SECONDARY CONTEXT)

Displays:

* Total Cost Burden (no strong emphasis)
* Break-even Revenue
* Required Recovery Rate
* Total Productive Output

Rules:

* This section provides context only
* Must not compete visually with the top summary
* No duplication of primary emphasis

---

## UI / DESIGN RULES (LOCKED)

* Full-width layout
* No right rail
* No duplicate summary cards
* Table layout (`labour-summary-table`) used consistently
* Sections wrapped in `ui-panel`
* Hierarchy:

  * Composition → Detail → Commercial

### Visual hierarchy:

1. Revenue per Productive Hour (dominant)
2. Total Cost Burden (top section)
3. Section totals
4. Supporting rows

---

## DATA RULES (LOCKED)

Only use existing outputs:

* total_cost_burden
* required_revenue
* required_recovery_rate
* total_productive_output
* people_cost_total
* asset_cost_total
* general_overheads_total

Allowed:

* % calculations from totals (composition only)

Not allowed:

* new business logic
* derived modelling
* predictive or advisory outputs

---

## INSIGHT RULE (LOCKED)

Single-line only.

Examples:

* "Labour is the dominant cost driver."
* "Assets are a major cost driver."
* "General overheads are a significant cost driver."

Must:

* be derived from existing values
* contain no system language
* contain no explanation

---

* charts / graphs
* P&L comparisons

* recommendations

* Single card, full width

## FINAL DEFINITION

The Cost Summary module is a clean, structured representation of the business’s internal cost baseline, presenting total cost composition, detailed cost breakdowns, and the required recovery rate per productive hour in a single, unified, non-analytical view.
