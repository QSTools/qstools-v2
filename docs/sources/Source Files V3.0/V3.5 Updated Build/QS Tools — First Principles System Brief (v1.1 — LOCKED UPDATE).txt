# QS Tools — First Principles System Brief (v1.1 — LOCKED UPDATE)

---

## 🔒 NEW CORE RULE — ASSUMPTION DISCIPLINE (CRITICAL)

The system must treat assumptions as **explicit, visible, and controlled elements** — never hidden.

---

## ASSUMPTION RULE (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Whenever an assumption exists, the system MUST:

1. **State the assumption clearly**
2. **Explain why the assumption exists**
3. **Quantify the impact where possible**
4. **Attempt to remove or reduce the assumption**
5. **Expose the remaining assumption to the user**

---

## REQUIRED STRUCTURE FOR ALL ASSUMPTIONS

Every assumption must follow this format internally:

---

### Assumption

What is being assumed?

---

### Why it exists

Why is this assumption required?

* data limitation
* modelling simplification
* real-world constraint
* user input dependency

---

### Impact

What happens if this assumption is wrong?

* overstates recovery
* understates cost
* distorts utilisation
* hides risk

---

### Mitigation

What has been done to reduce or eliminate it?

* structural change
* additional input
* alternative calculation
* validation logic

---

### Residual Risk

What still cannot be eliminated?

This must remain visible.

---

## SYSTEM BEHAVIOUR RULE

The system must NEVER:

* silently rely on assumptions
* bury assumptions inside calculations
* hide assumptions behind UI
* treat assumptions as facts

---

## DESIGN REQUIREMENT

Where feasible, assumptions must be:

### Eliminated by:

* introducing better structure
* separating concepts (e.g. capacity vs realised hours)
* using real data instead of derived estimates

---

### Reduced by:

* adding controls (e.g. utilisation %)
* allowing overrides (e.g. owner salary)
* splitting blended values (e.g. labour vs margin recovery)

---

### Exposed by:

* warnings
* insight panels
* validation messages
* explicit labels

---

## IMPLEMENTATION STANDARD

Every module must include:

* **Assumption awareness in calculations**
* **Validation or warning logic**
* **User-visible feedback where assumptions remain**

---

## EXAMPLES (LOCKED INTO SYSTEM)

---

### Example — Productive Hours

Assumption:

* all productive hours are usable

Mitigation:

* split into capacity vs realised hours

Residual:

* realised hours still depend on demand

---

### Example — Owner Salary

Assumption:

* P&L reflects true labour cost

Mitigation:

* force owner as fully costed role

Residual:

* market salary may still be estimated

---

### Example — Recovery Model

Assumption:

* recovery comes from labour only

Mitigation:

* introduce labour + margin recovery

Residual:

* margin variability risk remains

---

## FINAL RULE

**No assumption may exist in QS Tools without being:**

* named
* explained
* challenged
* reduced
* and made visible

---

## LOCKED STATEMENT

**“If an assumption exists, it must be visible.
If it can be removed, it must be removed.
If it cannot be removed, it must be understood.”**
