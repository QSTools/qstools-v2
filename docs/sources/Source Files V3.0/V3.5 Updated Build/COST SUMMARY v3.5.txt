# QS Tools — Cost Summary Module (v3.5 — LOCKED)

STATUS: PURE COST AGGREGATION LAYER

---

## PURPOSE

The Cost Summary Module defines what the business costs to run.

It aggregates stable module outputs into:

- People Cost
- Business Cost
- Total Cost Burden
- Required Revenue
- Required Recovery Rate

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

Cost Summary = WHAT the business costs.

Recovery Summary = HOW the business recovers it.

---

## SYSTEM ROLE

Cost Summary is the pure aggregation layer.

It consumes upstream module outputs only.

---

## PEOPLE COST BUILD

People Cost includes Labour only.

total_people_cost_annual =
total_gross_wages_annual
+ total_entitlements_annual
+ total_employer_kiwisaver_annual
+ total_esct_annual

Employer Contribution Total may be displayed as a subtotal:

total_employer_contribution_annual =
total_employer_kiwisaver_annual + total_esct_annual

---

## BUSINESS COST BUILD

total_business_overheads =
total_general_overheads

total_business_cost_annual =
total_asset_cost_annual + total_business_overheads

---

## TOTAL COST BURDEN BUILD

total_cost_burden =
total_people_cost_annual
+ total_asset_cost_annual
+ total_business_overheads

required_revenue =
total_cost_burden

required_recovery_rate =
required_revenue / total_productive_output

If total_productive_output = 0:

required_recovery_rate = 0

warning required.

## READINESS BEHAVIOUR

If model_ready = false:

- totals may display
- system must be marked as not trusted
- downstream modelling is blocked

---

## CORE RULES

Cost Summary must NOT:

- rebuild Labour maths
- rebuild Asset maths
- rebuild General Overheads maths
- consume raw P&L lines
- use benchmark totals in calculations
- consume Employee Overheads
- consume Scenario outputs
- define recovery strategy
- calculate pricing
- distribute costs

---

## FINAL DEFINITION

The Cost Summary Module v3.5 is the pure business cost aggregation layer. It combines Labour, Assets, and General Overheads into total_cost_burden and required_revenue without distributing, assigning, or interpreting recovery.
