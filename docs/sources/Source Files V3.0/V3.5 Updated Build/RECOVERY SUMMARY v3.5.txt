# QS Tools — Recovery Summary Page (v3.5 — LOCKED)

STATUS: BUSINESS STRUCTURE / RECOVERY LAYER

---

## PURPOSE

The Recovery Summary page defines how the business is structured to recover costs and generate revenue.

It shows how the business is designed to earn.

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

Recovery Summary defines how the business is structured to earn — not whether it can.

---

## SYSTEM ROLE

Recovery Summary sits downstream of Cost Summary and upstream of Rate Models.

Cost Summary
↓
Recovery Summary
↓
Rate Models

---

## INPUTS

From Cost Summary:

- total_cost_burden
- required_revenue
- required_recovery_rate
- total_productive_output
- total_people_cost_annual
- total_asset_cost_annual
- total_business_overheads

---

## LOCAL INPUTS

- recovery_model
- labour_share_percent
- asset_share_percent
- material_share_percent
- overhead_share_percent

Allowed recovery_model values:

- labour_only
- asset_driven
- material_margin
- hybrid

---

## OUTPUTS

- active_recovery_model
- labour_share_percent
- asset_share_percent
- material_share_percent
- overhead_share_percent
- labour_recovery_cost
- asset_recovery_cost
- material_recovery_cost
- overhead_absorbed_cost
- required_labour_recovery_rate
- required_asset_recovery
- required_material_margin_recovery

---

## RULES

Recovery Summary must NOT:

- rebuild upstream costs
- consume raw Labour state
- consume raw Asset state
- consume raw General Overheads state
- calculate final charge rates
- assess capacity
- validate quotes

---

## FINAL DEFINITION

Recovery Summary v3.5 defines how the business intends to recover its cost burden through labour, assets, materials, and overhead absorption, while leaving pricing, capacity, and validation to downstream layers.
