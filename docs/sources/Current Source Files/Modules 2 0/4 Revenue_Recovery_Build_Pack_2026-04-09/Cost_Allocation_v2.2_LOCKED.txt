QS Tools — Cost Allocation (v2.2 — LOCKED)
PURPOSE

The Cost Allocation Module is the micro operating-unit page.

It validates how resources are assembled into delivery units and shows whether each configured unit appears commercially workable.

It answers:

what works together
what must work together
what labour basis is attached to each unit
whether each unit appears:
unhealthy
borderline
healthy
SYSTEM ROLE (CRITICAL)

Cost Allocation is a:

structural + operational + micro health visibility layer

It must:

consume Recovery Summary outputs
consume Labour identities and outputs
consume Asset identities and outputs
define capability links
define operational groups
show labour basis per unit
show simple unit health signals

It must NOT:

calculate total business cost
calculate full recovery strategy
calculate final business outcome
own staff_id
own asset_id
CORE PRINCIPLE

Recovery Summary = intended recovery strategy
Cost Allocation = how delivery units are assembled and whether they look workable
Recovery Outcome = whether the total business model works

LABOUR BASIS RULE

Cost Allocation must not own a manual generic labour rate.

It must reference Labour-owned roles/categories.

Examples:

Machine Driver
Pump Operator
Digger Driver

The page must make it clear:
if the user wants to change the labour basis, they must go back to Labour.

DEFINITION

Cost Allocation v2.2 is the micro operating-unit and structural readiness page. It shows how units are assembled, what labour basis they rely on, and whether each unit appears commercially workable without becoming the final business verdict page.
