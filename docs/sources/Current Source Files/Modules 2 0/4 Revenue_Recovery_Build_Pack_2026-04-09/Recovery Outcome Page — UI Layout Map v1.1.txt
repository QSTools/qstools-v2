1. RecoveryOutcomeStatusStrip

Show:

outcome_status

Fast top-line answer:
“Is this business model working?”

2. RecoveryOutcomeMainCard
Section A — Outcome banner

Show:

outcome_status
outcome_title
outcome_message
recommended_action

This must be the most prominent section on the page.

Section B — Cost baseline block

Show:

total_cost_burden
required_revenue
required_recovery_rate
total_productive_output

This anchors the whole decision.

Section C — Recovery strategy context

Show:

active_recovery_model
labour_share_percent
asset_share_percent
overhead_share_percent
required_labour_recovery_rate
required_asset_recovery
Section D — Recovery streams block

Uses:

RecoveryOutcomeRecoveryStreamsCard

Show each stream:

labour recovery stream
asset-linked recovery stream
material margin stream
square metre stream
volume stream

For each stream show:

stream name
contribution value
contribution % where useful
health or adequacy note

This is the heart of the macro reconciliation.

Section E — Structure summary block

Show:

structure_valid
staff_coverage_percent
asset_coverage_percent
group_coverage_percent
linked/unlinked counts
valid/invalid groups

This keeps structure visible, but secondary to the outcome.

Section F — Macro driver block

Uses:

RecoveryOutcomeDriverCard

Show:

dominant_recovery_stream
macro_driver_title
macro_driver_message
primary_constraint_title
primary_constraint_message

Examples:

“Material margin is carrying the model.”
“Labour recovery is too weak for total cost burden.”
“Structure is sound, but revenue streams are insufficient.”
“The business is viable but too dependent on one recovery stream.”
Section G — Warnings block

Combined warnings from:

Recovery Summary
Materials
Rate Models
Cost Allocation

Grouped cleanly, not noisy.

3. RecoveryOutcomeHelpPanel

Collapsed by default.

Explain:

this is the macro business verdict page
it combines cost, recovery, revenue streams, and structure
this page tells you whether the model works
it is not the place to author rates or structure
go back upstream to change assumptions
Recommended page feel
Materials

Simple, calm, accounting-like.

Square Metre / Volume Rate pages

Commercial modelling feel.
A bit more “what do I sell and at what rate?”

Cost Allocation

Operational planning feel.
“What units do I actually run?”

Recovery Outcome

Decision-engine feel.
“Is the whole business model commercially viable?”

Strong build order from here
Materials
Square Metre Rate
Volume Rate
Cost Allocation
Recovery Outcome

That order matches the logic of:

define revenue streams
define micro delivery structure
then reconcile everything at macro level
