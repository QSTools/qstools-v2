This pack now fully separates the business model into five layers:

1. Cost

Owned by:

Labour
Employee Overheads
Assets
General Overheads

Question answered:

What does the business cost?
2. Recovery Strategy

3. Revenue Streams

Owned by:

Materials
Rate Models

Question answered:

What actually creates revenue?
4. Delivery Structure

Owned by:

Cost Allocation

Question answered:

Can the business physically deliver the chosen model?
5. Business Verdict

Owned by:

Recovery Outcome

Question answered:

IMPORTANT COST SUMMARY RULE

Cost Summary remains a pure cost layer.

It:

consumes upstream cost outputs
produces total_cost_burden
produces required_revenue
produces required_recovery_rate baseline

It does NOT:

define recovery strategy
define materials margin
define square metre / tonne / m3 rate logic
consume Cost Allocation outputs
consume Recovery Outcome outputs

This remains locked by the updated Cost Summary authority files

IMPORTANT RECOVERY RULE

Recovery Summary remains the owner of:

active_recovery_model
labour_share_percent
asset_share_percent
overhead_share_percent

It defines recovery intent only.

It does not define:

materials margin
rate model production assumptions
operating-unit configuration
final macro business verdict

This remains aligned to the current recovery architecture

IMPORTANT NEW MATERIALS RULE

Materials exists because many businesses recover a large share of cost through material margin rather than labour charge-out alone.

Materials v1.0 is therefore now a required source file in the aligned pack.

It defines:

annual_material_cost
annual_material_revenue
material_margin_annual
material_margin_percent

It does not become:

inventory control
quote pricing
purchasing workflow
IMPORTANT NEW RATE MODELS RULE

Rate Models exist because many businesses do not recover cost only through hourly labour or machine hire.

They instead recover through:

square metre rates
tonne rates
cubic metre rates
small-job minimums
volume-sensitive pricing

Rate Models therefore become the source-of-truth for:

what is physically sold
what the annual production assumption is
what expected annual recovery contribution is generated
IMPORTANT COST ALLOCATION RULE

Cost Allocation v2.2 is not a full cost engine and not a macro business reconciliation page.

It is the:

structural
operational
micro unit health page

It may now show:

labour basis labels
unit health
delivery groups
warnings about weak or incomplete units

But it must not:

own materials
own rate models
own the macro business decision

This remains consistent with the locked architecture where Cost Allocation is downstream of Recovery Summary and upstream of the final decision layer

IMPORTANT RECOVERY OUTCOME RULE

Recovery Outcome v1.1 is the macro reconciliation page.

It combines:

cost baseline
recovery strategy
material margin contribution
production-based rate contribution
structural readiness

It is the page that tells the user whether the business model actually works.

It must now be understood as:

the macro reality check
the business verdict page
the place where recovery streams are reconciled

Not:

a structural setup page
a materials input page
a detailed quoting page

WHY THIS PACK CHANGED

The previous aligned pack correctly separated:

cost
recovery
structure
decision

But it still lacked formal ownership for the actual revenue-generation streams the business uses in real life.

This updated v1.18 pack fixes that by introducing:

Materials
Rate Models

so QS Tools can now model:

what the business costs
how recovery is intended
what actually generates revenue
how work is physically delivered
whether the model is viable overall
FINAL DEFINITION

This pack is the fully aligned source baseline for the next QS Tools build phase.

It updates the platform from a pure cost-and-recovery architecture into a production-based recovery system by formally adding:

Materials as a material margin layer
Rate Models as the production/output revenue layer
Cost Allocation as the micro operating-unit health layer
Recovery Outcome as the macro business reconciliation and verdict layer
