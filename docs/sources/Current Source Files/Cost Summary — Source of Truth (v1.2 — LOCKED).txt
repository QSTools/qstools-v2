QS Tools — Cost Summary — Source of Truth (v1.2 — LOCKED)

Purpose

The Cost Summary Module is the internal cost and recovery engine of QS Tools.

It is responsible for:

- aggregating all internal business cost components
- calculating total cost burden
- determining required revenue
- calculating required recovery rate

It provides the commercial baseline for the business.

SYSTEM ROLE (CRITICAL)

The Cost Summary Module is a:

decision + output layer for internal cost structure

Position in system:

INPUT MODULES
↓
COST SUMMARY
↓
MATERIALS / P&L (future)
↓
TOTAL BUSINESS SUMMARY (future)
↓
BUDGET

CORE PRINCIPLE

Cost Summary represents:

what the business MUST recover to break even

It does NOT represent:

- actual business performance
- material margin
- subcontract margin
- full P&L view

Core Calculations

labour_cost_per_productive_hour =
labour_cost_rate + overhead_per_productive_hour

total_labour_cost_annual =
sum(labour_cost_per_productive_hour × productive_hours)

total_productive_output =
sum(productive_hours)

total_asset_cost_annual =
sum(total_asset_cost_annual)

total_business_overheads =
total_general_overheads

total_cost_burden =
total_labour_cost_annual
+ total_asset_cost_annual
+ total_business_overheads

required_revenue =
total_cost_burden

required_recovery_rate =
required_revenue / total_productive_output

Rules

- MUST use upstream outputs only
- MUST NOT include materials or subcontract logic
- MUST NOT duplicate upstream calculations
- MUST NOT rename variables

Definition

The Cost Summary Module is a structured aggregation layer that calculates the total internal cost burden of the business and determines the required revenue and recovery rate needed to sustain operations.
