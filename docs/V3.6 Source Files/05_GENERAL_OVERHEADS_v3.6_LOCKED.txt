# QS Tools — General Overheads Module Brief (v3.6 — LOCKED)

STATUS: ACTIVE SOURCE BRIEF
VERSION: v3.6

---

## PURPOSE

General Overheads is the fixed, shared, unclear, staff-related, vehicle-running, and non-asset business overhead classification engine.

It receives P&L-classified overheads and turns them into a clean business overhead total for downstream modelling.

General Overheads answers:

WHAT shared operating costs does the business carry?

It does not answer:

- what Labour costs
- what Assets cost
- what Revenue / COGS produced
- how the business should recover cost
- what quote price should be used
- what cash-flow pressure exists

## CORE ROLE

General Overheads is the post-P&L classification engine for shared business overheads.

It owns:

- business overhead classification
- shared operating costs
- unclear / unallocated business costs
- staff overhead review categories
- vehicle running costs
- fleet running costs
- non-asset interest / other interest
- total_general_overheads

General Overheads does not own asset ownership cost.

General Overheads does not own asset finance interest.

General Overheads does not own Labour employer obligations.

## STAFF OVERHEADS RULE

Staff Overheads is a General Overheads review category only.

It is not:

- a module
- a downstream output
- a Cost Summary input
- a Model Readiness input
- a recovery source

Staff Overheads must roll into total_general_overheads.

Staff Overheads may support review and classification only.

---

## VEHICLE RUNNING COST RULE

Vehicle running costs belong in General Overheads.

This includes:

- fuel
- servicing
- repairs
- maintenance
- registration
- licensing
- tyres
- consumables
- shared fleet costs

These are not Assets module costs.

Assets owns ownership-only asset cost.

General Overheads owns vehicle / fleet running cost.

---

## ASSET OWNERSHIP BOUNDARY

Assets owns:

- asset finance cost
- asset finance interest
- lease cost
- depreciation cost
- ownership cost
- total_asset_cost_annual

General Overheads must not consume or duplicate those values.

If a P&L overhead category includes asset ownership cost, that amount is treated as reconciliation context only.

The specialised Assets module remains the source of truth for asset ownership costs.

---

## INTEREST CLASSIFICATION RULE

P&L interest values may include a mixture of:

- asset finance interest
- overdraft interest
- working capital interest
- bank interest
- accountant journal adjustments
- miscellaneous finance costs

General Overheads owns only the non-asset portion of interest.

Asset finance interest belongs to Assets.

If the user indicates that P&L interest includes asset finance, General Overheads must not treat the full P&L interest value as a clean overhead total.

The system must preserve the distinction between:

- asset_interest_annual from Assets
- other_interest / non_asset_interest in General Overheads

General Overheads must not recalculate asset interest.

General Overheads must not override asset interest from Assets.

---

## P&L INTEREST RELATIONSHIP

P&L interest is historical reported accounting data.

General Overheads may use P&L interest as a classification input for non-asset interest.

General Overheads must not use P&L interest to override asset finance interest.

If P&L interest includes asset finance:

- asset finance interest is handled by Assets
- remaining / non-asset interest may remain in General Overheads where available
- any mismatch between P&L reported interest and Assets modelled interest is informational only

General Overheads must not force exact reconciliation between historical P&L interest and forward-looking asset interest.

---

## FORWARD-LOOKING COST RULE

General Overheads should support the forward-looking Cost Summary model.

P&L values may be used as the current best available overhead estimate.

However, where a specialised module owns a clearer forward-looking cost, the specialised module output must take priority.

General Overheads must not pull forward asset ownership costs from historical P&L values.

---

## ACC RULE

Employer ACC levy belongs in Labour.

General Overheads must not include employer ACC levy.

Business insurance and compliance costs may remain in General Overheads, but employer ACC levy must not be grouped into Insurance & Compliance.

---

## CASH FLOW SEPARATION RULE

General Overheads does not own cash-flow modelling.

Principal repayments must not feed total_general_overheads.

Principal repayments belong in a future Cash Flow layer.

General Overheads may include non-asset interest where it is a true overhead cost.

General Overheads must not include asset principal repayments as overhead.

---

## OUTPUT CONTRACT

General Overheads may output:

- total_general_overheads
- category_totals
- general_overheads_ready
- general_overheads_variance_amount
- general_overheads_variance_percent
- non_asset_interest_annual where available as supporting display detail only

Only total_general_overheads may flow to Cost Summary.

Category subtotals are diagnostic / display values.

non_asset_interest_annual is supporting detail only unless a later locked brief makes it part of a broader reporting contract.

---

## READINESS RULE

general_overheads_ready = true when:

- total_general_overheads exists
- category totals exist
- no employee_overheads category remains
- staff overheads are category-only
- vehicle running costs are included where applicable
- asset ownership costs are excluded
- asset finance interest is not duplicated
- benchmark variance is visible
- no banned downstream overhead variables are exposed

---

## RECONCILIATION

General Overheads may compare to P&L benchmark values.

Variance must be visible.

Variance does not flow downstream as a calculation input.

Module Reconciliation owns the final evidence check.

If an interest mismatch exists between P&L reported interest and Assets modelled asset interest, General Overheads may surface the mismatch as informational context but must not alter the Assets output.

---

## COST SUMMARY HANDOFF

Cost Summary must consume only:

- total_general_overheads

Cost Summary must not consume:

- category subtotals as separate calculation inputs
- staff_overheads_total
- vehicle_running_total
- employee_overheads_total
- raw P&L overhead lines
- P&L benchmark totals as calculation inputs
- asset finance interest from General Overheads
- principal repayments

General Overheads must remain one clean downstream value.

---

## DISPLAY INTENT

General Overheads should be clear but not overbuilt.

Top-level display should show:

- Total General Overheads

Drilldown may show:

- staff overheads
- vehicle running costs
- insurance / compliance
- admin / office costs
- non-asset interest
- other overhead categories

The drilldown is for transparency and review.

Only total_general_overheads flows downstream.

---

## NOT ALLOWED

General Overheads must not:

- calculate recovery rates
- calculate pricing
- calculate productive capacity
- calculate cash flow
- assign costs to labour profiles
- assign costs to asset profiles
- split shared costs during setup
- output staff_overheads_total as a downstream variable
- output vehicle_running_total as a downstream variable
- output employee_overheads_total
- consume Assets ownership cost
- consume asset finance interest
- consume Labour employer obligations
- include employer ACC levy
- include principal repayments
- override Assets with P&L asset finance values
- reconcile historical P&L asset interest into forward-looking asset interest

---

## FINAL DEFINITION

General Overheads v3.6 is the post-P&L classification engine for shared, unclear, staff-related, vehicle-running, and non-asset overhead costs.

It produces one downstream source-of-truth value:

- total_general_overheads

Vehicle running costs and staff overheads remain General Overheads categories only.

Asset ownership costs and asset finance interest belong to Assets.

Principal repayments belong to the future Cash Flow layer.

General Overheads supports Cost Summary by providing a clean non-asset overhead burden.
