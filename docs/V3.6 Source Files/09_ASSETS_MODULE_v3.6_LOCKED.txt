# QS Tools — Assets Module Brief (v3.6 — LOCKED)

STATUS: ACTIVE SOURCE BRIEF
VERSION: v3.6

---

## PURPOSE

Assets is the ownership-only asset cost engine.

It calculates the annual cost of owning or financing business assets.

It does not calculate running costs.

---

## SYSTEM POSITION

P&L
↓
General Overheads
↓
Labour
↓
Assets
↓
Module Reconciliation
↓
Model Readiness
↓
Cost Summary

---

## CORE ROLE

Assets owns:

- asset finance cost
- asset finance interest
- lease cost
- depreciation cost
- ownership cost
- total_asset_cost_annual

Assets is the source of truth for asset-specific ownership cost.

Assets is also the source of truth for asset finance interest.

The P&L may contain historical interest values, but the P&L is not the source of truth for asset finance interest.

---

## INCLUDE

Assets may include:

- finance cost
- asset finance interest
- lease cost
- depreciation cost
- ownership cost
- annualised replacement / purchase recovery if explicitly treated as ownership cost
- other locked ownership-only asset costs

---

## EXCLUDE

Assets must not include:

- fuel
- servicing
- repairs
- maintenance
- registration
- licensing
- tyres
- consumables
- shared fleet costs
- shared maintenance pools
- vehicle running costs
- direct running cost
- staff assignment
- labour recovery
- pricing
- overhead allocation
- hired equipment / delivery cost
- principal repayments as operating cost

Vehicle running costs belong in General Overheads.

Hired equipment / delivery cost belongs in COGS or delivery cost, not Assets.

Principal repayments belong in the future Cash Flow layer, not Cost Summary.

## DEPRECATED FIELD

The following field is deprecated in v3.6:

- running_cost_annual

It must not feed Cost Summary.

If still present in code during migration, it must be treated as legacy only and excluded from total_asset_cost_annual unless explicitly migrated into General Overheads.

## ASSET FINANCE INTEREST RULE — FORWARD-LOOKING

Assets is the source of truth for asset finance interest.

Asset interest must be calculated from the Assets module, not from the P&L.

The P&L may contain historical or accountant-journaled interest values, but those values must not be treated as the source of truth for asset finance.

Assets must calculate asset interest using the current asset finance position.

The purpose is to estimate the asset finance interest the business needs to recover going forward, not to reconstruct the exact historical interest charged in the previous P&L period.

---

## ASSET INTEREST CALCULATION BASIS

Each financed asset should support enough finance inputs to estimate current annual asset interest:

- purchase_date
- finance_term_years
- interest_rate
- financed_amount or loan_amount

For each active financed asset:

1. Calculate elapsed time since purchase_date.
2. Calculate loan progress through the finance term.
3. Estimate remaining balance.
4. Calculate current annual interest from the estimated remaining balance.

Simple accepted model:

remaining_term_percent =
1 - finance_progress_percent

estimated_remaining_finance_balance =
loan_amount × remaining_term_percent

asset_interest_annual =
estimated_remaining_finance_balance × interest_rate

This is an approximation only.

The system does not need to build a full amortisation schedule in v3.6.

---

## FORWARD-LOOKING COST RULE

Cost Summary is forward-looking.

Assets must therefore output the current / forward annual asset interest estimate, not the historical P&L interest amount.

The system must not calculate asset interest by looking backwards over the previous 12 months unless a future locked Cash Flow or finance schedule brief explicitly requires that.

Cost Summary should use the asset cost expected to apply going forward.

---

## P&L INTEREST RELATIONSHIP

If the P&L interest category includes asset finance, the P&L value is treated as a historical reported value only.

The Assets module still remains the authority for asset finance interest.

The system must not force Assets to reconcile exactly to the P&L interest value.

The correct interpretation is:

- asset_interest_annual comes from Assets
- non_asset_interest / other_interest may remain in General Overheads
- any mismatch between P&L reported interest and modelled asset interest is informational only

Do not silently blend historical P&L asset interest with forward-looking asset interest.

Do not rebuild past asset interest merely to reconcile the P&L.

---

## CASH FLOW SEPARATION RULE

Principal repayments are real cash pressure but are not operating cost.

Principal must not feed total_asset_cost_annual.

Principal must not feed Cost Summary.

Assets may calculate or expose principal repayment information later for a Cash Flow layer, but it must remain separate from Cost Summary.

Interest belongs in asset cost.

Principal belongs in Cash Flow.

---

## OPTIONAL CASH FLOW OUTPUTS — FUTURE ONLY

Assets may later expose separate cash-flow support outputs such as:

- asset_principal_repayment_annual
- asset_principal_repayment_monthly
- asset_total_finance_payment_annual
- asset_total_finance_payment_monthly

These outputs must not feed total_asset_cost_annual unless a later locked brief explicitly changes the model.

They are for Cash Flow visibility only.

---

## READINESS RULE

assets_ready = true when one of the following is true:

```text
1. Active assets exist
AND total_asset_cost_annual exists
AND ownership-only rules are followed
AND running_cost_annual does not feed total_asset_cost_annual
AND no vehicle running costs are included
AND no labour costs are included
AND asset finance interest is calculated from Assets when finance applies
