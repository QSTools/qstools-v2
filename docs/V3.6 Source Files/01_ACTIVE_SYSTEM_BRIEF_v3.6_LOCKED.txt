## PURPOSE

This brief defines the active QS Tools system architecture, module order, module ownership, and downstream trust flow.

QS Tools is a first-principles commercial decision system.

The system must show:

- what the business actually costs
- where those costs belong
- whether the model is complete enough to trust
- how the cost burden is recovered
- whether the commercial model is viable
- whether real quotes align with the model

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

Simple in hindsight. Invisible beforehand.

QS Tools reflects the business back to the user.

The system must remove hidden assumptions wherever feasible.

Where an assumption remains, it must be visible, named, and explainable.

Every cost must belong to exactly one module.

If a cost appears in more than one module, the system is incorrect.

## MODULE ROLE DEFINITIONS

### P&L

Raw financial input and first classification layer.

P&L captures business reality.

P&L may provide benchmark totals for reconciliation.

P&L does not perform recovery, pricing, reconciliation verdicts, cost allocation, or downstream modelling.

P&L benchmark values flow to Module Reconciliation only.

P&L values must not feed Cost Summary calculations directly.

---

### General Overheads

Post-P&L classification engine.

General Overheads owns:

- business overhead classification
- shared operating costs
- staff overhead review categories
- vehicle running costs
- fleet running costs
- unclear / unallocated business costs
- total_general_overheads

General Overheads sits immediately after P&L so unclear P&L costs are classified before downstream totals are trusted.

General Overheads does not perform recovery, pricing, cost allocation, or asset ownership calculations.

---

### Labour

People cost engine.

Labour owns:

- gross wages
- paid entitlements
- productive hours
- employer KiwiSaver
- ESCT
- employer ACC levy
- total_labour_cost_annual
- total_productive_output

Labour does not own staff overheads, vehicle running costs, assets, or general overheads.

---

### Assets

Ownership-only asset cost engine.

Assets owns:

- finance cost
- lease cost
- depreciation cost
- ownership cost
- total_asset_cost_annual

Assets does not own:

- fuel
- servicing
- repairs
- maintenance
- registration
- licensing
- tyres
- consumables
- shared running costs
- shared fleet costs
- labour cost

Vehicle and fleet running costs belong in General Overheads.

---

### Module Reconciliation

Evidence layer.

Module Reconciliation checks whether upstream modules are internally consistent, contract-compliant, and safe to trust.

It does not create costs.

It does not alter upstream values.

It produces reconciliation checks, blockers, warnings, and diagnostics.

---

### Model Readiness

Trust gate.

Model Readiness converts reconciliation results into a simple verdict:

- model_ready
- blocking_modules
- warning_modules
- model_readiness_status

If the model is not ready, downstream outputs may display but are not trusted.

---

### Cost Summary

Cost aggregation layer.

Cost Summary answers:

WHAT does the business cost?

Cost Summary consumes upstream outputs only.

It does not reclassify, distribute, price, or rebuild upstream maths.

---

### Recovery Summary

Commercial recovery strategy layer.

Recovery Summary answers:

HOW is the cost recovered?

Recovery Summary consumes Cost Summary outputs only.

---

### Rate Models

Practical earning-rate layer.

Rate Models translates the recovery requirement into usable rates.

It does not calculate cost, capacity, or quote validation.

---

### Production Capacity

Operational deliverability layer.

Production Capacity checks whether the required earning / recovery model can be delivered within available productive capacity.

---

### Recovery Outcome

Commercial decision layer.

Recovery Outcome determines whether the model is viable, marginal, or not viable.

---

### Quote Benchmark

Real-world validation layer.

Quote Benchmark compares model outputs against actual quote behaviour.

---

## REMOVED MODULES

The following modules are removed from the active architecture:

- Employee Overheads Module
- Staff Overheads Module

Staff Overheads may exist only as a General Overheads review category.

It is not:

- a module
- an output
- a downstream input
- a readiness source
- a Cost Summary input

---

## FINAL DEFINITION

QS Tools v3.6 is a staged commercial decision system where P&L captures reality, General Overheads classifies shared costs, Labour and Assets calculate clean source-of-truth totals, Module Reconciliation proves the model, Model Readiness gates trust, and downstream modules convert cost into recovery, rates, capacity, outcomes, and quote validation.
