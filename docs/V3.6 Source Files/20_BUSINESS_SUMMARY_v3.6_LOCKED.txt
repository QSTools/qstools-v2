## PURPOSE

Business Summary is the current business mirror.

It combines the trading result from Revenue / COGS with the operating cost burden from Cost Summary.

Business Summary answers:

What is the business currently doing?

It does not answer:

- what should the user change?
- how should the user price?
- what scenario should the business run?
- what cash-flow pressure exists?
- what quote should be accepted?

Those belong to later layers.

## CORE FORMULAS

net_position =
margin_pool - total_cost_burden

current_margin_per_productive_hour =
margin_pool / total_productive_output

recovery_gap_per_hour =
current_margin_per_productive_hour - required_recovery_rate

If total_productive_output = 0:

current_margin_per_productive_hour = 0
recovery_gap_per_hour = 0
warning required.

## WORDING RULES

Use neutral wording.

Use:

- Revenue
- Direct Costs
- Margin Pool
- Operating Costs
- Net Position

Do not use:

- Loss
- Profit
- Failure
- Fix this
- You should
- Recommended action

Net Position should show the signed number.

Example:

Net Position: -$50,000

Do not relabel it as:

Loss: $50,000

---

## OPERATIONAL REALITY DISPLAY

Business Summary must show the per-hour reality.

Display:

Required Recovery Rate:          $X/hr
Current Margin per Productive Hour: $Y/hr
Gap:                             $Z/hr

This is not a recommendation.

It is a factual comparison.

Required Recovery Rate comes from Cost Summary.

Current Margin per Productive Hour comes from:

margin_pool / total_productive_output

Gap comes from:

current_margin_per_productive_hour - required_recovery_rate

---

## WHY THIS IS NOT JUST A P&L

Business Summary is not a P&L replacement.

A P&L shows historical accounting results.

Business Summary restructures the business into:

Trading result
vs
Operating burden

This reveals whether the current margin pool is enough to carry the operating cost burden.

The key difference is the link to productive output and required recovery rate.

---

## BUSINESS SUMMARY PRINCIPLE

Business Summary must feel like:

Here is your business as it currently stands.

It must not feel like:

Here is what you should do next.

No advice.

No judgement.

No scenario modelling.

No pricing decisions.

---

## TRUST / READINESS RULE

Business Summary is trusted only when:

- Revenue / COGS is ready
- Cost Summary is trusted or usable with warnings
- Model Readiness state is visible
- no blocking upstream checks exist

If model_ready = false:

- Business Summary may display preview values
- values must be marked not trusted
- downstream Business Modelling must not treat the baseline as final

If model_readiness_status = warning:

- Business Summary may display values
- warnings must remain visible

If model_readiness_status = ready:

- Business Summary outputs are trusted

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO COST SUMMARY

Business Summary consumes Cost Summary.

Business Summary must not:

- recalculate Cost Summary
- rebuild Labour maths
- rebuild Asset maths
- rebuild General Overheads maths
- change total_cost_burden
- change required_recovery_rate

Cost Summary owns the operating cost burden.

Business Summary only compares it against Margin Pool.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO REVENUE / COGS

Business Summary consumes Revenue / COGS.

Business Summary must not:

- reclassify P&L lines
- recalculate direct cost ownership
- use micro / activity classifications as calculation drivers
- change margin_pool

Revenue / COGS owns the trading result.

Business Summary only compares it against Cost Summary.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS MODELLING

Business Modelling consumes Business Summary as the current business baseline.

Business Summary does not create scenarios.

Business Summary does not save editable model states.

Business Summary does not overwrite source modules.

Business Modelling later creates:

Baseline = locked current business

Scenario = editable copy of baseline

---

## CASH FLOW BOUNDARY

Business Summary does not include principal repayments in Net Position.

Principal repayments are cash-flow pressure.

They belong to the future Cash Flow layer.

Business Summary may later show a separate Cash Flow indicator if a Cash Flow brief is locked, but principal must not alter:

- net_position
- total_cost_burden
- required_recovery_rate
- recovery_gap_per_hour

## USER EXPERIENCE PRINCIPLE

The user should not need to interpret a dashboard.

The page should directly show:

- what came in
- what went out as direct cost
- what margin pool remained
- what the business costs to operate
- what the net position is
- what the per-hour gap is

This page is the bridge between accounting reality and operational reality.

---

## NOT ALLOWED

Business Summary must not:

- suggest actions
- create scenarios
- alter source data
- mutate P&L
- mutate Cost Summary
- mutate Revenue / COGS
- calculate quote pricing
- calculate cash flow
- include principal repayments in operating result
- treat gross margin as proof of profit
- treat Net Position as a recommendation
- hide upstream readiness warnings
- display untrusted values as final

---

## FINAL DEFINITION

Business Summary v3.6 is the factual mirror layer.

It combines:

Revenue / COGS Margin Pool

with:

Cost Summary Operating Cost Burden

to show:

Net Position

and:

Required Recovery Rate vs Current Margin per Productive Hour

It shows the current state of the business without advice, scenarios, pricing, or cash-flow modelling.
