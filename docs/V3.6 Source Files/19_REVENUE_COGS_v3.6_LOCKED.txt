## PURPOSE

Revenue / COGS is the trading-side summary layer.

It calculates the margin pool created by the business before operating cost burden is applied.

Revenue / COGS answers:

What did the business sell?

What did the business directly spend to create those sales?

What margin pool was left?

## CORE ROLE

Revenue / COGS owns the trading-side calculation:

Revenue
- Direct Costs
= Margin Pool

It does not own operating cost.

It does not own Cost Summary.

It does not own pricing.

It does not own recovery strategy.

It does not own modelling scenarios.

---

## REQUIRED INPUTS

### From P&L

Revenue / COGS consumes classified P&L outputs only:

- income_benchmark_total
- cogs_benchmark_total
- direct_cost_benchmark_total where available
- classified income / revenue lines
- classified COGS / direct cost lines

P&L remains the source of classified trading data.

---

## OPTIONAL DISPLAY INPUTS

Revenue / COGS may display:

- revenue_category_totals
- direct_cost_category_totals
- COGS / Direct Cost category breakdown
- review-required COGS items
- excluded trading lines where useful

These are for transparency only.

## OUTPUT CONTRACT

Revenue / COGS may output:

- total_revenue
- total_direct_costs
- margin_pool
- gross_margin_percent
- revenue_cogs_ready
- revenue_cogs_warnings

Optional display outputs:

- revenue_category_totals
- direct_cost_category_totals

---

## CORE FORMULAS

margin_pool =
total_revenue - total_direct_costs

gross_margin_percent =
margin_pool / total_revenue

If total_revenue = 0:

gross_margin_percent = 0
warning required.

---

## MARGIN POOL DEFINITION

Margin Pool is the amount left after direct costs are removed from revenue.

It is not final profit.

It is not net position.

It is not target profit.

It is not quote margin.

Margin Pool is the pool available to recover:

- Labour cost
- General Overheads
- Asset ownership cost
- Target profit later

Business Summary later compares Margin Pool against Cost Summary’s operating cost burden.

---

## DIRECT COST / COGS DEFINITION

Direct Costs / COGS may include:

- materials
- subcontractors
- hired equipment / plant for delivery
- freight / cartage directly related to jobs
- waste / tipping directly related to jobs
- direct consumables
- other direct job costs

Direct Costs / COGS must not include:

- internal Labour module cost
- General Overheads
- asset ownership cost
- asset finance interest
- vehicle running overheads unless directly classified as job COGS
- principal repayments
- cash-flow pressure
- target profit
- pricing margin

---

## SUBCONTRACT LABOUR RULE

Subcontract labour may appear in COGS / Direct Costs.

Subcontract labour is external labour cost.

It must not be confused with internal productive labour capacity.

Subcontract labour must not be treated as internal Labour module recovery unless a later locked brief explicitly allows that treatment.

Future quote and recovery analysis should distinguish:

- internal labour recovery
- subcontract labour cost
- total labour content
- internal vs external labour mix

---

## MACRO CLASSIFICATION RULE

Revenue / COGS uses macro P&L classification only in v3.6.

Active classifications:

- income
- cogs

Optional micro / activity categories may be stored for future insight, but they must not drive Revenue / COGS calculations in v3.6.

Rule:

Macro classification = active calculation input.

Micro classification = optional stored detail.

---

## MICRO / ACTIVITY CLASSIFICATION RULE

The P&L may capture optional activity categories such as:

- concrete
- excavation
- subcontract type
- supplier group
- delivery category
- user-defined business activity

Revenue / COGS must not rely on those categories in v3.6.

They may be displayed as supporting detail only.

They must not be required for readiness.

They must not block Revenue / COGS.

They must not change the margin_pool calculation unless a later locked brief explicitly promotes them.

---

## READINESS RULE

revenue_cogs_ready = true when:

- total_revenue exists
- total_direct_costs exists
- required income lines are classified
- required COGS / direct cost lines are classified
- no required trading lines remain unassigned
- no blocking review-required trading items remain unresolved

Optional micro / activity classification is not required for readiness.

If total_revenue = 0, Revenue / COGS may still display, but must show a warning.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO COST SUMMARY

Revenue / COGS does not consume Cost Summary.

Revenue / COGS does not calculate Cost Summary.

Revenue / COGS produces the trading-side output:

- margin_pool

Cost Summary produces the operating-cost output:

- total_cost_burden
- required_recovery_rate
- total_productive_output

Business Summary later combines them.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS SUMMARY

Business Summary consumes Revenue / COGS outputs:

- total_revenue
- total_direct_costs
- margin_pool
- gross_margin_percent where useful

Business Summary then compares:

Margin Pool
-
Total Cost Burden
=
Net Position

Revenue / COGS must not calculate Net Position.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS MODELLING

Business Modelling may later use Revenue / COGS outputs as part of the baseline snapshot.

Revenue / COGS must not create scenarios.

Revenue / COGS must not mutate scenario values.

Revenue / COGS remains the trading-side source layer.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO QUOTE ENGINE

Quote Engine later may compare quoted revenue and direct costs against the Revenue / COGS model.

Revenue / COGS does not price quotes.

Revenue / COGS does not decide whether work should be accepted.

Revenue / COGS provides the concept of Margin Pool that Quote Engine later applies quote-by-quote.

---

## DISPLAY STRUCTURE

Revenue / COGS should be simple and macro-first.

Top-level display should show:

- Revenue
- Direct Costs
- Margin Pool
- Gross Margin %

Supporting drilldown may show:

- income categories
- direct cost categories
- review-required trading lines
- optional activity detail

The main page must not become a detailed transaction review page.

---

## USER EXPERIENCE PRINCIPLE

Revenue / COGS should help the user see the trading engine clearly.

The user should immediately understand:

- what came in
- what was spent directly to produce it
- what was left to recover the business

The page should not suggest fixes.

The page should not model pricing changes.

The page should not calculate recovery strategy.

---

## NOT ALLOWED

Revenue / COGS must not:

- consume Labour internals
- consume Assets internals
- consume General Overheads internals
- consume Cost Summary internals to calculate margin_pool
- calculate required_recovery_rate
- calculate total_cost_burden
- calculate Net Position
- calculate pricing
- calculate recovery strategy
- calculate scenarios
- calculate cash flow
- use micro / activity classification as a required calculation input in v3.6
- treat gross margin as proof of profit
- confuse subcontract labour with internal productive labour
- include principal repayments

---

## FINAL DEFINITION

Revenue / COGS v3.6 is the trading-side summary layer.

It consumes classified P&L income and direct cost outputs.

It calculates:

Revenue
- Direct Costs
= Margin Pool

It does not calculate operating cost, pricing, recovery strategy, scenario modelling, or cash flow.

Revenue / COGS produces the Margin Pool that Business Summary later compares against Cost Summary’s operating cost burden.
