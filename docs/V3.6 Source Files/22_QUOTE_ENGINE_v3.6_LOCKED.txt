## PURPOSE

Quote Engine is the future quote-level commercial test layer.

It applies the trusted business model to individual quotes.

Quote Engine answers:

Does this quote recover the business model?

It does not answer:

- what does the business currently cost?
- what is the current business position?
- what scenario should the user model?
- can the business physically deliver the work?
- what cash-flow pressure exists?

Those belong to other layers.

## CORE ROLE

Quote Engine applies the selected trusted business model to real quote data.

It tests whether a quote creates enough margin / recovery pool to recover:

- internal labour requirement
- general overhead requirement
- asset ownership requirement
- target profit later

It must not rely on gross margin alone.

A quote can look profitable on gross margin and still fail the business recovery model.

---

## CORE FORMULA CONCEPT

At quote level:

Quote Revenue
- Quote Direct Costs
= Quote Margin / Recovery Pool

Then compare that pool against the recovery requirement of the model.

Future quote recovery test:

Quote Margin / Recovery Pool
- Required Internal Labour Recovery
- Required General Overhead Recovery
- Required Asset Ownership Recovery
- Target Profit Requirement later
= Quote Recovery Surplus / Shortfall

If the result is negative, the quote is under-recovering against the model.

## QUOTE ALIGNMENT STATUS

Allowed future quote_alignment_status values may include:

- recovering
- under_recovering
- commercially_loss_making_against_model
- warning
- blocked

Definitions:

recovering =
quote margin / recovery pool is sufficient to recover the selected model requirement.

under_recovering =
quote margin / recovery pool is positive but insufficient to recover the selected model requirement.

commercially_loss_making_against_model =
quote fails the model’s recovery requirement even if the quote appears profitable on gross margin.

warning =
quote can be assessed, but assumptions or source model warnings exist.

blocked =
quote cannot be trusted because the upstream model or quote inputs are incomplete.

## GROSS MARGIN RULE

Gross margin is not proof of profit.

Quote Engine must not treat a positive gross margin as proof that the quote is commercially healthy.

The quote must be tested against the selected business model.

Core principle:

A quote is only commercially healthy if the margin / recovery pool is enough to recover the business model requirement.

---

## INTERNAL LABOUR RECOVERY RULE

Quote Engine must distinguish internal labour recovery from subcontract labour.

Internal labour belongs to the business productive capacity.

Subcontract labour is an external direct cost unless explicitly treated otherwise by a later locked brief.

Quote Engine must not automatically treat subcontractor cost as internal labour recovery.

Future quote tests should show:

- internal labour recovery
- subcontract labour cost
- internal vs external labour mix
- whether internal labour is being under-recovered

---

## SUBCONTRACT LABOUR RULE

Subcontract labour may appear in quote direct costs.

Subcontract labour should normally be treated as:

external/direct labour cost

It must not be treated as internal productive labour unless the user explicitly classifies it that way under a later locked rule.

This prevents the quote model from overstating internal labour recovery when work is delivered by external subcontractors.

---

## DIRECT COST RULE

Quote direct costs may include:

- materials
- subcontractors
- hired equipment / plant
- freight / cartage
- waste / tipping
- direct consumables
- other direct job costs

Direct costs must not include:

- internal Labour module cost unless explicitly represented as quoted internal labour
- general overheads
- asset ownership cost
- asset finance interest
- principal repayments
- cash-flow pressure
- target profit

---

## SELECTED MODEL RULE

Quote Engine must test against one selected model state.

Allowed model sources later:

- locked baseline
- upside scenario
- downside scenario
- selected active scenario

Quote Engine must always show which model state was used.

Quote Engine must not silently switch models.

Quote Engine must not mutate Business Modelling scenarios.

---

## TRUST RULE

Quote Engine must inherit upstream trust state.

If selected model is not trusted:

- Quote Engine may display preview only
- quote outputs must be marked not trusted
- quote alignment status should not be final

If selected model has warnings:

- quote may be assessed
- warnings must remain visible

If selected model is trusted:

- quote assessment may be treated as valid

## RELATIONSHIP TO COST SUMMARY

Cost Summary defines the operating cost burden and required recovery rate.

Quote Engine consumes those values directly or through Business Modelling.

Quote Engine must not recalculate Cost Summary.

Quote Engine must not rebuild Labour, Assets, or General Overheads maths.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO REVENUE / COGS

Revenue / COGS defines the margin pool concept at business level.

Quote Engine applies the same concept at quote level.

Quote Engine must not mutate Revenue / COGS.

Quote Engine must not treat historical business gross margin as the quote result.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS SUMMARY

Business Summary is the factual current business mirror.

Quote Engine does not change Business Summary.

Business Summary shows what the business currently is.

Quote Engine tests whether a quote aligns with the model.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS MODELLING

Business Modelling provides the selected baseline or scenario.

Quote Engine may use the selected scenario as the quote benchmark.

Quote Engine must not edit or overwrite Business Modelling scenarios.

Quote Engine must not create modelling assumptions silently.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO PRODUCTION CAPACITY

Production Capacity belongs after Quote Engine.

Quote Engine may later output required internal labour hours or production demand for Production Capacity.

Production Capacity answers:

Can the business actually deliver this work?

Quote Engine answers:

Does this quote recover the model?

These are separate tests.

A quote may recover the model but still fail capacity.

A quote may fit capacity but fail recovery.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO CASH FLOW

Cash Flow belongs later.

Principal repayments and liquidity pressure are not Quote Engine operating cost inputs.

Cash Flow may later test whether accepted quotes support finance payment pressure.

Quote Engine must not include principal repayments in quote recovery requirement unless a later locked Cash Flow integration brief explicitly defines that treatment.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO MONTHLY ACTUALS

Monthly Actuals / Feedback belongs later.

Quote Engine may eventually support accepted / rejected quote feedback.

Future feedback loop:

quote prepared
→ quote issued
→ quote accepted / rejected
→ accepted work appears in monthly P&L
→ actual performance compared against model

This is not part of the v3.6 Quote Engine build.

---

## FINAL DEFINITION

Quote Engine v3.6 is a future quote-level commercial test layer.

It applies the trusted baseline or selected scenario to individual quotes.

It tests whether the quote margin / recovery pool is enough to recover the selected business model.

It does not build the model.

It does not calculate Cost Summary.

It does not model Cash Flow.

It does not test Production Capacity.

It exists to prevent quotes from appearing commercially healthy merely because gross margin is positive.
