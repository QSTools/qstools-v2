# QS Tools — Labour Recovery & Margin Pool Standard (v3.6 — LOCKED)

STATUS: ACTIVE FUTURE STANDARD  
VERSION: v3.6

---

## PURPOSE

This brief defines the labour recovery and margin pool standard for QS Tools.

It protects one of the core commercial insights of the product:

```text
A fixed-price quote can show gross margin and still fail to recover internal labour, overheads, assets, and target profit.
```

QS Tools must expose this clearly.

---

## CORE RULE

For fixed-price and quote-based businesses, QS Tools must not treat gross margin as proof of profit.

The quote must generate enough margin / recovery pool to recover:

- internal labour cost
- general overheads
- asset ownership costs
- target profit

If it does not, the quote is under-recovering or commercially loss-making against the model.

---

## FIXED-PRICE RECOVERY TEST

The core fixed-price test is:

```text
Revenue
- external/direct costs
= margin / recovery pool
```

Then:

```text
margin / recovery pool
- required internal labour recovery
- required general overhead recovery
- required asset ownership recovery
- target profit
= recovery surplus / shortfall
```

If the recovery surplus / shortfall is negative, the quote is commercially loss-making against the model.

This may be true even if the quote appears profitable using a simple gross-margin view.

---

## EXTERNAL / DIRECT COST RULE

External/direct costs may include:

- materials
- freight
- plant hire
- consumables
- third-party job costs
- subcontract labour where it is bought-in external labour

External/direct costs are removed before calculating the margin / recovery pool.

---

## SUBCONTRACT LABOUR RULE

Subcontract labour must be visible separately from internal labour recovery.

Subcontract labour may appear in COGS, but it is not internal productive labour capacity.

For labour recovery analysis, subcontract labour should normally be treated as external labour cost unless explicitly reclassified.

Future analysis should distinguish:

- internal labour recovery
- subcontract labour cost
- total labour content
- internal vs external labour mix

This prevents QS Tools from overstating internal labour recovery when the work is actually being delivered by subcontractors.

---

## INTERNAL LABOUR RECOVERY RULE

Internal labour recovery must be based on the Labour module.

Required internal labour recovery rate:

```text
required_labour_recovery_rate =
total_labour_cost_annual
/ total_productive_output
```

Revenue available for internal labour recovery:

```text
revenue_available_for_internal_labour_recovery =
margin / recovery pool
- required general overhead recovery
- required asset ownership recovery
- target profit
```

Effective internal labour recovery rate:

```text
effective_internal_labour_recovery_rate =
revenue_available_for_internal_labour_recovery
/ total_productive_output
```

Labour recovery variance:

```text
effective_internal_labour_recovery_rate
- required_labour_recovery_rate
```

---

## SUMMARY-LEVEL VISIBILITY RULE

QS Tools must expose baseline labour recovery before quote-level analysis exists.

The summary layers should show:

- total labour cost burden
- total productive labour hours
- required labour recovery rate
- revenue available for internal labour recovery
- effective internal labour recovery rate
- labour recovery variance
- labour recovery status

The summary layer should answer:

```text
After external/direct costs, overheads, assets, and target profit are allowed for, what is actually left to recover internal labour?
```

---

## QUOTE BENCHMARK RULE

Future Quote Benchmark must eventually show, quote-by-quote:

- quoted revenue
- external/direct costs
- margin / recovery pool
- required internal labour recovery
- required general overhead recovery
- required asset ownership recovery
- target profit
- recovery surplus / shortfall
- effective labour recovery rate
- required labour recovery rate
- accepted / rejected status

The product should clearly state when:

```text
This quote is under-recovering by $X.
```

or:

```text
This quote is commercially loss-making against your model.
```

---

## COMMERCIAL RECOVERY STATUS

Future status language may include:

- recovering
- under-recovering
- commercially loss-making against model

Definitions:

```text
recovering =
margin / recovery pool covers labour, overheads, assets, and target profit
```

```text
under-recovering =
margin / recovery pool covers some but not all required recovery
```

```text
commercially loss-making against model =
margin / recovery pool is not large enough to recover the model requirements
```

---

## FINAL STANDARD

QS Tools must show whether a fixed-price quote actually recovers the business model.

The system must not allow a quote to look commercially healthy merely because gross margin is positive.

The key question is:

```text
Does the margin / recovery pool recover internal labour, general overheads, asset ownership, and target profit?
```

If not, QS Tools must make the shortfall visible.
