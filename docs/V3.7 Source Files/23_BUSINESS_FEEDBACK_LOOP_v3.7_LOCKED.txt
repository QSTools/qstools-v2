## PURPOSE

The Business Feedback Loop is the future learning layer of QS Tools.

It connects accepted quote predictions to monthly business reality.

It answers:

**Are we consistently pricing work correctly as a business?**

It does not answer:

* did this exact job perform perfectly?
* which exact trade caused the loss?
* what did every individual job cost?
* what invoice belongs to which job line?
* what should the user change automatically?

Those belong to later job-costing, invoicing, production, or advisory layers.

---

## PRODUCT ROLE

The Business Feedback Loop is not machine learning.

It is not a black box.

It is not the system inventing rules.

It is a structured feedback loop based on first principles.

The user defines the business assumptions.

QS Tools compares those assumptions against reality over time.

Core idea:

**Quotes are predictions. Monthly P&L is reality. The feedback loop compares the two.**

---

## CORE PRODUCT QUESTION

The feedback loop supports the main QS Tools product question:

**Do your assumptions about how your business runs match reality?**

Supporting positioning:

**Simple in hindsight. Invisible beforehand.**

**We mirror your business back to you.**

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

The feedback loop is macro-level first, not job-level first.

V1 must compare:

Accepted quotes in a month
vs
Monthly P&L actuals

It must not try to perfectly job-cost each individual job in V1.

The purpose is to detect whether the business is consistently pricing work correctly, not to prove every job line item.

---

## WHY MACRO FIRST

Job-level costing is valuable later, but it is messy early.

Jobs may:

* span multiple months
* be invoiced progressively
* include blended costs
* have incomplete trade-level breakdowns
* be priced differently by different businesses
* include materials, subcontractors, labour, and plant in different ways

Trying to solve perfect job costing first creates too much friction.

The macro loop gives useful signal sooner.

It shows whether pricing assumptions are generally right or wrong across the business.

---

## SIMPLE FLOW

1. User builds business baseline
2. User creates quote
3. Quote is revised if needed
4. Quote is accepted
5. Accepted quote snapshot is locked
6. Accepted quote is assigned to an accepted month
7. Monthly P&L is imported later
8. System compares accepted quote predictions against monthly actuals
9. System shows variance
10. User updates assumptions if needed
11. Future quotes improve

---

## PREDICTION VS REALITY RULE

The feedback loop must keep prediction and reality separate.

Prediction = accepted quote snapshot

Reality = monthly P&L actuals

The system must never overwrite accepted quote predictions with actual results.

The system must never rewrite history to make past predictions match reality.

The value comes from comparing what was believed at the time with what actually happened later.

---

## ACCEPTED QUOTE SNAPSHOT RULE

Only accepted quote versions enter the feedback loop.

Drafts and revised quote versions are working states only.

When a quote is marked accepted, the system must lock a snapshot.

That snapshot represents:

**What we believed when we priced the job.**

Accepted snapshots must be immutable.

If the quote changes after acceptance, later versions or variations may be created, but the original accepted snapshot remains intact.

---

## QUOTE VERSIONING RULE

A job may have multiple quote versions.

Example:

Job: Smith House Slab
Version 1: initial estimate
Version 2: revised after client feedback
Version 3: submitted quote
Version 4: accepted quote

Only the accepted version becomes the feedback-loop prediction.

## CORE COMPARISON QUESTIONS

The feedback loop should answer:

* Did accepted quotes predict enough margin pool?
* Did actual P&L produce the expected margin pool?
* Did the business recover the required operating cost burden?
* Is the business consistently under-recovering?
* Are accepted quotes aligned with actual monthly performance?
* Are assumptions improving or drifting?

---

## USER-FACING INSIGHTS

Use plain wording.

Examples:

**Accepted quotes predicted $X margin pool this month. Actual P&L produced $Y.**

**The business recovered $X/hr against a required $Y/hr.**

**Your accepted quotes are tracking above baseline.**

**Your accepted quotes are not currently producing enough margin pool to carry the business.**

**This is a monthly business signal, not job-level costing.**

---

## WHAT THE FEEDBACK LOOP SHOULD NOT DO IN V1

The feedback loop must not:

* attempt perfect job-level costing
* allocate every P&L cost line to individual jobs
* require invoice-by-invoice job allocation
* rewrite accepted quote predictions
* automatically change Labour assumptions
* automatically change Cost Summary
* automatically change Business Modelling scenarios
* tell the user what to do as a black-box recommendation
* treat one month as proof of a permanent pattern
* treat monthly P&L as exact job-level truth

## RELATIONSHIP TO REVENUE / COGS

Revenue / COGS provides the trading actuals.

For monthly actuals, Revenue / COGS may be run by accounting period.

It produces:

* actual_total_revenue
* actual_total_direct_costs
* actual_margin_pool
* actual_gross_margin_percent

The feedback loop consumes these outputs.

It must not rebuild Revenue / COGS logic.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS SUMMARY

Business Summary provides the current business mirror.

The feedback loop may compare accepted quote predictions and monthly actuals against the business baseline.

Business Summary does not perform feedback analysis.

The feedback loop consumes Business Summary outputs where needed.

It must not mutate Business Summary.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS MODELLING

Business Modelling provides baseline, upside, and downside benchmarks.

The feedback loop may later compare actual performance against the selected benchmark.

Business Modelling scenarios must not be overwritten automatically by feedback results.

Any baseline refresh or scenario update must be explicit.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO QUOTE ENGINE

Quote Engine creates quote predictions.

When a quote is accepted, Quote Engine must preserve a locked accepted quote snapshot.

The feedback loop consumes accepted quote snapshots.

The feedback loop must not recalculate quote logic.

The feedback loop must not edit accepted quote snapshots.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO XERO / API IMPORTS

Xero API import belongs later.

When implemented, it should provide monthly P&L actuals by accounting period.

The import process should preserve source references, import date, and period identity.

Xero actuals must be stored separately from accepted quote predictions.

---

## STORAGE RULE

The feedback loop should be database-ready.

Separate storage concepts:

1. accepted_quote_snapshots
2. monthly_actuals
3. feedback_periods
4. feedback_results

Do not store feedback results inside editable quote records.

Do not store monthly actuals inside accepted quote snapshots.

---

## READINESS RULE

feedback_ready = true when:

* at least one accepted quote snapshot exists for the period
* monthly actuals exist for the same period
* required baseline/model reference exists
* no blocking data integrity issue exists

If accepted quotes exist but monthly actuals are missing:

* show pending actuals state

If monthly actuals exist but no accepted quotes exist:

* show no accepted quote predictions for this period

---

## WARNING REQUIREMENTS

Warnings must be explicit.

Minimum warnings:

* no_accepted_quotes_for_period
* no_monthly_actuals_for_period
* accepted_quotes_without_locked_snapshot
* monthly_actuals_not_imported
* baseline_reference_missing
* model_trust_warning
* period_mismatch
* actuals_import_warning

Warning wording examples:

* No accepted quote snapshots exist for this period.
* Monthly actuals have not been imported for this period.
* Some accepted quotes are missing locked snapshots.
* Actuals period does not match the selected feedback period.
