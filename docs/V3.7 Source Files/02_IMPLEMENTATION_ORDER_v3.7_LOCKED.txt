## PURPOSE

This brief defines the active build sequence for QS Tools v3.7.

It protects the system from building downstream pages before upstream contracts, source ownership, readiness gates, and baseline commercial outputs are stable.

The build order must follow the first-principles rule:

**Brief first. Narrow build second. Verify. Commit. Move on.**

Do not run broad Codex tasks against unclear behaviour.

## PHASE 2 — CORE INPUT + CLASSIFICATION MODULES

1. P&L
2. Revenue / COGS
3. General Overheads
4. Labour
5. Assets

These modules define the source inputs and trading baseline.

They must not be bypassed by downstream modules.

P&L provides classification and future monthly actuals preparation.

Revenue / COGS produces Margin Pool.

General Overheads, Labour, and Assets provide operating cost source totals.

---

## PHASE 3 — MODEL TRUST

1. Module Reconciliation
2. Model Readiness
3. Cost Summary

Module Reconciliation is the evidence layer.

Model Readiness is the trust gate.

Cost Summary is the first trusted downstream operating cost summary.

Do not treat Cost Summary as final unless Model Readiness exists and upstream module outputs are stable.

---

## PHASE 4 — ASSETS / COST SUMMARY ALIGNMENT

Before finalising Cost Summary, align Assets to the locked v3.7 rules:

1. Confirm Assets remains ownership-only.
2. Confirm running costs are excluded from Assets.
3. Confirm vehicle / fleet running costs belong to General Overheads.
4. Confirm asset finance interest is calculated by Assets.
5. Confirm asset finance interest is forward-looking.
6. Confirm asset finance interest does not rely on historical P&L interest.
7. Confirm paid-off / term-ended finance produces no current finance cost.
8. Confirm principal repayments do not feed Assets total_asset_cost_annual.
9. Confirm principal repayments do not feed Cost Summary.
10. Confirm principal belongs to Cash Flow later.
11. Confirm Assets exposes clean outputs for Cost Summary.

Required Assets outputs:

* total_asset_cost_annual
* total_asset_interest_annual where available for supporting display
* assets_ready
* no_active_assets_confirmed

## PHASE 5 — COST SUMMARY FINALISATION

Build / finalise Cost Summary after Assets, Labour, General Overheads, Module Reconciliation, and Model Readiness are aligned.

Cost Summary must consume:

* total_labour_cost_annual from Labour
* total_productive_output from Labour
* total_asset_cost_annual from Assets
* total_general_overheads from General Overheads
* model_ready from Model Readiness
* model_readiness_status from Model Readiness

Cost Summary must calculate:

* total_people_cost_annual
* total_business_overheads
* total_business_cost_annual
* total_cost_burden
* required_revenue
* required_recovery_rate

Cost Summary must clearly explain:

**Required Recovery Rate is not a labour charge-out rate.**

**It is the business break-even pressure per productive hour.**

**All staff cost must be recovered. Only productive staff hours can recover it.**

Cost Summary must not:

* rebuild Labour maths
* rebuild Asset maths
* rebuild asset interest
* rebuild General Overheads maths
* consume raw P&L lines
* use P&L benchmark values as calculation inputs
* use P&L interest as asset finance authority
* include principal repayments
* consume Revenue / COGS
* consume Business Summary
* consume Business Modelling
* consume Quote Engine
* consume monthly actuals
* define pricing
* define cash flow
* perform feedback-loop comparison

## PHASE 6 — REVENUE / COGS

Build / patch Revenue / COGS after P&L classification is stable and Cost Summary trust flow is clear.

Revenue / COGS consumes classified P&L income and direct cost / COGS outputs.

It calculates:

```text
Revenue
- Direct Costs
= Margin Pool
```

Revenue / COGS must produce:

* total_revenue
* total_direct_costs
* margin_pool
* gross_margin_percent where useful
* revenue_cogs_ready
* revenue_cogs_warnings

Revenue / COGS must not:

* consume Cost Summary to calculate Margin Pool
* consume Labour internals
* consume Assets internals
* consume General Overheads internals
* use micro / activity classification in v3.7 core calculations unless later promoted by a locked brief
* calculate Net Position
* define pricing
* define recovery strategy
* define scenarios
* test quotes

---

## PHASE 7 — BUSINESS SUMMARY

Build / patch Business Summary after Revenue / COGS exists and Cost Summary is trusted or warning-usable.

Business Summary combines:

```text
Margin Pool from Revenue / COGS
```

with:

```text
Total Cost Burden from Cost Summary
```

It shows:

* Revenue
* Direct Costs
* Margin Pool
* Operating Cost Burden
* Net Position
* Required Recovery Rate
* Current Margin per Productive Hour
* Recovery Gap per Hour
* trust state
* explicit warnings

Business Summary is the mirror layer.

It shows the current state of the business.

Business Summary must not:

* suggest fixes
* model scenarios
* mutate source modules
* calculate Cost Summary
* calculate Revenue / COGS
* define quote pricing
* create repair levers
* store accepted quote snapshots
* define cash flow

---

## PHASE 8 — BUSINESS MODELLING

Build / patch Business Modelling after Business Summary.

Business Modelling starts from a locked baseline snapshot.

Baseline = current business reality.

Scenario = editable copy of baseline.

The baseline must not be overwritten automatically.

Business Modelling may include:

* Baseline model
* Upside / improvement scenario
* Downside / pressure scenario
* revenue changes
* margin changes
* productivity changes
* operating cost changes
* live delta against baseline
* selected model for Quote Engine later

Business Modelling must not:

* mutate P&L
* mutate Labour
* mutate Assets
* mutate General Overheads
* mutate Cost Summary
* mutate Revenue / COGS
* mutate Business Summary
* treat required_recovery_rate as a scenario
* include principal repayments in operating cost
* price quotes
* store accepted quote snapshots
* perform feedback-loop comparison

## PHASE 9 — QUOTE ENGINE BASELINE BUILD

Quote Engine is now governed by:

```text
22_QUOTE_ENGINE_v3.7_LOCKED.txt
```

Quote Engine may be built after:

* Cost Summary is trusted or warning-usable
* Revenue / COGS exists
* Business Summary exists
* Business Modelling boundary exists
* selected baseline / scenario trust state exists or baseline-only mode is explicitly used
* Quote Engine brief 22 is used as the controlling source brief

First build should be baseline-only.

Do not block first build on full Business Modelling scenario comparison.

Quote Engine applies the trusted baseline or selected scenario to real quote pricing.

First build should include:

* quick quote entry
* quote price
* material cost / sell
* labour sell / allowed hours
* direct cost package pool
* true labour cost from Labour where available
* required_recovery_rate from Cost Summary / selected model
* quote_margin_pool
* business_recovery_required_total
* quote_gap
* hourly_gap
* max_viable_hours
* required_price_at_allowed_hours
* repair levers
* original quote vs adjusted test quote
* visible calculation build-up
* explicit warnings

Baseline quote test:

```text
quote_price_total
- direct_cost_total
= quote_margin_pool
```

```text
base_labour_hours_allowed
× required_recovery_rate
= business_recovery_required_total
```

```text
quote_margin_pool
- business_recovery_required_total
= quote_gap
```

Quote Engine must not:

* rely on gross margin alone
* confuse subcontract labour with internal productive labour
* include principal repayments as operating cost
* mutate Business Modelling
* mutate Business Summary
* mutate Cost Summary
* mutate Revenue / COGS
* rebuild Labour maths
* build Production Capacity
* build Cash Flow
* perform monthly feedback analysis

## PHASE 10 — QUOTE VERSIONING / ACCEPTED SNAPSHOT STRUCTURE

After baseline Quote Engine works, add quote status and snapshot readiness.

Required concepts:

* draft quote
* issued quote
* revised quote
* accepted quote
* rejected quote
* quote version
* accepted quote snapshot

Only accepted quote versions enter the future Feedback Loop.

Accepted snapshots must be immutable.

Accepted snapshot represents:

**What we believed when we priced the job.**

Accepted quote snapshots must preserve:

* quote values
* model used
* model trust state
* accepted date
* accepted period
* quote gap
* hourly gap
* result status

Do not build monthly actual comparison yet.

## PHASE 11 — FEEDBACK LOOP LATER

Feedback Loop is governed by:

```text
23_BUSINESS_FEEDBACK_LOOP_v3.7.txt
```

Build only after:

* Quote Engine stores accepted quote snapshots
* accepted period logic exists
* monthly actuals storage exists
* Revenue / COGS can run actuals by period or accept period-based inputs
* P&L monthly actuals boundary is clear

Feedback Loop compares:

```text
accepted quote predictions in a month
```

against:

```text
monthly P&L actuals
```

Feedback Loop is macro-level first, not job-level first.

Feedback Loop must not:

* attempt perfect job-level costing in V1
* overwrite quote predictions
* overwrite accepted quote snapshots
* overwrite monthly actuals
* mutate source modules automatically
* act like machine learning
* invent rules

---

## PHASE 12 — CASH FLOW LATER

Build Cash Flow after the core commercial model and Quote Engine are trusted.

Cash Flow owns:

* principal repayments
* full finance payment pressure
* liquidity pressure
* principal + interest visibility
* cash repayment burden

Cash Flow must remain separate from Cost Summary.

Principal repayments must not feed:

* total_asset_cost_annual
* total_general_overheads
* total_cost_burden
* required_recovery_rate
* Cost Summary

Cash Flow answers:

**Can the business survive the payment pressure?**

Cost Summary answers:

**What does the business cost to operate per productive hour?**

These must not be mixed.

---

## PHASE 13 — EXECUTION / PRODUCTION CAPACITY LATER

Production Capacity and execution realism belong after the Quote Engine.

They answer:

**Can the business actually deliver the priced work?**

Production Capacity must not be built before:

* baseline model is trusted
* quote logic exists
* business modelling exists
* quote accepted / planning data structure is clear

Future execution layer may include:

* available productive capacity
* required productive capacity
* utilisation required
* capacity gap
* delivery constraints
* asset / crew pressure
* schedule defaults from quote labour allowances later

---

## FINAL DEFINITION

The v3.7 build order makes P&L the raw classification and monthly actuals preparation layer, Revenue / COGS the Margin Pool layer, General Overheads the shared non-asset overhead engine, Labour the people cost and productive output engine, Assets the ownership and asset finance engine, Module Reconciliation the evidence layer, Model Readiness the trust gate, and Cost Summary the forward-looking operating cost summary.

After Cost Summary is trusted, the build proceeds to Business Summary, Business Modelling, Quote Engine, accepted quote snapshots, Feedback Loop later, Cash Flow later, and Production Capacity later.

Quote Engine is now approved as the next commercial layer only when built baseline-first from the v3.7 Quote Engine brief.

The Feedback Loop remains future architecture until accepted quote snapshots and monthly actuals exist.

The system must not move downstream until upstream source ownership and trust are stable.
