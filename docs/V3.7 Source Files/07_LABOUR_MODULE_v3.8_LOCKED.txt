# 1. PURPOSE

Labour is the internal people-cost and productive-hour engine.

It calculates the true annual cost of internal labour and the productive output available to recover business costs.

Labour answers:

```text
What does internal labour actually cost, and how many productive hours are available to recover business cost?
```

It does not answer:

```text
what General Overheads cost
what Assets cost
what Revenue / COGS produced
how the business should recover cost
what recovery strategy should be selected
whether the recovery plan can be delivered
whether the business model works
what quote price should be used
whether accepted quotes matched monthly actuals
what cash-flow pressure exists
```

Those belong to later layers.

---

# 2. PRODUCT ROLE

Labour is the internal people-cost source of truth.

It is not:

```text
General Overheads

Revenue / COGS
Cost Summary
Business Summary
Recovery Summary

Business Outcome
Business Modelling
Quote Engine
Feedback Loop
```

Labour provides true internal labour cost and productive-hour outputs for downstream modules.

Core idea:

```text
All staff cost must be recovered. Only productive staff hours can recover it.
```

# 5. INTERNAL LABOUR PRINCIPLE

This principle must remain visible across Labour, Cost Summary, Business Summary, Recovery Summary, Quote Engine later, and the margin-pool standard:

```text
All staff cost must be recovered. Only productive staff hours can recover it.
```

Labour owns the calculation of productive hours.

Cost Summary uses productive hours to calculate the baseline business recovery pressure per productive hour.

Business Summary may use productive hours as the activity driver in `labour_based` mode.

Recovery Summary may use productive hours when calculating labour recovery pressure.

Quote Engine later may use allowed quote hours to test whether an individual quote supports the baseline.

---

# 6. PRODUCTIVE HOURS RULE

Labour must clearly distinguish:

```text
paid hours
non-productive paid hours
productive hours
```

Productive hours are the only hours that can recover cost.

Non-productive time still costs the business, but it does not create recoverable output.

This distinction is central to QS Tools.

Use this wording where appropriate:

```text
Paid hours are not the same as productive hours.
```

---

# 7. EMPLOYER ACC RULE

Employer ACC levy belongs in Labour.

It is not:

```text
General Overheads
Insurance & Compliance
optional where accurate labour cost is required
```

ACC is part of true employer labour cost.

ACC must be visible in the Labour rate build and included in downstream Labour totals.

Without ACC, productive labour cost is understated.

If productive labour cost is understated, Cost Summary, Recovery Summary, Cost Allocation, Business Outcome, and Quote Engine later may understate recovery pressure.

---

# 8. WHY ACC MATTERS

A user may think labour cost is only the hourly wage.

That is not correct.

True labour cost must include employer obligations, including:

```text
Employer KiwiSaver
ESCT
Employer ACC Levy
```

Use these user-facing statements where appropriate:

```text
Employer ACC Levy is part of true labour cost.
ACC belongs in Labour, not General Overheads.
A wage rate is not the full cost of labour.
```

# 11. FORMULAS

## ACC levy

Per staff profile:

```js
acc_levy_annual = gross_wages_annual * acc_rate_percent
```

The exact ACC calculation method may be refined later based on real ACC rate structure, but the output contract must remain stable.

---

## Employer contribution total

```js
total_employer_contribution_annual =
  total_employer_kiwisaver_annual
  + total_esct_annual
  + total_acc_levy_annual
```

---

## Total labour cost

```js
total_labour_cost_annual =
  total_gross_wages_annual
  + total_entitlements_annual
  + total_employer_contribution_annual
```

---

## Productive labour cost rate

Where exposed:

```js
productive_labour_cost_rate = total_productive_output === 0
  ? 0
  : total_labour_cost_annual / total_productive_output
```

This is true internal labour cost per productive hour.

It is not the same as the whole-business required recovery rate.

# 13. ACTIVE PROFILE AGGREGATION RULE

Labour output contracts must aggregate all active saved Labour profiles.

Active means:

```js
profile.is_active !== false
```

Inactive profiles must not count.

Aggregate Labour outputs should include:

```js
total_labour_cost_annual
total_productive_output
total_gross_wages_annual
total_entitlements_annual
total_employer_kiwisaver_annual
total_esct_annual
total_acc_levy_annual
total_employer_contribution_annual
active_staff
labour_ready
```

`current_staff` may remain available for current form/UI use.

Downstream modules must consume aggregate Labour outputs, not only the currently open form/profile.

---

# 14. READINESS RULE

```js
labour_ready = true
```

when:

```text
at least one active labour profile exists
total_labour_cost_annual exists
total_productive_output exists
total_productive_output > 0 where hour-based recovery is required
employer KiwiSaver is included
ESCT is included
employer ACC levy is included
total_employer_contribution_annual exists
Labour does not expose staff_overheads_total
Labour does not expose employee_overheads_total
Labour does not include General Overheads costs
Labour does not include Assets costs
```

In product-based mode, zero productive hours may still require a warning depending on downstream use, but Business Summary uses `units_sold_annual` as the primary recovery driver.

---

# 15. MODEL READINESS / RECONCILIATION RULE

Module Reconciliation must check that Labour outputs are available and employer ACC is included before the model is trusted.

Model Readiness consumes Labour readiness state.

Module Reconciliation and Model Readiness must not recalculate Labour, KiwiSaver, ESCT, or ACC.

They may only check availability, ownership, and readiness.

---

# 16. COST SUMMARY HANDOFF

Cost Summary must consume Labour outputs only.

Cost Summary must consume:

```js
total_labour_cost_annual
total_productive_output
```

Cost Summary may display Labour components for transparency.

Cost Summary must not:

```text
rebuild Labour maths
recalculate ACC
recalculate KiwiSaver
recalculate ESCT
reclassify productive and non-productive hours
use paid hours
use raw working hours
use pre-productivity hours
use current Labour form only
ignore active saved Labour profiles
```

Cost Summary must use Labour’s final `total_productive_output`.

This must be the final productive output after entitlement and productivity logic has already been applied.

---

# 17. BUSINESS SUMMARY BOUNDARY

Business Summary does not consume raw Labour directly unless explicitly wired through a locked output contract.

Business Summary should primarily consume Labour through Cost Summary outputs:

```text
Labour
→ total_labour_cost_annual / total_productive_output
→ Cost Summary total_cost_burden / required_recovery_rate
→ Business Summary current business mirror
```

Business Summary must not recalculate Labour.

In `labour_based` mode, Business Summary uses `total_productive_output` as the activity driver through the Cost Summary / Business Summary contract.

In `product_based` mode, Labour remains a source cost and productive-capacity input, but Business Summary uses `units_sold_annual` as the primary recovery driver.

---

# 18. RECOVERY SUMMARY BOUNDARY

Recovery Summary may consume Labour-derived productive output only through Business Summary / Recovery Summary contracts.

Recovery Summary must not:

```text
read raw Labour form state
recalculate Labour costs
recalculate ACC
recalculate productive hours
mutate Labour profiles
```

Recovery Summary may calculate labour recovery pressure using:

```js
total_productive_output
labour_recovery_cost
```

but only after those values are exposed through the approved upstream contracts.

---

Cost Allocation must not:

```text

```

Labour owns staff identity and productive capacity.

Cost Allocation owns structural links and dependency status.

---

# 20. BUSINESS OUTCOME BOUNDARY

Business Outcome does not consume raw Labour directly.

Business Outcome consumes Recovery Summary and Cost Allocation outputs.

Labour affects Business Outcome only through:

```text
Labour
→ Cost Summary / Business Summary
→ Recovery Summary
→ Cost Allocation
→ Business Outcome
```

Business Outcome must not recalculate Labour.

---

# 21. BUSINESS MODELLING BOUNDARY

Business Modelling must not mutate Labour.

Business Modelling may use Labour-derived baseline values as scenario inputs only through Business Summary, Cost Summary, or explicit Labour target values.

Scenario labour changes are assumptions only.

They must not overwrite Labour profiles.

If a scenario needs different ACC treatment later, that must be a scenario-only assumption under a later locked brief.

The live Labour ACC model remains unchanged unless the user explicitly updates Labour.

---

# 22. QUOTE ENGINE BOUNDARY

Quote Engine may consume Labour-derived outputs to test internal labour assumptions later.

Quote Engine may need:

```js
productive_labour_cost_rate
charge_out_rate where available
total_productive_output where required through Cost Summary
```

Quote Engine must not ask the user to enter true labour cost if QS Tools already knows it.

Quote Engine must not:

```text
rebuild Labour maths
recalculate entitlements
recalculate KiwiSaver
recalculate ESCT
recalculate ACC
choose ACC CU codes
infer ACC rates
overwrite Labour ACC assumptions
mutate Labour profiles
treat user-entered wage rate as full labour cost
treat subcontract labour as internal Labour unless explicitly assigned as internal labour
```

Quote Engine should use Labour as the source of truth for internal labour cost.

User-facing principle:

```text
Internal labour is checked against your true labour model, including employer costs.
```

---

# 23. SUBCONTRACT LABOUR BOUNDARY

Subcontract labour is not internal Labour by default.

Subcontract labour may belong in:

```text
COGS / Direct Costs
Quote Engine direct cost packages
external labour package costs
```

It must not be confused with internal productive labour capacity.

Only internal staff profiles owned by Labour contribute to:

```js
total_productive_output
```

For labour recovery analysis, subcontract labour should normally be treated as external labour cost unless explicitly reclassified.

---

# 24. FEEDBACK LOOP / MONTHLY ACTUALS BOUNDARY

Monthly actuals may later show labour cost drift or labour recovery variance.

The Feedback Loop may compare accepted quote labour assumptions against monthly business reality at macro level.

However, Feedback Loop must not overwrite Labour assumptions automatically.

Any update to Labour from actuals must be explicit.

Any change to ACC assumptions must be explicit and owned by Labour.

Labour remains the forward-looking people-cost model.

Monthly actuals remain historical reality.

---

# 25. CASH FLOW BOUNDARY

Labour does not own cash-flow modelling.

Labour does not calculate principal repayments or liquidity pressure.

Cash Flow later may consume relevant business model outputs under a separate locked brief.

Labour must not include cash-flow pressure in `total_labour_cost_annual`.

# 27. RATE TRANSPARENCY

Labour-only recovery rates may be displayed inside Labour.

Full business recovery strategy remains downstream and is owned by Recovery Summary / Business Modelling / Quote Engine depending on build phase.

Cost Summary may display baseline required recovery rate, but must not define recovery strategy or pricing.

Quote Engine may later use Labour true cost outputs to check internal labour assumptions inside a quote.

Quote Engine must not calculate ACC itself.

# 33. FINAL DEFINITION

Labour v3.8 is the combined people-cost, productive-hour, and employer-obligation source module.

It calculates true Labour cost including wages, entitlements, KiwiSaver, ESCT, and employer ACC levy.

It exposes:

```js
total_labour_cost_annual
total_productive_output
total_acc_levy_annual
total_employer_contribution_annual
```

to downstream modules.

It provides the internal labour reality that Cost Summary, Business Summary, Recovery Summary, Cost Allocation, Business Outcome, and Quote Engine later can consume through approved contracts.

It does not price quotes, create recovery strategy, create allocation status, create business outcome verdicts, store accepted quote snapshots, perform feedback-loop analysis, or mutate assumptions from monthly actuals automatically.

ACC is Labour-owned.

File 08 is no longer required as a separate source file.
