## PURPOSE

Business Summary is the current business mirror.

It combines the trading result from Revenue / COGS with the operating cost burden from Cost Summary.

Business Summary answers:

**Is the current business generating enough margin pool to carry its operating cost burden?**

It does not answer:

* what should the user change?
* how should the user price?
* what scenario should the business run?
* what quote should be accepted?
* what cash-flow pressure exists?
* did a specific job perform correctly?

Those belong to later layers.

Business Summary v3.6 already defined this page as the factual mirror that combines Revenue / COGS with Cost Summary to show Net Position and required recovery vs current margin per productive hour. This v3.7 update preserves that role and clarifies its boundary against Quote Engine.

---

## PRODUCT ROLE

Business Summary is not a quote tool.

Business Summary is not a modelling tool.

Business Summary is not a recommendation engine.

Business Summary is the macro current-state mirror.

It tells the user:

**This is what the business currently looks like when trading margin is compared against operating cost burden.**

The page should help answer:

**Do your assumptions about how your business runs match reality?**

But it should do this at current-business level, not quote level.

  = Net Position

## KEY USER-FACING IDEA

Business Summary should make this clear:

**Margin pool is what remains after direct costs. It must be enough to carry the business operating cost burden.**

And:

**Required Recovery Rate is not a labour charge-out rate. It is the business break-even pressure per productive hour.**

And:

**Current Margin per Productive Hour shows what the current trading result produces per productive hour.**

---

## WHY THIS PAGE MATTERS

A normal P&L shows accounting results.

Business Summary reorganises those results into commercial reality:

Trading result
vs
Operating burden
vs
Productive output

This reveals whether the business is producing enough margin to carry the cost of operating.

The page should make the current business position obvious without requiring the user to interpret a dashboard.

---

## WHAT THIS PAGE ANSWERS

Business Summary answers:

* What revenue is the business generating?
* What direct costs are consuming that revenue?
* What margin pool remains?
* What does the business cost to operate?
* Is the margin pool enough to carry the operating cost burden?
* What is the current margin per productive hour?
* How does that compare to the required recovery rate?
* Is the current business position trusted, warning, or blocked?

---

## WHAT THIS PAGE DOES NOT ANSWER

Business Summary does not answer:

* should I accept this quote?
* what price should I charge?
* what quote changes close the gap?
* what should my scenario be?
* can I physically deliver the work?
* can I afford the cash flow?
* did this exact job perform correctly?

Those belong elsewhere:

Quote Engine = individual quote test
Business Modelling = scenario testing
Production Capacity = delivery ability
Cash Flow = liquidity pressure
Monthly Actuals / Feedback = prediction vs reality over time

## CORE FORMULAS

### Net Position

net_position = margin_pool - total_cost_burden

Where:

margin_pool comes from Revenue / COGS.

total_cost_burden comes from Cost Summary.

---

### Current Margin per Productive Hour

current_margin_per_productive_hour = margin_pool / total_productive_output

If total_productive_output = 0:

current_margin_per_productive_hour = 0

warning required.

---

### Recovery Gap per Hour

recovery_gap_per_hour =
current_margin_per_productive_hour - required_recovery_rate

If total_productive_output = 0:

recovery_gap_per_hour = 0

warning required.

* this is a recommendation

## TRUST / READINESS RULE

Business Summary is trusted only when:

* Revenue / COGS is ready
* Cost Summary is trusted or usable with warnings
* Model Readiness state is visible
* no blocking upstream checks exist

If model_ready = false:

* Business Summary may display preview values
* values must be marked not trusted
* downstream Business Modelling and Quote Engine must not treat the baseline as final

If model_readiness_status = warning:

* Business Summary may display values
* warnings must remain visible

If model_readiness_status = ready:

* Business Summary outputs are trusted

---

## BUSINESS SUMMARY STATUS VALUES

Allowed values:

* ready
* ready_with_warnings
* not_trusted
* blocked

Suggested interpretation:

ready =
Revenue / COGS and Cost Summary are ready and no blocking warnings exist.

ready_with_warnings =
Business Summary can be displayed but upstream warnings remain visible.

not_trusted =
Business Summary can preview outputs but should not be used as locked baseline.

blocked =
Required upstream values are missing or invalid.

---

## WARNING REQUIREMENTS

Warnings must be explicit.

Do not show vague wording like:

“1 warning”

Instead show what the issue is.

Minimum warnings:

* no_revenue_data
* no_direct_cost_data
* no_margin_pool
* no_cost_summary_data
* no_total_productive_output
* no_required_recovery_rate
* negative_margin_pool
* negative_net_position
* upstream_model_not_ready
* revenue_cogs_not_ready
* cost_summary_not_ready

Warning wording examples:

* Revenue / COGS has no revenue data.
* Revenue / COGS has no direct cost data.
* Cost Summary has no productive output.
* Required recovery rate is zero because productive output is zero.
* Business Summary is preview only because the upstream model is not ready.

## RELATIONSHIP TO COST SUMMARY

Business Summary consumes Cost Summary.

Business Summary must not:

* recalculate Cost Summary
* rebuild Labour maths
* rebuild Asset maths
* rebuild General Overheads maths
* change total_cost_burden
* change required_recovery_rate
* change total_productive_output

Cost Summary owns the operating cost burden.

Business Summary only compares it against Margin Pool.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO REVENUE / COGS

Business Summary consumes Revenue / COGS.

Business Summary must not:

* reclassify P&L lines
* recalculate direct cost ownership
* use micro / activity classifications as calculation drivers
* change margin_pool
* mutate Revenue / COGS

Revenue / COGS owns the trading result.

Business Summary only compares it against Cost Summary.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO QUOTE ENGINE

Quote Engine consumes the business baseline and applies it to individual quotes.

Business Summary does not test individual quotes.

Business Summary does not:

* create quote inputs
* calculate quote gaps
* create repair levers
* compare quote versions
* store accepted quote snapshots
* decide whether a quote should be accepted

Quote Engine owns quote-level leak detection.

Business Summary owns current business mirror.

Boundary statement:

**Business Summary shows whether the current business position supports the recovery baseline. Quote Engine applies that same baseline to individual quotes.**

And:

**Business Summary is the factual mirror. Quote Engine is the quote-level reality check.**

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS MODELLING

Business Modelling consumes Business Summary as the current business baseline.

Business Summary does not create scenarios.

Business Summary does not save editable model states.

Business Summary does not overwrite source modules.

Business Modelling later creates:

Baseline = locked current business

Scenario = editable copy of baseline

Business Summary should provide a clean baseline for Business Modelling but must not become the modelling layer.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO MONTHLY ACTUALS / FEEDBACK LOOP

Monthly Actuals / Feedback belongs later.

Business Summary may later be used as part of the macro feedback loop.

Future loop:

accepted quotes in a month
vs
monthly P&L actuals
vs
business baseline

Business Summary provides the factual business baseline.

It does not perform feedback analysis in V1.

The feedback loop is macro-level first, not job-level first.

Business Summary must not attempt job-level actual costing.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO CASH FLOW

Business Summary does not include principal repayments in Net Position.

Principal repayments are cash-flow pressure.

They belong to the future Cash Flow layer.

Business Summary may later show a separate Cash Flow indicator if a Cash Flow brief is locked, but principal must not alter:

* net_position
* total_cost_burden
* required_recovery_rate
* recovery_gap_per_hour

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO PRODUCTION CAPACITY

Production Capacity belongs later.

Business Summary does not test capacity.

Business Summary may show productive output from Cost Summary, but it does not answer:

**Can the business deliver this work?**

That belongs to Production Capacity.
