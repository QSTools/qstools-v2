# QS Tools — Business Outcome Brief

## v3.8 — VERDICT LAYER

**STATUS:** ACTIVE BUILD BRIEF
**VERSION:** v3.8
**POSITION:** Recovery Summary → Cost Allocation → Business Outcome
**REPLACES / SUPERSEDES:** Older outcome assumptions where the final verdict was blended into Business Summary, Recovery Summary, Cost Allocation, Business Modelling, or Quote Engine
**PRIMARY RULE:** Business Outcome gives the verdict but does not rebuild the evidence.

---

# 1. PURPOSE

Business Outcome is the final verdict layer of the Recovery Chain.

It combines the selected recovery strategy from Recovery Summary with the delivery/dependency evidence from Cost Allocation.

Business Outcome answers:

```text
Does this business model work under the selected recovery plan and delivery structure?
```

It gives the user a clear, plain-English result.

It does not rebuild upstream calculations.

It does not choose the recovery strategy.

It does not create allocation structure.

It does not run scenarios.

It does not price quotes.

---

# 2. CORE FIRST-PRINCIPLES RULE

There must be no hidden assumptions.

Business Outcome must make the final consequence visible.

The locked recovery-chain rule is:

```text
You can choose anything, but you cannot hide from what it causes.
```

Business Outcome is where the consequence becomes a verdict.

It must show whether the selected recovery plan is:

```text
financially viable
structurally supported
externally dependent
strained
not currently viable
```

---

# 3. SYSTEM POSITION

Active flow:

```text
Business Summary
↓
Recovery Summary
↓
Cost Allocation
↓
Business Outcome
```

Expanded system flow:

```text
Business Setup
→ P&L
→ Revenue / COGS
→ General Overheads / Labour / Assets
→ Cost Summary
→ Business Summary
→ Recovery Summary
→ Cost Allocation
→ Business Outcome
→ Business Modelling later
→ Quote Engine later
→ Accepted Quote Snapshot later
→ Monthly Actuals / Feedback Loop later
→ Cash Flow later
```

Business Outcome sits after Cost Allocation because it needs both:

```text
Recovery plan evidence
Delivery/dependency evidence
```

---

# 4. CLEAN ROLE SPLIT

```text
Business Summary = current reality
Recovery Summary = chosen strategy + visible consequence
Cost Allocation = delivery structure + dependency
Business Outcome = verdict
```

More detail:

```text
Business Summary tells us where the business is now.
Recovery Summary tells us what recovery plan has been chosen and what pressure it creates.
Cost Allocation tells us whether the plan is internally supported, externally dependent, strained, or unsupported.
Business Outcome tells us whether the model works.
```

---

# 5. PRODUCT ROLE

Business Outcome is the verdict layer.

It is not:

```text
Business Summary
Recovery Summary
Cost Allocation
Business Modelling
Quote Engine
pricing engine
optimisation engine
cash-flow model
feedback loop
```

Business Outcome must be conclusion-first.

It should help the user understand the state of the model without hunting through all upstream pages.

It must remain traceable back to upstream outputs.

---

# 6. WHAT BUSINESS OUTCOME ANSWERS

Business Outcome answers:

```text
Does the selected recovery plan work?
Is the model viable?
Is the model viable only because it depends on something external?
Is the structure strained?
Is the plan unsupported?
What is the main constraint?
What is the next most important action?
```

---

# 7. WHAT BUSINESS OUTCOME DOES NOT ANSWER

Business Outcome does not answer:

```text
what recovery split should be chosen?
how should the user optimise the model?
what quote price should be used?
what individual quote should be accepted?
what cash-flow pressure exists?
can the business physically schedule the work?
did monthly actuals match predictions?
```

Those belong later.

# 11. BUSINESS OUTCOME STATUS VALUES

Allowed values:

```js
"viable"
"viable_with_dependency"
"at_risk"
"not_viable"
```

---

## viable

Meaning:

```text
The selected recovery plan is financially and structurally supported.
```

Use when:

```text
Recovery Summary is ready
Cost Allocation is ready
allocation_status = ready
net_position is acceptable
recovery gap is not materially negative
no blocking warnings exist
model trust state is ready or warning-usable
```

Display wording:

```text
The current model is supported under the selected recovery plan.
```

---

## viable_with_dependency

Meaning:

```text
The selected recovery plan can work, but it depends on visible external delivery or another declared dependency.
```

Use when:

```text
allocation_status = ready_with_dependency
external delivery is visible or explicitly enabled
no hard blocking issue exists
financial result is not blocked
```

This is not failure.

It is a valid business state with a visible dependency.

Display wording:

```text
The current model can work, but it depends on external delivery capacity.
```

---

## at_risk

Meaning:

```text
The selected recovery plan may work, but pressure, dependency, or financial weakness is material.
```

Use when:

```text
allocation_status = strained
recovery gap is materially negative
net position is weak or negative
coverage is low
warnings are material but not fatal
external dependency is high or unclear
```

Display wording:

```text
The current model is under pressure. The selected recovery plan may be possible, but the structure or recovery gap is weak.
```

---

## not_viable

Meaning:

```text
The selected recovery plan is not currently supported by the visible model and structure.
```

Use when:

```text
allocation_status = not_supported
Recovery Summary is blocked
required recovery structure is missing
share model is invalid
upstream model is not trusted
blocking warnings exist
```

Display wording:

```text
The current model does not currently support the selected recovery plan.
```

# 14. DECISION LOGIC

Business Outcome must be evidence-based.

Recommended decision order:

```text
1. If model_trust_state is blocked/not trusted → not_viable or preview / model_not_trusted
2. If Recovery Summary is blocked/not ready → not_viable / recovery_summary_not_ready
3. If recovery_plan_split is missing or invalid → not_viable / share_model_invalid
4. If component_required_recovery is missing → not_viable / share_model_invalid
5. If activity_driver_value is zero/missing → not_viable / no_recoverable_base
6. If allocation_status = not_supported → not_viable / allocation_not_supported
7. If asset recovery is material but asset structure is missing → not_viable or at_risk / asset_strategy_without_assets
8. If allocation_status = ready_with_dependency → viable_with_dependency / external_delivery_dependency
9. If allocation_status = strained → at_risk / weak_structure
10. If net_position is negative and material → at_risk / negative_net_position
11. If recovery_gap_per_driver is negative and material → at_risk / recovery_gap_negative
12. If allocation_status = ready and no material warnings exist → viable / healthy
13. Otherwise → at_risk / unknown
```

Do not optimise here.

Do not create scenario options here.

Business Outcome gives the current verdict only.

# 18. RECOMMENDED ACTION RULE

Business Outcome must provide one primary recommended action.

Recommended action must be based on the primary constraint.

It must be short, direct, and non-prescriptive.

It must not become consulting advice.

It must not create a strategy automatically.

# 29. RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS MODELLING

Business Modelling comes later.

Business Outcome may become the handoff checkpoint before Business Modelling.

Business Modelling may use the verdict as context, but Business Outcome must not create scenarios.

Correct relationship:

```text
Business Outcome = verdict
Business Modelling = scenario testing later
```

Business Outcome must not optimise recovery mix.

---

# 30. RELATIONSHIP TO QUOTE ENGINE

Quote Engine comes later.

Business Outcome does not test individual quotes.

Business Outcome may later help determine whether the current model is safe to use as a quote benchmark.

Quote Engine owns quote-level leak detection.

Business Outcome must not calculate quote gaps, quote repair levers, accepted quote snapshots, or quote status.

---

# 31. RELATIONSHIP TO CASH FLOW

Cash Flow comes later.

Business Outcome must not include principal repayments in operating viability.

Principal repayments are cash-flow pressure.

They may affect a future Cash Flow verdict, but not the v3.8 Business Outcome verdict unless a later locked Cash Flow integration brief explicitly changes the model.

Business Outcome must not alter:

```js
net_position
total_cost_burden
required_recovery_per_driver
recovery_gap_per_driver
```

because of principal repayments.

---

# 32. RELATIONSHIP TO FEEDBACK LOOP

Feedback Loop comes later.

Business Outcome is a current model verdict.

Feedback Loop is prediction-vs-reality comparison later.

Business Outcome must not compare accepted quote snapshots with monthly actuals.

Business Outcome must not mutate baselines based on actuals.

---

# 33. TRUST RULE

Business Outcome must inherit upstream trust state.

If upstream model trust is blocked:

```text
Business Outcome may display preview only.

```

If upstream model trust is warning:

```text
Business Outcome may produce a warning-state verdict.

```

If upstream model trust is ready:

```text
Business Outcome may issue a trusted verdict, subject to its own warnings.
```

# 40. FINAL DEFINITION

Business Outcome v3.8 is the verdict layer of the Recovery Chain.

It consumes the selected recovery strategy from Recovery Summary and the delivery/dependency evidence from Cost Allocation.

It answers:

```text
Does this business model work under the selected recovery plan and delivery structure?
```

It separates:

```text
viable
viable with dependency
at risk
not viable
```

It makes the primary constraint and next action visible.

It does not rebuild upstream calculations.

It does not optimise.

It does not price quotes.

It does not model cash flow.

It gives the verdict, backed by the evidence already created upstream.
