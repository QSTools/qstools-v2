## PURPOSE

Cost Summary combines locked upstream cost engines into a single total operating cost burden.

Cost Summary answers:

**What does the business cost to run?**

It does not answer:

* how should the business recover it?
* what revenue did the business generate?
* what direct costs / COGS did the business incur?
* what profit target should be applied?
* what should a quote be priced at?
* whether a quote should be accepted?
* whether accepted quotes matched monthly actuals?

Those belong to downstream commercial layers.

---

## PRODUCT ROLE

Cost Summary is the business baseline engine.

It defines the forward-looking operating cost burden that later pages test against.

It is not a P&L page.

It is not Revenue / COGS.

It is not Business Summary.

It is not Business Modelling.

It is not Quote Engine.

It is not Feedback Loop.

Core idea:

**Cost Summary defines the business cost baseline. Quote Engine tests quotes against that baseline.**

## CLEAN ROLE SPLIT

P&L = historical financial reality and classification

## FORWARD-LOOKING COST RULE

Cost Summary is forward-looking.

It is not a historical reconstruction of the P&L.

Cost Summary uses current / forward-looking upstream module outputs to determine what the business needs to recover going forward.

Past P&L values may inform setup and reconciliation, but they must not override locked module outputs.

---

## ASSET COST RULE

Assets is the source of truth for asset ownership cost.

Cost Summary must consume:

* total_asset_cost_annual

Cost Summary may display:

* total_asset_interest_annual

Cost Summary must not recalculate asset interest.

Cost Summary must not use P&L interest as the source of truth for asset finance.

If the P&L interest category includes asset finance, that P&L value is treated as historical context only.

Asset finance interest must come from the Assets module.

---

## CASH FLOW SEPARATION RULE

Principal repayments are real cash pressure but are not operating cost.

Principal must not feed:

* total_asset_cost_annual
* total_cost_burden
* required_recovery_rate
* Cost Summary calculations

Principal belongs in a future Cash Flow layer.

Cost Summary may not include principal repayments as part of operating cost.

Interest belongs in asset cost.

Principal belongs in Cash Flow.

## CORE FORMULAS

total_people_cost_annual =
total_labour_cost_annual

total_business_overheads =
total_general_overheads

total_business_cost_annual =
total_asset_cost_annual + total_business_overheads

total_cost_burden =
total_people_cost_annual

* total_asset_cost_annual
* total_business_overheads

required_revenue =
total_cost_burden

required_recovery_rate =
required_revenue / total_productive_output

If total_productive_output = 0:

required_recovery_rate = 0
warning required

---

## PRODUCTIVE OUTPUT RULE

Cost Summary must use Labour’s final productive output.

This must be the post-entitlement, post-productivity output.

Cost Summary must not use:

* paid hours
* raw working hours
* pre-productivity hours
* estimated hours created inside Cost Summary
* total staff hours including non-productive roles unless Labour has explicitly classified them as productive output

If total_productive_output is missing or zero, Cost Summary must warn and set required_recovery_rate to 0.

---

## REQUIRED RECOVERY RATE WORDING

Required Recovery Rate must be explained clearly.

Use:

**This is not a labour charge-out rate.**

**This is the business break-even pressure per productive hour.**

**It shows what the business must recover across productive hours to carry its operating cost burden.**

Do not imply:

* this is what every staff member should be charged out at
* this is quote price
* this is profit
* this is a recommendation

---

## INTERNAL LABOUR PRINCIPLE

This principle must be visible:

**All staff cost must be recovered. Only productive staff hours can recover it.**

Cost Summary should explain why productive hours are the denominator.

All business cost is real cost.

Only productive output can carry that cost.

total_business_cost_annual =

The main Cost Summary view must remain simple and macro-first.

## TOTAL COST DISPLAY

Show:

* Total People Cost
* Total Asset Cost
* Total General Overheads
* Total Cost Burden
* Total Productive Output

* Required Recovery Rate

It represents:

total operating cost burden ÷ final productive output

Warnings must be explicit.

Minimum warnings:

* no_total_productive_output
* no_total_cost_burden
* no_required_recovery_rate
* model_not_ready
* model_ready_with_warnings
* upstream_labour_not_ready
* upstream_assets_not_ready
* upstream_general_overheads_not_ready
* principal_detected_in_operating_cost
* running_cost_detected_in_assets

## RECONCILIATION RULE

Cost Summary does not own reconciliation.

Cost Summary may display reconciliation status from Module Reconciliation / Model Readiness.

Module Reconciliation owns P&L benchmark comparison.

P&L benchmark totals must not drive Cost Summary calculations.

If mismatch exists:

* show warning / error state from Module Reconciliation
* do not silently ignore
* do not alter Cost Summary totals

---

## REVENUE / COGS BOUNDARY

Cost Summary must not include:

* Revenue
* Direct Costs
* COGS
* Margin Pool
* Gross Margin
* Target Profit
* Quote Margin
* Sale price logic

Revenue / COGS owns:

Revenue - Direct Costs = Margin Pool

Business Summary later compares:

Margin Pool - Total Cost Burden = Net Position

Cost Summary only produces the operating burden side of that comparison.

---

## BUSINESS SUMMARY BOUNDARY

Business Summary consumes Cost Summary outputs.

Cost Summary must not consume Business Summary.

Business Summary answers whether trading Margin Pool carries the operating cost burden.

Cost Summary only defines the operating cost burden.

---

## BUSINESS MODELLING BOUNDARY

Business Modelling may copy Cost Summary outputs into a scenario baseline through Business Summary.

Business Modelling must not mutate Cost Summary.

Scenario adjustments must not overwrite Cost Summary source outputs.

---

## QUOTE ENGINE BOUNDARY

Quote Engine consumes Cost Summary baseline outputs directly or through Business Summary / Business Modelling.

Quote Engine must not:

* recalculate Cost Summary
* alter required_recovery_rate
* alter total_cost_burden
* alter total_productive_output
* treat required_recovery_rate as a labour charge-out rate
* use Cost Summary as quote price
* store accepted quote snapshots inside Cost Summary

Quote Engine uses Cost Summary to test whether a quote supports the business baseline.

---

## FEEDBACK LOOP / MONTHLY ACTUALS BOUNDARY

The Feedback Loop belongs later.

Monthly actuals must not overwrite Cost Summary outputs automatically.

If actuals reveal drift, updates to Cost Summary source modules must be explicit.

Cost Summary remains the forward-looking operating cost baseline.

Monthly actuals remain historical reality.

---

## FINAL DEFINITION

Cost Summary v3.7 is a read-only, forward-looking aggregation layer that consumes Labour, Assets, General Overheads, and Model Readiness outputs only.

It produces total operating cost burden, total productive output, and required recovery rate without reclassifying, distributing, pricing, modelling, or reconstructing historical P&L values.

Cost Summary defines the cost of operating the business going forward and provides the baseline that Business Summary, Business Modelling, and Quote Engine test against.
