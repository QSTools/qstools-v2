# QS Tools — Labour Module ACC Integration & Rate Transparency

## v3.7 — QUOTE ENGINE AND MODEL BOUNDARY UPDATED

**STATUS:** ACTIVE LABOUR ADDENDUM
**VERSION:** v3.7
**FILE NUMBER:** 08
**REPLACES:** Labour ACC Integration v3.6
**BUILD STATUS:** ACTIVE / UPDATE REQUIRED FOR QUOTE ENGINE AND FEEDBACK LOOP BOUNDARY

---

## PURPOSE

This brief locks employer ACC levy into the Labour module as a true employer cost.

ACC must be visible in the Labour rate build and included in downstream Labour totals.

This v3.7 update preserves the v3.6 ACC ownership rule and clarifies that Quote Engine, Business Modelling, and Feedback Loop must not recalculate or overwrite ACC logic.

---

## CORE RULE

Employer ACC levy belongs in Labour.

It is not General Overheads.

It is not Insurance & Compliance.

It is not optional if accurate labour cost is required.

ACC is part of true employer labour cost.

---

## WHY THIS MATTERS

A user may think labour cost is only the hourly wage.

That is not correct.

True labour cost must include employer obligations, including:

* Employer KiwiSaver
* ESCT
* Employer ACC Levy

Without ACC, productive labour cost is understated.

If productive labour cost is understated, Cost Summary and Quote Engine will understate the recovery pressure.

---

## LABOUR CALCULATION

Per staff profile:

acc_levy_annual =
gross_wages_annual * acc_rate_percent

The exact ACC calculation method may be refined based on real ACC rate structure, but the output contract must remain stable.

---

## REQUIRED OUTPUTS

Labour must expose:

* total_acc_levy_annual
* total_employer_contribution_annual
* total_labour_cost_annual

Per-staff outputs may expose:

* acc_cu_code
* acc_rate_percent
* acc_levy_annual

---

## EMPLOYER CONTRIBUTION FORMULA

total_employer_contribution_annual =
total_employer_kiwisaver_annual

* total_esct_annual
* total_acc_levy_annual

---

## TOTAL LABOUR COST FORMULA

total_labour_cost_annual =
total_gross_wages_annual

* total_entitlements_annual
* total_employer_contribution_annual

---

## DOWNSTREAM CONTRACT

Cost Summary must consume Labour outputs only.

Cost Summary must not recalculate ACC.

Module Reconciliation must check that ACC is included before the model is trusted.

Business Summary consumes ACC only through Cost Summary outputs.

Business Modelling consumes ACC only through baseline/scenario values and must not mutate Labour ACC calculations.

Quote Engine consumes true labour cost outputs and must not recalculate ACC.

Feedback Loop may compare actual labour movement later, but must not overwrite ACC assumptions automatically.

---

## DISPLAY REQUIREMENT

Labour summary should show:

* Gross Wages
* Entitlements
* Employer KiwiSaver
* ESCT
* Employer ACC Levy
* Employer Contribution Total
* Total Labour Cost
* Productive Hours

This display is required because ACC materially affects true productive labour cost.

---

## RATE TRANSPARENCY

Labour-only recovery rates may be displayed inside Labour.

Full business recovery strategy remains downstream and is owned by Recovery Summary / Business Modelling / Quote Engine depending on build phase.

Cost Summary may display baseline required recovery rate, but must not define recovery strategy or pricing.

Quote Engine may later use Labour true cost outputs to check internal labour assumptions inside a quote.

Quote Engine must not calculate ACC itself.

---

## QUOTE ENGINE BOUNDARY

Quote Engine may consume Labour-derived true labour cost outputs.

Quote Engine must not:

* calculate ACC levy
* choose ACC CU codes
* infer ACC rates
* overwrite Labour ACC assumptions
* treat user-entered wage rate as full labour cost

Quote Engine should use Labour as the source of truth for internal labour cost.

User-facing principle:

**Internal labour is checked against your true labour model, including employer costs.**

---

## BUSINESS MODELLING BOUNDARY

Business Modelling may model labour recovery or productive output assumptions.

It must not edit Labour ACC settings directly.

If a scenario needs different ACC treatment later, that must be a scenario-only assumption under a later locked brief.

The live Labour ACC model remains unchanged unless the user explicitly updates Labour.

---

## FEEDBACK LOOP / MONTHLY ACTUALS BOUNDARY

Monthly actuals may later show that labour-related costs differ from baseline.

The Feedback Loop may surface that as drift or variance.

It must not automatically rewrite ACC rates or Labour assumptions.

Any change to ACC assumptions must be explicit and owned by Labour.

---

## USER-FACING STATEMENTS

Use these where appropriate:

**Employer ACC Levy is part of true labour cost.**

**ACC belongs in Labour, not General Overheads.**

**A wage rate is not the full cost of labour.**

**Quote Engine uses Labour’s true cost. It does not recalculate ACC.**

**Monthly actuals may reveal labour cost drift later, but they do not automatically overwrite Labour assumptions.**

---

## NOT ALLOWED

ACC logic must not be:

* moved to General Overheads
* grouped into Insurance & Compliance
* recalculated in Cost Summary
* recalculated in Business Summary
* recalculated in Business Modelling
* recalculated in Quote Engine
* recalculated in Feedback Loop
* overwritten automatically from monthly actuals
* treated as optional when accurate labour cost is required

---

---

## FINAL DEFINITION

ACC is a Labour employer cost in QS Tools v3.7.

It must be included in total_employer_contribution_annual and total_labour_cost_annual, then consumed downstream as part of Labour's source-of-truth total.

Quote Engine, Business Modelling, and Feedback Loop may consume Labour-derived outputs later, but must not recalculate or overwrite ACC logic.
