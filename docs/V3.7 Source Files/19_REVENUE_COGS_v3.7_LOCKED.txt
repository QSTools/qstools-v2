## PURPOSE

Revenue / COGS is the trading-side summary layer.

It calculates the margin pool created by the business before operating cost burden is applied.

Revenue / COGS answers:

**What did the business generate after direct costs?**

It answers:

* what did the business sell?
* what did the business directly spend to create those sales?
* what margin pool was left?

It does not answer:

* what does the business cost to run?
* is the business currently viable?
* what should the user change?
* what quote should be accepted?
* what scenario should be modelled?
* what cash-flow pressure exists?

Those belong to other layers.

Revenue / COGS v3.6 already defined this page as the trading-side summary layer that calculates Revenue - Direct Costs = Margin Pool. This v3.7 update preserves that role and clarifies that Margin Pool is the core output consumed by Business Summary and later applied at quote level by Quote Engine.

---

## PRODUCT ROLE

Revenue / COGS is not a P&L replacement.

Revenue / COGS is not Cost Summary.

Revenue / COGS is not Business Summary.

Revenue / COGS is not Quote Engine.

Revenue / COGS is the trading margin layer.

It tells the user:

**This is what trading produced before operating cost burden was applied.**

The page should help answer:

**How much margin pool is the business creating from its revenue and direct costs?**

---

## CLEAN ROLE SPLIT

Revenue / COGS = what trading produces
Cost Summary = what the business costs
Business Summary = whether trading carries cost
Business Modelling = what the business could become
Quote Engine = whether an individual quote creates enough recovery pool
Monthly Actuals later = whether accepted quote predictions matched reality

Revenue / COGS owns the margin pool concept at business level.

Quote Engine later applies the same concept at quote level.

## MARGIN POOL DEFINITION

Margin Pool is the amount left after direct costs are removed from revenue.

It is not final profit.

It is not net position.

It is not target profit.

It is not quote margin.

It is not the same as cash flow.

Margin Pool is the pool available to recover:

* Labour cost
* General Overheads
* Asset ownership cost
* business operating burden
* target profit later

Business Summary later compares Margin Pool against Cost Summary’s operating cost burden.

Quote Engine later applies the same Margin Pool concept to an individual quote.

---

## GROSS MARGIN RULE

Gross margin is not proof of profit.

Revenue / COGS may display gross_margin_percent, but it must not imply that positive gross margin means the business is commercially healthy.

A business can have positive gross margin and still fail to carry operating cost burden.

Business Summary owns the next test.

Quote Engine owns the quote-level test.

---

## DIRECT COST / COGS DEFINITION

Direct Costs / COGS may include:

* materials
* subcontractors
* hired equipment / plant for delivery
* freight / cartage directly related to jobs
* waste / tipping directly related to jobs
* direct consumables
* other direct job costs

Direct Costs / COGS must not include:

* internal Labour module cost unless explicitly represented as direct job labour under a later locked rule
* General Overheads
* asset ownership cost
* asset finance interest
* vehicle running overheads unless directly classified as job COGS
* principal repayments
* cash-flow pressure
* target profit
* pricing margin

---

## SUBCONTRACT LABOUR RULE

Subcontract labour may appear in COGS / Direct Costs.

Subcontract labour is external labour cost.

It must not be confused with internal productive labour capacity.

Subcontract labour must not be treated as internal Labour module recovery unless a later locked brief explicitly allows that treatment.

Future quote and recovery analysis should distinguish:

* internal labour recovery
* subcontract labour cost
* total labour content
* internal vs external labour mix

---

## INTERNAL LABOUR BOUNDARY

Revenue / COGS must not rebuild Labour module cost.

Revenue / COGS must not calculate productive labour cost.

Revenue / COGS must not calculate required recovery rate.

Internal Labour remains owned by Labour and Cost Summary.

Revenue / COGS only classifies and summarises the trading side of the P&L.

---

## MACRO CLASSIFICATION RULE

Revenue / COGS uses macro P&L classification only in v3.7.

Active classifications:

* income
* cogs
* direct_cost where available

Optional micro / activity categories may be stored for future insight, but they must not drive Revenue / COGS calculations in v3.7.

Rule:

Macro classification = active calculation input.

Micro classification = optional stored detail.

## MICRO / ACTIVITY CLASSIFICATION RULE

The P&L may capture optional activity categories such as:

* concrete
* excavation
* subcontract type
* supplier group
* delivery category
* user-defined business activity

Revenue / COGS must not rely on those categories in v3.7.

They may be displayed as supporting detail only.

They must not be required for readiness.

They must not block Revenue / COGS.

They must not change the margin_pool calculation unless a later locked brief explicitly promotes them.

---

## READINESS RULE

revenue_cogs_ready = true when:

* total_revenue exists
* total_direct_costs exists
* required income lines are classified
* required COGS / direct cost lines are classified
* no required trading lines remain unassigned
* no blocking review-required trading items remain unresolved

Optional micro / activity classification is not required for readiness.

If total_revenue = 0, Revenue / COGS may still display, but must show a warning.

---

## WARNING REQUIREMENTS

Warnings must be explicit.

Do not show vague wording like:

“1 warning”

Instead show what the issue is.

Minimum warnings:

* no_revenue_data
* no_direct_cost_data
* no_margin_pool
* zero_total_revenue
* negative_margin_pool
* unclassified_revenue_lines
* unclassified_direct_cost_lines
* review_required_trading_lines
* revenue_cogs_not_ready

Warning wording examples:

* Revenue / COGS has no revenue data.
* Revenue / COGS has no direct cost data.
* Gross margin percent is held at 0 because revenue is 0.
* Some trading lines still need classification.
* Margin Pool is negative because direct costs exceed revenue.

## RELATIONSHIP TO COST SUMMARY

Revenue / COGS does not consume Cost Summary.

Revenue / COGS does not calculate Cost Summary.

Revenue / COGS produces the trading-side output:

* margin_pool

Cost Summary produces the operating-cost outputs:

* total_cost_burden
* required_recovery_rate
* total_productive_output

Business Summary later combines them.

Revenue / COGS must not:

* calculate required_recovery_rate
* calculate total_cost_burden
* consume Labour internals
* consume Assets internals
* consume General Overheads internals

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS SUMMARY

Business Summary consumes Revenue / COGS outputs:

* total_revenue
* total_direct_costs
* margin_pool
* gross_margin_percent where useful
* revenue_cogs_ready
* revenue_cogs_warnings

Business Summary then compares:

Margin Pool

* Total Cost Burden
  = Net Position

Revenue / COGS must not calculate Net Position.

Boundary statement:

**Revenue / COGS creates Margin Pool. Business Summary tests whether that pool carries operating cost burden.**

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS MODELLING

Business Modelling may later use Revenue / COGS outputs as part of the baseline snapshot.

Revenue / COGS must not create scenarios.

Revenue / COGS must not mutate scenario values.

Revenue / COGS remains the trading-side source layer.

Business Modelling owns scenario copies and deltas.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO QUOTE ENGINE

Quote Engine later applies the same margin pool concept at quote level.

Revenue / COGS does not price quotes.

Revenue / COGS does not decide whether work should be accepted.

Revenue / COGS does not calculate quote gaps.

Revenue / COGS does not store accepted quote snapshots.

Revenue / COGS provides the business-level concept of Margin Pool that Quote Engine later applies quote-by-quote.

Boundary statement:

**Revenue / COGS shows the business-level trading margin pool. Quote Engine tests whether an individual quote creates enough recovery pool.**

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO MONTHLY ACTUALS / FEEDBACK LOOP

Monthly Actuals / Feedback belongs later.

Revenue / COGS may later be run month-by-month from imported P&L data.

Monthly Revenue / COGS actuals may feed the macro feedback loop.

Future loop:

accepted quote predictions
vs
monthly P&L actuals
vs
business baseline

Revenue / COGS provides the trading actuals.

It does not perform feedback analysis in V1.

The feedback loop is macro-level first, not job-level first.

Revenue / COGS must not attempt job-level actual costing in V1.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO CASH FLOW

Revenue / COGS does not calculate cash flow.

Revenue / COGS must not include principal repayments.

Principal repayments are cash-flow pressure and belong to the future Cash Flow layer.
