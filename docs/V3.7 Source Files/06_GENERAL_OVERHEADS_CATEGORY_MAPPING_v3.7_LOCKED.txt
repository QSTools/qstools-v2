## PURPOSE

This brief defines how overhead categories should be treated after Employee Overheads and Staff Overheads are removed as standalone modules.

It also defines the boundary between:

* General Overheads
* Labour
* Assets
* COGS / Direct Costs
* Business Modelling
* Quote Engine
* Feedback Loop later
* Cash Flow later

---

## CORE RULE

Staff Overheads is a General Overheads category only.

Vehicle Running Costs is a General Overheads category only.

Non-asset interest is a General Overheads category only.

All roll into total_general_overheads.

None are standalone downstream modules.

Only total_general_overheads flows downstream to Cost Summary.

Category subtotals may support future review, automation, monthly actuals comparison, and reporting.

Category subtotals must not become Quote Engine inputs.

---

## STAFF OVERHEADS MAY INCLUDE

* staff training
* staff welfare
* uniforms
* PPE
* recruitment
* staff amenities
* general HR overheads
* non-wage people support costs

---

## STAFF OVERHEADS MUST NOT INCLUDE

* gross wages
* paid entitlements
* employer KiwiSaver
* ESCT
* employer ACC levy
* PAYE
* Labour module employer contribution outputs
* productive labour cost
* staff charge-out rates
* quote-level labour allowances
* accepted quote labour snapshots

---

## VEHICLE RUNNING COSTS MAY INCLUDE

* fuel
* servicing
* repairs
* maintenance
* registration
* licensing
* tyres
* consumables
* shared vehicle costs
* shared fleet costs
* fleet running costs
* shared maintenance pools

---

## VEHICLE RUNNING COSTS MUST NOT INCLUDE

* asset finance cost
* asset finance interest
* asset lease cost
* depreciation cost
* ownership cost
* replacement recovery
* asset principal repayments
* Labour module costs
* quote-level plant or hired equipment direct costs

---

## NON-ASSET INTEREST MAY INCLUDE

* overdraft interest
* working capital interest
* bank interest
* miscellaneous finance interest
* other non-asset borrowing costs

These may remain in General Overheads.

---

## NON-ASSET INTEREST MUST NOT INCLUDE

* asset finance interest
* vehicle loan interest
* equipment loan interest
* asset lease interest where owned by Assets
* asset principal repayments

Asset finance interest belongs to Assets.

Principal repayments belong to Cash Flow later.

---

## ASSET FINANCE INTEREST RULE

Assets is the source of truth for asset finance interest.

If a P&L interest category includes asset finance, the P&L value is historical context only.

Do not map asset finance interest into General Overheads.

Do not force General Overheads to reconcile exact historical P&L interest to Assets modelled interest.

Any mismatch belongs to Module Reconciliation / Model Readiness as informational context.

---

## ASSET OWNERSHIP COSTS MUST NOT MAP TO GENERAL OVERHEADS

The following must map to Assets, not General Overheads:

* asset finance cost
* asset finance interest
* asset lease cost
* depreciation cost
* ownership cost
* replacement recovery
* ownership-only asset cost

---

## COGS / DIRECT COSTS MUST NOT MAP TO GENERAL OVERHEADS

The following must map to COGS / Direct Costs, not General Overheads:

* materials
* subcontractors
* hired equipment / plant for delivery
* delivery cost
* freight / cartage directly related to jobs
* waste / tipping directly related to jobs
* direct consumables
* other direct job costs
* quote-level direct cost packages

---

## ACC CLARITY

ACC employer levy must be classified as Labour.

General business insurance and compliance costs remain General Overheads.

Do not map ACC employer levy into Insurance & Compliance.

---

## CASH FLOW CLARITY

Principal repayments are cash-flow pressure, not operating cost.

Principal repayments must not map to General Overheads.

Principal repayments must not map to Cost Summary.

A future Cash Flow layer may display:

* asset principal repayment
* total finance payment
* monthly finance pressure
* annual cash repayment burden

This is separate from General Overheads.

---

## MONTHLY ACTUALS CLARITY

Monthly P&L actuals may reveal overhead category drift over time.

Examples:

* vehicle running costs rising above baseline
* staff overheads increasing
* software subscriptions increasing
* non-asset interest changing
* insurance / compliance costs moving materially

These actuals may support review and future automation.

They must not automatically overwrite General Overheads assumptions.

Any update to General Overheads from monthly actuals must be explicit.

---

## FEEDBACK LOOP CLARITY

The Feedback Loop may later use monthly overhead movement as part of the macro business reality signal.

However, category subtotals remain review/detail values.

The Feedback Loop must not treat category subtotals as quote-level truth.

The Feedback Loop must not perform job-level costing from General Overheads categories in V1.

---

## QUOTE ENGINE CLARITY

Quote Engine must not consume General Overheads category subtotals directly.

Quote Engine uses the business baseline created downstream.

The quote test must not ask:

* how much of this quote uses staff overheads?
* how much of this quote uses vehicle running cost?
* how much of this quote uses software subscription overhead?

Those are business overhead assumptions, not quote inputs.

Quote Engine tests whether the quote margin pool supports the business baseline.

---

## DOWNSTREAM RULE

General Overheads may show internal category subtotals, but only this value flows downstream:

* total_general_overheads

Do not create downstream calculation variables for:

* staff_overheads_total
* vehicle_running_total
* employee_overheads_total
* non_asset_interest_total

Category subtotals are for display, review, reconciliation, and future automation only.

---

## DISPLAY RULE

General Overheads may display useful category subtotals such as:

* Staff Overheads
* Vehicle Running Costs
* Non-Asset Interest
* Insurance / Compliance
* Admin / Office
* Software / Subscriptions
* Other Overheads

These are supporting detail only.

They must not become separate Cost Summary inputs.

They must not become Quote Engine inputs.

They must not become Feedback Loop job-costing inputs in V1.

---

## USER-FACING STATEMENTS

Use these where appropriate:

**Overhead categories help review the business cost structure.**

**Only Total General Overheads flows downstream.**

**Category subtotals are not separate quote inputs.**

**Monthly actuals may show overhead drift later, but updates to the overhead model must be explicit.**

---

## NOT ALLOWED

General Overheads category mapping must not:

* create standalone Staff Overheads module logic
* create standalone Vehicle Running Costs module logic
* create standalone Non-Asset Interest downstream variables
* feed category subtotals into Quote Engine
* feed category subtotals into Cost Summary separately
* overwrite baseline assumptions from monthly actuals automatically
* perform job-level costing in V1
* treat category drift as automatic model update
* map COGS / Direct Costs to General Overheads
* map asset ownership costs to General Overheads
* map principal repayments to General Overheads

## FINAL DEFINITION

In v3.7, Staff Overheads, Vehicle Running Costs, and Non-Asset Interest are General Overheads categories only.

They support user review, classification, reconciliation, future automation, and monthly actuals comparison, but roll into one downstream value:

* total_general_overheads

Asset ownership costs and asset finance interest belong to Assets.

Principal repayments belong to the future Cash Flow layer.

Cost Summary consumes only total_general_overheads from General Overheads.

Quote Engine must not consume General Overheads category subtotals directly.

The Feedback Loop may later observe overhead movement at macro level, but must not overwrite the overhead model automatically.
