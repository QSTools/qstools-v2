## HIGHEST-LEVEL PRINCIPLE

QS Tools is built on:

**First principles for everything.**

**Simple in hindsight. Invisible beforehand.**

The system must reveal what was previously hidden in the business.

Every cost must belong to exactly one owner.

Every important output must be traceable, explainable, and defensible.

If a number cannot be defended, it should not be trusted.

Core product question:

**Do your assumptions about how your business runs match reality?**

---

## CORE SYSTEM PHILOSOPHY

QS Tools uses historical accounting data to build a forward-looking commercial model.

The P&L shows what happened.

QS Tools uses that information to classify, reconcile, and model what the business needs to recover going forward.

Past accounting data is input evidence.

Forward-looking commercial cost is the decision basis.

Accepted quotes are locked predictions.

Monthly actuals are reality later.

The Feedback Loop compares them without overwriting either.

## LOCKED SYSTEM FLOW

The active v3.7 flow is:

P&L
→ Revenue / COGS
→ General Overheads / Labour / Assets
→ Module Reconciliation
→ Model Readiness
→ Cost Summary
→ Business Summary
→ Business Modelling
→ Quote Engine
→ Accepted Quote Snapshot later
→ Monthly Actuals / Feedback Loop later
→ Cash Flow later
→ Production Capacity later

Operationally, Cost Summary depends on General Overheads / Labour / Assets / Model Readiness.

Commercially, Revenue / COGS creates Margin Pool and Business Summary compares that Margin Pool against Cost Summary.

---

## CORE SEPARATION

P&L = raw financial input, classification, and future monthly actuals preparation

Revenue / COGS = what trading produces / Margin Pool

General Overheads = shared / unclear / staff overhead / vehicle running / non-asset overhead cost classification

Labour = people cost, employer obligations, productive hours

Assets = ownership-only asset costs and asset finance interest

Module Reconciliation = evidence layer

Model Readiness = trust gate

Cost Summary = what the business costs to operate going forward

Business Summary = current business mirror: Margin Pool vs operating cost burden

Business Modelling = baseline snapshot and scenario benchmark layer

Quote Engine = quote-level leak detection against baseline or selected model

Accepted Quote Snapshot = locked prediction

Monthly Actuals = period-based reality later

Feedback Loop = macro prediction-vs-reality comparison later

Cash Flow = principal repayments, finance payment pressure, and liquidity view later

Production Capacity = can the priced work be delivered later

## MODULE OWNERSHIP SUMMARY

### P&L owns

* raw P&L lines
* P&L classification
* P&L benchmark totals
* income / revenue classification
* COGS / direct cost classification
* review-required status
* excluded status
* WIP / accounting adjustment visibility
* reusable P&L mapping over time
* future monthly actuals by accounting period

P&L does not own downstream source-of-truth cost totals.

P&L benchmark values flow to Module Reconciliation only.

P&L values must not feed Cost Summary calculations directly.

P&L classified income and COGS / Direct Cost outputs may feed Revenue / COGS.

P&L later provides monthly actuals.

P&L does not compare predictions to actuals.

---

### Revenue / COGS owns

* total_revenue
* total_direct_costs
* margin_pool
* gross_margin_percent
* Revenue - Direct Costs = Margin Pool

Revenue / COGS consumes classified P&L income and COGS / direct cost outputs.

Revenue / COGS does not consume Labour, Assets, or General Overheads raw internals.

Revenue / COGS does not calculate Cost Summary.

Revenue / COGS does not calculate Net Position.

Revenue / COGS does not define pricing or scenarios.

Margin Pool is not final profit.

Margin Pool is the pool available to carry the business operating cost burden.

Micro / activity classifications may be stored for future insight but must not drive v3.7 core calculations unless a later locked brief promotes them.

* finance/admin overheads
* non-asset interest

* travel overheads
* unclear / unallocated business costs after review
* total_general_overheads

General Overheads does not own Labour employer obligations.

General Overheads does not own asset ownership costs.

General Overheads does not own asset finance interest.

Only total_general_overheads flows to Cost Summary.

Category subtotals are review/detail only.

Quote Engine must not consume General Overheads category subtotals directly.

### Labour owns

* staff_id
* gross wages
* paid entitlements
* employer KiwiSaver
* ESCT
* employer ACC levy
* productive hours
* final productive output after entitlement and productivity logic
* total_labour_cost_annual
* total_productive_output
* productive_labour_cost_rate where available
* labour_ready

Labour does not own staff overheads.

Labour does not own vehicle running costs.

Labour does not own asset ownership costs.

Labour output contracts must aggregate all active saved Labour profiles.


Cost Summary must use Labour’s final total_productive_output as the recovery denominator.

Cost Summary must not use paid hours, raw working hours, or pre-productivity hours.

Quote Engine may consume Labour-derived true labour cost, but must not recalculate Labour.

### Assets owns

* asset_id

Assets does not own fuel, servicing, repairs, maintenance, registration, licensing, tyres, consumables, shared fleet costs, or labour costs.

Assets must distinguish:

* active asset
* active finance
* paid-off finance
* term-ended finance
* retired asset
* sold asset

Assets readiness is based on valid ownership records, not P&L benchmark matching.

P&L asset benchmark variance remains diagnostic only.

Asset finance interest must be calculated from the current / forward-looking asset finance position.

Asset finance interest must not be sourced from historical P&L interest.

Principal repayments are cash-flow pressure, not operating cost.

Principal repayments must not feed total_asset_cost_annual or Cost Summary.

## DOWNSTREAM CONTRACT RULE

Upstream modules own inputs and calculations.

Downstream modules consume outputs only.

Never rebuild upstream maths downstream.

Never read raw upstream form state in downstream modules unless the relevant brief explicitly allows it.

Never rename locked contract variables without a matching source brief update.

Never use display names as join keys.

Use IDs only for joins.

If a downstream module needs a value, the upstream module must expose it through its output contract.

## COST OWNERSHIP RULE

Every cost must have one owner.

No duplicate cost ownership is allowed.

Examples:

Salary & Wages → Labour
Employer KiwiSaver → Labour
ESCT → Labour
Employer ACC Levy → Labour
Staff Expenses → General Overheads / Staff Overheads category
Entertainment → General Overheads / Staff Overheads category
Vehicle fuel / servicing / repairs / tyres / registration → General Overheads / Vehicle Running Costs
Vehicle finance / asset ownership → Assets
Asset finance interest → Assets
Paid-off asset finance → $0 current finance cost
Non-asset interest / overdraft / working capital interest → General Overheads
P&L interest line → historical / reported context and reconciliation evidence only where it includes asset finance
Revenue / COGS → Revenue / COGS
Quote direct cost packages → Quote Engine
Principal repayments → Cash Flow later, not Cost Summary
WIP Adjustment → Review Required until explicitly resolved
Penalties / Non-Deductible → Excluded unless explicitly included

## MODEL READINESS RULE

The user must never accidentally trust an incomplete model.

If the model is incomplete, QS Tools must say so clearly.

If a number is preliminary, QS Tools must say so clearly.

If a cost is excluded, QS Tools must keep it visible.

If a material item is unresolved, QS Tools must block readiness.

Readiness formula:

```text
model_ready =
pnl_ready
AND general_overheads_ready
AND labour_ready
AND assets_ready
AND reconciliation_ready
AND blocking_checks.length = 0
```

Status formula:

```text
if blocking_checks.length > 0:
  model_readiness_status = "blocked"

else if warning_checks.length > 0:
  model_readiness_status = "warning"

else:
  model_readiness_status = "ready"
```

Business benchmark variance may create warning status.

Business benchmark variance must not block readiness when all source modules are valid.

## COST SUMMARY RULE

Cost Summary formulas:

```text
total_people_cost_annual = total_labour_cost_annual

total_business_overheads = total_general_overheads

total_business_cost_annual = total_asset_cost_annual + total_business_overheads

total_cost_burden =
total_people_cost_annual
+ total_asset_cost_annual
+ total_business_overheads

required_revenue = total_cost_burden

required_recovery_rate = required_revenue / total_productive_output
```

If total_productive_output = 0:

```text
required_recovery_rate = 0
warning required
```

Cost Summary must consume Model Readiness outputs and show not-trusted state when model_ready = false.

Cost Summary must not consume raw P&L lines directly.

Cost Summary must not rebuild Labour, Assets, or General Overheads calculations.

Cost Summary is forward-looking.

Cost Summary uses future-facing operating cost, not historical P&L reconstruction.


It consumes classified P&L income and COGS / direct-cost outputs.

It must not use micro / activity classifications in v3.7 core calculations.

It produces the trading side of the business model.

## BUSINESS SUMMARY RULE

Business Summary combines:

```text
Margin Pool from Revenue / COGS
```

with:

```text
Total Cost Burden from Cost Summary
```

to show:

```text
Net Position
```

It also shows the operational reality:

* Required Recovery Rate
* Current Margin per Productive Hour
* Recovery Gap per Hour

Business Summary is a mirror.

It shows what the business currently is.

It does not recommend actions.

It does not model scenarios.

It does not test individual quotes.

## BUSINESS MODELLING RULE

Business Modelling creates a locked baseline snapshot from the current business model.

Baseline = locked current business.

Scenario = editable copy of baseline.

Upside and Downside scenarios may be provided, but must not overwrite the baseline.

Required Recovery Rate is a reference line, not a scenario.

Business Modelling tests what changes if rates, margins, productivity, or costs move.

Business Modelling must not mutate source modules.

## QUOTE ENGINE RULE

Quote Engine is a quote-level commercial test layer.

Quote Engine applies the trusted baseline or selected scenario to real quote pricing.

At quote level:

```text
Quote Price
- Quote Direct Costs
= Quote Margin / Recovery Pool
```

Then for the baseline build:

```text
Allowed Hours
× Required Recovery Rate
= Required Business Recovery
```

Then:

```text
Quote Margin / Recovery Pool
- Required Business Recovery
= Quote Gap
```

Quote Engine must not treat gross margin as proof of profit.

Quote Engine must not confuse subcontract labour with internal productive labour.

Quote Engine must not include principal repayments as operating cost.

Quote Engine must inherit the trust state of the selected model.

Quote Engine must preserve accepted quote snapshots later.

## FINAL STANDARD

QS Tools is successful when the user can trust the model because every cost, assumption, treatment, variance, prediction, actual, and downstream number can be explained.

The product goal is not to produce a number quickly.

The product goal is to produce a number that can be defended.

The long-term product goal is to connect baseline, quote behaviour, accepted work, and actual monthly P&L performance so the business can see what is happening commercially at any given moment.

QS Tools must show whether the model and fixed-price quotes actually recover the business model.

The system must not allow a quote or model to look commercially healthy merely because gross margin is positive.

The key question is:

**Does the Margin Pool recover the business model?**

If not, QS Tools must make the shortfall visible.
