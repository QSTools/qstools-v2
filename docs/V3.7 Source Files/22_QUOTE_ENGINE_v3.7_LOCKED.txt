## PURPOSE

The Quote Engine is the quote-level leak detection and repair layer of QS Tools.

It applies the trusted business baseline to an individual quote and answers:

**Does this quote recover what the business actually needs?**

It does not simply calculate a quote.

It tests whether the quote’s price, direct costs, labour allowance, and margin pool actually support the business model.

The core user-facing idea is:

**This quote may not be making the margin you think it is.**

---

## PRODUCT LOCK

QS Tools is a quote leak detection system based on the business reflection created from first principles.

A normal quote tool says:

**Cost + markup = price**

QS Tools says:

**This is your price — but does it recover your actual business?**

The Quote Engine exists to prevent quotes from appearing commercially healthy simply because gross margin is positive.

Gross margin is not proof of profit.

A quote is only commercially healthy if the margin / recovery pool is enough to recover the selected business baseline or model.

This updates the earlier v3.6 direction, which already defined Quote Engine as a future quote-level commercial test layer that applies the trusted business model to individual quotes and tests whether quote margin/recovery pool is enough to recover the selected model.

## CORE ROLE

Quote Engine applies the business baseline to a quote and shows whether the quote is leaking against the business recovery requirement.

It should not just say:

* pass
* fail
* profitable
* not profitable

It should show:

* quote gap
* hourly gap
* max viable hours
* required price
* repair levers
* original quote vs adjusted test quote

The Quote Engine should make the gap visible and allow the user to test what changes would close that gap.

Core behaviour:

**Try changes and watch the gap close.**

---

## WHAT THIS PAGE ANSWERS

Quote Engine answers:

* Does this quote work against the current business baseline?
* Is the quote making the margin the user thinks it is?
* Where is the leak?
* How far away is the quote from working?
* What needs to change for the quote to work?
* What happens if price, labour hours, material margin, or direct cost packages change?

Future versions also answer:

* Does this quote work against the upside model?
* Does this quote survive against the downside model?
* Does accepted quote performance match monthly actuals over time?

---

## WHAT THIS PAGE DOES NOT ANSWER

Quote Engine does not answer:

* What does the business currently cost?
* What is the current business position?
* What scenario should the user model?
* Can the business physically deliver the work?
* What cash-flow pressure exists?
* Did this exact job perform correctly after completion?

Those belong to other layers.

Quote Engine is not:

* a full estimating system
* a full takeoff system
* a job-costing module
* a production capacity tool
* a cash-flow tool
* a monthly actuals tool

## CORE CALCULATIONS

### Labour sell

If entered directly:

labour_sell_total = user input

If entered as rate:

labour_sell_total = labour_charge_out_rate × base_labour_hours_allowed

---

### True labour cost

true_labour_cost_total = base_labour_hours_allowed × model_true_labour_cost_rate

Where:

model_true_labour_cost_rate = productive_labour_cost_rate from Labour

---

### Direct cost total

direct_cost_total =
material_cost_total

* true_labour_cost_total
* direct_cost_package_cost_total

---

### Quote margin pool

quote_margin_pool =
quote_price_total - direct_cost_total

This is the money left to carry the business.

---

### Business recovery required

business_recovery_required_total =
base_labour_hours_allowed × required_recovery_rate

---

### Achieved margin per hour

achieved_margin_per_hour =
quote_margin_pool / base_labour_hours_allowed

If base_labour_hours_allowed = 0:

* achieved_margin_per_hour = 0
* hourly_gap = 0
* raise no_labour_hours warning

---

### Hourly gap

hourly_gap =
achieved_margin_per_hour - required_recovery_rate

---

### Quote gap

quote_gap =
quote_margin_pool - business_recovery_required_total

If positive:

quote clears the baseline

If negative:

quote is leaking against the baseline

---

### Max viable hours

max_viable_hours =
quote_margin_pool / required_recovery_rate

If required_recovery_rate = 0:

* max_viable_hours = 0
* raise no_required_recovery_rate warning

---

### Required price at allowed hours

required_price_at_allowed_hours =
direct_cost_total + business_recovery_required_total

---

### Price gap

price_gap =
required_price_at_allowed_hours - quote_price_total

---

## GROSS MARGIN RULE

Gross margin is not proof of profit.

Quote Engine must not treat a positive gross margin as proof that the quote is commercially healthy.

The quote must be tested against the selected business baseline or business model.

Core principle:

A quote is only commercially healthy if the margin / recovery pool is enough to recover the business model requirement.

## INTERNAL LABOUR RULE

Internal labour is:

* hours-driven
* model-validated
* connected to QS Tools Labour

The user does not enter true labour cost.

The user enters or assigns:

* base labour hours allowed
* labour sell total or charge-out rate
* optional role/type allocation later

QS Tools calculates:

* true labour cost
* labour sell
* labour margin
* variance

Internal labour can be reality-checked because QS Tools knows the labour model.

## INTERNAL LABOUR PRINCIPLE

**All staff cost must be recovered. Only productive staff hours can recover it.**

## QUOTE RESULT STATUS

Allowed quote_result_status values:

* works
* marginal
* leaking
* invalid

Suggested interpretation:

works =
quote margin pool is sufficient to recover the selected model requirement.

marginal =
quote is close to break-even or has warnings that require review.

leaking =
quote margin pool is positive but insufficient to recover the selected model requirement.

invalid =
quote cannot be trusted because required quote inputs or upstream model inputs are missing.

Cost Summary defines the operating cost burden and required recovery rate.

Quote Engine must not recalculate Cost Summary.

Quote Engine must not rebuild Labour, Assets, or General Overheads maths.

## RELATIONSHIP TO REVENUE / COGS

Revenue / COGS defines the margin pool concept at business level.

Quote Engine applies the same concept at quote level.

Quote Engine must not mutate Revenue / COGS.

Quote Engine must not treat historical business gross margin as the quote result.

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS SUMMARY

Business Summary is the factual current business mirror.

Quote Engine does not change Business Summary.

Business Summary shows what the business currently is.

Quote Engine tests whether a quote aligns with the business baseline or selected model.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS MODELLING

Business Modelling later provides the selected baseline or scenario.

Quote Engine may use the selected scenario as the quote benchmark.

Quote Engine must not edit or overwrite Business Modelling scenarios.

Quote Engine must not create modelling assumptions silently.

First Quote Engine build may use baseline only.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO PRODUCTION CAPACITY

Production Capacity belongs after Quote Engine.

Quote Engine may later output required internal labour hours or production demand for Production Capacity.

Production Capacity answers:

**Can the business actually deliver this work?**

Quote Engine answers:

**Does this quote recover the model?**

These are separate tests.

A quote may recover the model but still fail capacity.

A quote may fit capacity but fail recovery.

## FINAL DEFINITION

Quote Engine is a quote leak detection and repair layer.

It checks whether a quote’s price, direct costs, labour allowance, and margin pool support the business baseline.

It exposes incorrect assumptions, shows where margin is being consumed, and lets the user test changes until the quote works against baseline, and later against upside and downside scenarios.

It does not build the model.

It does not calculate Cost Summary.

It does not model Cash Flow.

It does not test Production Capacity.

It exists to show whether a quote is leaking money — and what must change to make it work.
