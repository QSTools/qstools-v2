# 1. PURPOSE

General Overheads is the fixed, shared, unclear, staff-related, vehicle-running, and non-asset business overhead classification engine.

It receives P&L-classified overheads and turns them into a clean forward-looking overhead total for downstream modelling.

General Overheads answers:

```text
What shared operating costs does the business carry?
```

It does not answer:

```text
what Labour costs
what Assets cost
what Revenue / COGS produced
how the business should recover cost
what recovery strategy should be selected
whether the recovery plan can be delivered
whether the business model works
what quote price should be used
what cash-flow pressure exists
whether accepted quotes matched monthly actuals
```

Those belong to later layers.

---

# 2. PRODUCT ROLE

General Overheads is an upstream operating overhead source module.

It is not:

```text
Labour
Assets
Revenue / COGS
Cost Summary
Business Summary
Recovery Summary
Cost Allocation
Business Outcome
Business Modelling
Quote Engine
Feedback Loop
Cash Flow
```

It produces one clean downstream source-of-truth value:

```js
total_general_overheads
```

All category detail is for review, transparency, reconciliation, and future automation only.

Category subtotals are not separate downstream recovery inputs.


These are not Assets module costs.

# 11. NON-ASSET INTEREST CATEGORY RULE

Non-Asset Interest is a General Overheads category only.

Non-asset interest may include:

```text
overdraft interest

miscellaneous finance interest
other non-asset borrowing costs
```

Non-asset interest must not include:

```text
asset finance interest
vehicle loan interest
equipment loan interest
asset lease interest where owned by Assets
asset principal repayments
```

Asset finance interest belongs to Assets.

Principal repayments belong to Cash Flow later.

---

# 12. ASSET OWNERSHIP BOUNDARY

Assets owns:

```text
asset finance cost
asset finance interest
lease cost
depreciation cost
ownership cost
replacement / ownership recovery if explicitly enabled
total_asset_cost_annual
```

General Overheads must not consume or duplicate those values.

If a P&L overhead category includes asset ownership cost, that amount is treated as reconciliation context only.

The specialised Assets module remains the source of truth for asset ownership costs.

The following must map to Assets, not General Overheads:

```text
asset finance cost
asset finance interest
asset lease cost
depreciation cost
ownership cost
replacement recovery
ownership-only asset cost
```

---

# 13. ASSET FINANCE INTEREST RULE

Assets is the source of truth for asset finance interest.

If a P&L interest category includes asset finance, the P&L value is historical context only.

Do not map asset finance interest into General Overheads.

Do not force General Overheads to reconcile exact historical P&L interest to Assets modelled interest.

Any mismatch belongs to Module Reconciliation / Model Readiness as informational context.

General Overheads must not:

```text
recalculate asset interest
override asset interest from Assets
use P&L interest as the source of truth for asset finance
force exact reconciliation between historical P&L interest and forward-looking asset interest
```

---

# 14. P&L INTEREST RELATIONSHIP

P&L interest is historical reported accounting data.

P&L interest values may include a mixture of:

```text
asset finance interest
overdraft interest
working capital interest
bank interest
accountant journal adjustments
miscellaneous finance costs
```

General Overheads may use P&L interest as a classification input for non-asset interest.

General Overheads owns only the non-asset portion of interest.

If P&L interest includes asset finance:

```text
asset finance interest is handled by Assets
remaining / non-asset interest may remain in General Overheads where available
any mismatch between P&L reported interest and Assets modelled interest is informational only
```

---

# 15. LABOUR BOUNDARY

Labour owns:

```text

paid entitlements
employer KiwiSaver

```

Employer ACC levy belongs in Labour.

General Overheads must not include employer ACC levy.

Business insurance and compliance costs may remain in General Overheads, but employer ACC levy must not be grouped into Insurance & Compliance.

---

# 17. FORWARD-LOOKING COST RULE

General Overheads supports the forward-looking Cost Summary model.

P&L values may be used as the current best available overhead estimate.

However, where a specialised module owns a clearer forward-looking cost, the specialised module output must take priority.

General Overheads must not pull forward asset ownership costs from historical P&L values.

---

# 18. CASH FLOW SEPARATION RULE

General Overheads does not own cash-flow modelling.

Principal repayments are cash-flow pressure, not operating cost.

Principal repayments must not map to General Overheads.

Principal repayments must not map to Cost Summary.

Principal repayments must not feed:

```js
total_general_overheads
total_cost_burden
required_recovery_rate
```

A future Cash Flow layer may display:

```text
asset principal repayment
total finance payment
monthly finance pressure
annual cash repayment burden
```

This is separate from General Overheads.

```text
→ Cost Summary total_cost_burden
→ Business Summary operating cost burden
```

Business Summary must not recalculate General Overheads.

---

# 24. RELATIONSHIP TO RECOVERY SUMMARY

Recovery Summary does not consume General Overheads directly.

Recovery Summary consumes Business Summary outputs.

General Overheads affects Recovery Summary only through:

```text
General Overheads
→ Cost Summary total_cost_burden
→ Business Summary current reality
→ Recovery Summary selected recovery plan
```

Recovery Summary must not recalculate General Overheads.

---

# 25. RELATIONSHIP TO COST ALLOCATION

Cost Allocation does not consume General Overheads directly.

Cost Allocation consumes Recovery Summary outputs and Labour / Asset structural identity where required.

General Overheads affects Cost Allocation only through the recovery plan created by Recovery Summary.

Cost Allocation must not use General Overheads categories as structure, capacity, or delivery inputs.

---

# 26. RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS OUTCOME

Business Outcome does not consume General Overheads directly.

Business Outcome consumes Recovery Summary and Cost Allocation outputs.

General Overheads affects Business Outcome only through the upstream cost burden already reflected in Business Summary / Recovery Summary.

Business Outcome must not recalculate General Overheads.

---

# 27. RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS MODELLING

Business Modelling must not edit General Overheads directly.

Business Modelling may create scenario-only operating cost adjustments, but those changes must not mutate General Overheads.

Baseline General Overheads remains owned by this module.

Scenario operating cost assumptions remain owned by Business Modelling.

---

# 28. RELATIONSHIP TO QUOTE ENGINE

Quote Engine must not consume General Overheads directly.

Quote Engine consumes baseline/model recovery outputs from Cost Summary, Business Summary, Business Modelling, or later selected model outputs.

Quote Engine must not:

```text
recalculate total_general_overheads
use General Overheads category subtotals as quote inputs
treat overhead categories as quote repair levers
mutate General Overheads
ask how much of a quote uses staff overheads
ask how much of a quote uses vehicle running cost
ask how much of a quote uses software subscription overhead
```

General Overheads is business baseline structure, not quote logic.

Quote Engine tests whether the quote margin pool supports the business baseline.

---

# 29. RELATIONSHIP TO FEEDBACK LOOP / MONTHLY ACTUALS

Monthly P&L actuals may later reveal overhead movement or category drift.

Examples:

```text
vehicle running costs rising above baseline
staff overheads increasing
software subscriptions increasing
non-asset interest changing
insurance / compliance costs moving materially
```

The Feedback Loop may compare monthly actual performance against baseline expectations at macro level.

However, Feedback Loop must not overwrite General Overheads assumptions automatically.

Any update to General Overheads from monthly actuals must be explicit.

General Overheads remains the forward-looking overhead structure.

Monthly actuals remain historical reality.

The Feedback Loop must not treat category subtotals as quote-level truth.

The Feedback Loop must not perform job-level costing from General Overheads categories in V1.

# 36. FINAL DEFINITION

General Overheads v3.8 is the combined post-P&L classification engine and category mapping source for shared, unclear, staff-related, vehicle-running, and non-asset overhead costs.

It produces one downstream source-of-truth value:

```js
total_general_overheads
```

Staff Overheads, Vehicle Running Costs, and Non-Asset Interest are General Overheads categories only.

They support user review, classification, reconciliation, future automation, and monthly actuals comparison.

They do not become separate modules.

They do not become separate Cost Summary inputs.

They do not become Quote Engine inputs.

Asset ownership costs and asset finance interest belong to Assets.

Employer ACC levy belongs to Labour.

COGS / Direct Costs belong to Revenue / COGS.

Principal repayments belong to the future Cash Flow layer.

Business Summary, Recovery Summary, Cost Allocation, Business Outcome, Business Modelling, Quote Engine, and Feedback Loop must consume General Overheads only through the correct downstream layer and must not mutate this module directly.
