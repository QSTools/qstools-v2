## PURPOSE

This brief defines the labour recovery and margin pool standard for QS Tools.

It protects one of the core commercial insights of the product:

```text
A fixed-price quote can show gross margin and still fail to recover internal labour, overheads, assets, and target profit.
```

QS Tools must expose this clearly.

In v3.7, this standard is aligned to the Quote Engine leak detection model.

---

## CORE RULE

For fixed-price and quote-based businesses, QS Tools must not treat gross margin as proof of profit.

The quote must generate enough margin / recovery pool to recover:

* internal labour cost
* general overheads
* asset ownership costs
* business operating burden
* target profit later

If it does not, the quote is under-recovering or commercially leaking against the model.

---

## UPDATED PRODUCT LANGUAGE

Use:

**This quote may not be making the margin you think it is.**

**Gross margin is not proof of profit.**

**Margin Pool is the money left to carry the business.**

**Try changes and watch the gap close.**

Avoid overusing:

* commercially loss-making
* failure
* bad quote
* you should

Use neutral, clear language unless a later UI brief defines stronger wording.

---

## FIXED-PRICE RECOVERY TEST

The core fixed-price test is:

```text
Revenue
- external/direct costs
= margin / recovery pool
```

Then:

```text
margin / recovery pool
- required internal labour recovery
- required general overhead recovery
- required asset ownership recovery
- target profit later
= recovery surplus / shortfall
```

If the recovery surplus / shortfall is negative, the quote is commercially under-recovering against the model.

This may be true even if the quote appears profitable using a simple gross-margin view.

---

## BASELINE QUOTE ENGINE TEST

For the first Quote Engine build, the baseline test may be simplified to:

```text
quote_price_total
- direct_cost_total
= quote_margin_pool
```

Then:

```text
base_labour_hours_allowed
× required_recovery_rate
= business_recovery_required_total
```

Then:

```text
quote_margin_pool
- business_recovery_required_total
= quote_gap
```

If quote_gap is negative, the quote is leaking against the baseline.

---

## EXTERNAL / DIRECT COST RULE

External/direct costs may include:

* materials
* freight
* plant hire
* consumables
* third-party job costs
* subcontract labour where it is bought-in external labour
* direct cost packages

External/direct costs are removed before calculating the margin / recovery pool.

---

## SUBCONTRACT LABOUR RULE

Subcontract labour must be visible separately from internal labour recovery.

Subcontract labour may appear in COGS, direct cost packages, or quote-level external labour cost.

It is not internal productive labour capacity.

For labour recovery analysis, subcontract labour should normally be treated as external labour cost unless explicitly reclassified.

Future analysis should distinguish:

* internal labour recovery
* subcontract labour cost
* total labour content
* internal vs external labour mix

This prevents QS Tools from overstating internal labour recovery when the work is actually being delivered by subcontractors.

---

## INTERNAL LABOUR RECOVERY RULE

Internal labour recovery must be based on the Labour module.

Required internal labour cost rate may be shown as:

```text
productive_labour_cost_rate =
total_labour_cost_annual / total_productive_output
```

Required business recovery rate may be shown as:

```text
required_recovery_rate =
total_cost_burden / total_productive_output
```

Important distinction:

* productive_labour_cost_rate = true labour cost per productive hour
* required_recovery_rate = whole-business recovery pressure per productive hour

Quote Engine must not confuse these two.

---

## REQUIRED RECOVERY RATE RULE

Required Recovery Rate is not a labour charge-out rate.

It is the business break-even pressure per productive hour.

It includes the wider business burden, not just wages.

Use this principle:

**All staff cost must be recovered. Only productive staff hours can recover it.**

---

## QUOTE ENGINE INTERNAL LABOUR CHECK

Quote Engine may use:

* base_labour_hours_allowed
* labour_sell_total
* labour_charge_out_rate
* productive_labour_cost_rate
* required_recovery_rate

Quote Engine should not ask the user to enter true labour cost if Labour already knows it.

Quote Engine calculates:

```text
true_labour_cost_total =
base_labour_hours_allowed × productive_labour_cost_rate
```

Then true labour cost becomes part of direct_cost_total.

---

## MARGIN POOL RULE

Margin Pool is not final profit.

Margin Pool is the amount left after direct costs are removed.

At business level:

Revenue / COGS owns Margin Pool.

At quote level:

Quote Engine applies the same concept:

```text
quote_margin_pool = quote_price_total - direct_cost_total
```

Business Summary compares business Margin Pool against operating cost burden.

Quote Engine compares quote Margin Pool against required business recovery for the hours allowed.

---

## SUMMARY-LEVEL VISIBILITY RULE

QS Tools must expose baseline labour recovery before quote-level analysis exists.

The summary layers should show:

* total labour cost burden
* total productive labour hours
* required labour recovery rate where useful
* required business recovery rate
* current margin per productive hour
* recovery variance / gap
* recovery status

The summary layer should answer:

```text
After direct costs, is there enough margin pool to carry the business?
```

---

## QUOTE BENCHMARK RULE

Quote Engine should show, quote-by-quote:

* quoted revenue / quote price
* external/direct costs
* true internal labour cost
* margin / recovery pool
* required business recovery
* quote gap
* hourly gap
* max viable hours
* required price at allowed hours
* accepted / draft / revised status later

The product should clearly state when:

```text
This quote is under-recovering by $X.
```

or:

```text
This quote is leaking against your baseline by $X.
```

---

## REPAIR LEVER STANDARD

If a quote is short, the system should not prescribe only one solution.

It should show options.

Examples:

* increase price
* reduce internal labour hours
* reduce direct cost package cost
* improve material margin
* combine several small changes

User-facing principle:

**Try changes and watch the gap close.**

---

## COMMERCIAL RECOVERY STATUS

Future status language may include:

* works
* marginal
* leaking
* invalid

Definitions:

```text
works =
margin / recovery pool covers the selected model requirement
```

```text
marginal =
quote is close to baseline or has warnings requiring review
```

```text
leaking =
margin / recovery pool is positive but insufficient to recover the model requirement
```

```text
invalid =
required inputs or trusted model values are missing
```

---

## BUSINESS MODELLING CONNECTION

Later, the same quote should be tested against:

* baseline
* upside
* downside

The quote should not be duplicated.

The same quote should run against multiple benchmarks.

Baseline shows whether the quote works today.

Upside shows whether the quote supports the target business.

Downside shows whether the quote survives pressure.

---

## FEEDBACK LOOP CONNECTION

Accepted quotes become locked predictions later.

Monthly P&L actuals become reality.

Feedback Loop compares accepted predictions against monthly actuals at macro level.

The feedback loop is macro-level first, not job-level first.

This standard helps define what the quote prediction must preserve.

---

## NOT ALLOWED

QS Tools must not:

* treat gross margin as proof of profit
* confuse subcontract labour with internal productive labour
* treat required_recovery_rate as a labour charge-out rate
* ask for true labour cost when Labour already owns it
* force every direct cost package into hours
* claim a specific trade is short unless the user created a trade-level allowance
* overwrite accepted quote snapshots later
* perform job-level actual costing in Feedback Loop V1

---

## FINAL STANDARD

QS Tools must show whether a fixed-price quote actually recovers the business model.

The system must not allow a quote to look commercially healthy merely because gross margin is positive.

The key question is:

```text
Does the margin / recovery pool recover the business baseline for the hours allowed?
```

If not, QS Tools must make the shortfall visible and show which levers can close the gap.
