# QS Tools — Recovery Summary Brief

## v3.8 — STRATEGY + CONSEQUENCE LAYER

**STATUS:** ACTIVE BUILD BRIEF
**VERSION:** v3.8
**POSITION:** Business Summary → Recovery Summary → Cost Allocation
**REPLACES / SUPERSEDES:** Older Recovery Summary assumptions where recovery was hour-only, Cost Summary-only, or disconnected from Business Summary reality
**PRIMARY RULE:** You can choose anything, but you cannot hide from what it causes.

---

# 1. PURPOSE

Recovery Summary defines the selected recovery strategy and shows the consequences of that strategy.

It consumes the current business reality from Business Summary.

It allows the user to choose how cost recovery is distributed.

It immediately shows what that choice causes.

Recovery Summary answers:

```text
How are we choosing to recover the cost burden?
What pressure does that choice create?
How does that compare with current reality?
```

Recovery Summary is not a raw-data entry page.

It is not where the user enters wages, costs, revenue, hours, assets, or P&L information.

It is where the user chooses recovery strategy and sees consequence.

---

# 2. CORE FIRST-PRINCIPLES RULE

There must be no hidden assumptions.

The user may choose a recovery strategy, but the consequence must always be visible.

Locked product rule:

```text
You can choose anything, but you cannot hide from what it causes.
```

The page must always show:

```text
REALITY      = current business position
STRATEGY     = selected recovery plan
CONSEQUENCE  = live pressure, gap, and downstream dependency
```

If the user changes a recovery input, the page must immediately show the impact.

If the consequence is not visible, the page is wrong.

---

# 3. SYSTEM POSITION

Active flow:

```text
Business Summary
↓
Recovery Summary
↓
Cost Allocation
↓
Business Outcome
```

Expanded system flow:

```text
Business Setup
→ P&L
→ Revenue / COGS
→ General Overheads / Labour / Assets
→ Cost Summary
→ Business Summary
→ Recovery Summary
→ Cost Allocation
→ Business Outcome
→ Business Modelling later
→ Quote Engine later
```

Recovery Summary sits after Business Summary because it needs the current business reality first.

Recovery Summary sits before Cost Allocation because Cost Allocation needs the recovery plan before it can test delivery structure.

---

# 4. CLEAN ROLE SPLIT

```text
Business Summary = current reality
Recovery Summary = chosen strategy + visible consequence
Cost Allocation = delivery structure + dependency
Business Outcome = verdict
```

More detail:

```text
Business Summary tells us where the business is now.
Recovery Summary lets the user choose how recovery is intended to happen.
Cost Allocation tests what that choice depends on.
Business Outcome decides whether the model works.
```

---

# 5. PRODUCT ROLE

Recovery Summary is a controlled strategy layer.

It is not:

```text
Business Summary
Cost Allocation
Business Outcome
Business Modelling
Quote Engine
pricing engine
optimisation engine
cash-flow model
```

Recovery Summary must show consequences.

Recovery Summary must not optimise the best recovery mix automatically.

Recovery Summary may guide the user with warnings and visible pressure signals.

Optimisation belongs to Business Modelling later.


# 11. NEW REQUIRED FIELD — recovery_plan_target_per_driver

This is the selected recovery target per active driver.

Default:

```js
recovery_plan_target_per_driver = required_recovery_per_driver
```

It must be derived from Business Summary / Recovery Summary values only.

It must not rebuild Cost Summary.

It must not rebuild Business Summary.

It must not be optimised automatically.

---

# 12. NEW REQUIRED FIELD — recovery_plan_split

This is the selected recovery strategy split.

Shape:

```js
recovery_plan_split = {
  labour_share_percent,
  asset_share_percent,
  overhead_share_percent
}
```

This is a strategy output.

It is not:

```text

a hidden default from history
a quote-level pricing split
```

The user can choose it.

The consequence must be visible.

---

# 13. NEW REQUIRED FIELD — component_required_recovery

This exposes the required recovery by component.

Shape:

```js
component_required_recovery = {
  labour: {
    share_percent: labour_share_percent,
    recovery_cost: labour_recovery_cost,
    required_recovery_rate: required_labour_recovery_rate
  },
  asset: {
    share_percent: asset_share_percent,
    recovery_cost: asset_recovery_cost,
    required_recovery: required_asset_recovery
  },
  overhead: {
    share_percent: overhead_share_percent,
    recovery_cost: overhead_absorbed_cost
  }
}
```

This must be derived from existing Recovery Summary values.

Cost Allocation must consume this field.

Cost Allocation must not rebuild it.

# 14. CORE CALCULATIONS

## Share balance

```js
share_total = labour_share_percent + asset_share_percent + overhead_share_percent
is_balanced = share_total === 100
```

If not balanced, warning required.

---

## Recovery distribution

```js
labour_recovery_cost = total_cost_burden * labour_share_percent / 100
asset_recovery_cost = total_cost_burden * asset_share_percent / 100
overhead_absorbed_cost = total_cost_burden * overhead_share_percent / 100
```

---

## Labour recovery rate

```js
required_labour_recovery_rate = total_productive_output === 0
  ? 0
  : labour_recovery_cost / total_productive_output
```

If `total_productive_output = 0` and labour share is greater than zero, warning required.

---

## Asset recovery requirement

```js
required_asset_recovery = asset_recovery_cost
```

V3.8 keeps this as an annual recovery requirement.

Per-asset utilisation logic belongs to a later locked brief if needed.

---

## Recovery plan target

```js
recovery_plan_target_per_driver = required_recovery_per_driver
```

## labour_based

If:

```js
business_type === "labour_based"
```

Then display:

```text
Recovery driver: Productive hours
Required recovery per hour
Current margin per hour
Recovery gap per hour
$/hour
```

Wording:

```text
Recovery is being viewed through productive hours.
```

Explain:

```text
This is not a labour charge-out rate. It is the business recovery pressure per productive hour.
```

---

## product_based

If:

```js
business_type === "product_based"
```

Then display:

```text
Recovery driver: Units sold
Required recovery per unit
Current margin per unit
Recovery gap per unit
$/unit
```

Wording:

```text
Recovery is being viewed through units sold.
```

Explain:

```text
Required recovery per unit is not COGS per unit. It is the operating cost burden spread across units sold.
```

# 17. GUIDANCE RULE

Recovery Summary may guide the user, but must not optimise automatically.

Allowed guidance:

```text
This increases labour recovery pressure.
This strategy depends more heavily on asset recovery.
This recovery plan creates a larger gap than the current position.
The selected recovery split does not total 100%.
Asset recovery is selected, so Cost Allocation will need asset structure.
Labour recovery is selected, so Cost Allocation will test internal capacity and external delivery dependency.
```

Not allowed:

```text
Use this exact optimal split.
This is the best recovery strategy.
The system recommends this model automatically.
This automatically solves your recovery gap.
```

Optimisation belongs to Business Modelling later.

Recovery Summary is consequence-first, not optimiser-first.

---

# 18. STATUS RULES

Allowed `recovery_summary_status` values:

```js
"ready"
"ready_with_warnings"
"not_trusted"
"blocked"
```

Suggested interpretation:

```text
ready = Business Summary is ready and recovery shares are valid
ready_with_warnings = values can display but warnings remain visible
not_trusted = upstream model or Business Summary is not trusted
blocked = required driver or required Business Summary values are missing
```

Recovery Summary must inherit upstream trust state.

If Business Summary is not trusted, Recovery Summary must not present outputs as final.

---

# 19. WARNING REQUIREMENTS

Warnings must be explicit.

Do not show only:

```text
1 warning
```

Minimum warning keys:

```text
business_summary_not_ready
upstream_model_not_ready
share_not_balanced
no_activity_driver
no_productive_output
no_units_sold
no_required_recovery_per_driver
negative_margin_pool
negative_net_position
recovery_gap_negative
labour_share_without_productive_output
asset_share_without_asset_recovery_base
```

Suggested warning wording:

```text
Business Summary is not ready, so Recovery Summary is preview only.

No activity driver is available, so recovery per output cannot be calculated.

Productive hours are required to calculate labour recovery pressure.

Units sold are required to calculate product-based recovery pressure.

The selected recovery shares do not total 100%.
```

# 24. RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS SUMMARY

Business Summary owns the current reality.

Recovery Summary consumes Business Summary outputs.

Recovery Summary must not rebuild:

```js
activity_driver_type
activity_driver_value
required_recovery_per_driver
current_margin_per_driver
recovery_gap_per_driver
margin_pool
total_cost_burden
net_position
```

If Recovery Summary needs one of those values, Business Summary must expose it.

---

# 25. RELATIONSHIP TO COST ALLOCATION

Cost Allocation consumes Recovery Summary outputs.

Recovery Summary must expose:

```js
recovery_plan_target_per_driver
recovery_plan_split
component_required_recovery
active_recovery_model
```

Cost Allocation tests whether the selected recovery plan can be delivered by internal structure, external dependency, or both.

Recovery Summary does not test delivery structure.

---

# 26. RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS OUTCOME

Business Outcome consumes Recovery Summary and Cost Allocation outputs.

Recovery Summary does not decide the final verdict.

Business Outcome owns:

```text
viable
viable_with_dependency
at_risk
not_viable
```

Recovery Summary only shows the plan and its immediate consequence.

---

# 27. RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS MODELLING

Business Modelling remains later.

Business Modelling may eventually optimise recovery mix, run scenarios, or compare model options.

Recovery Summary must not become that optimiser.

Current rule:

```text
Recovery Summary may guide, but must not optimise.
```

# 32. FINAL DEFINITION

Recovery Summary v3.8 is the strategy and consequence layer.

It consumes Business Summary’s current-reality baseline and allows the user to choose how recovery is intended to happen.

It exposes the selected recovery plan in a clean contract for Cost Allocation.

It does not optimise the plan.

It does not test delivery structure.

It does not produce the final verdict.

It exists to make every recovery choice visible, traceable, and consequence-based.
