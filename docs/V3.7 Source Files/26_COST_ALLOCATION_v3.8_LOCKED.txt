# QS Tools — Cost Allocation Brief

## v3.8 — STRUCTURE, CAPACITY, AND DEPENDENCY LAYER

**STATUS:** ACTIVE BUILD BRIEF
**VERSION:** v3.8
**POSITION:** Recovery Summary → Cost Allocation → Business Outcome
**REPLACES / SUPERSEDES:** Older Cost Allocation assumptions where Cost Allocation owned recovery strategy or treated staff shortage as automatic failure
**PRIMARY RULE:** Not enough internal capacity is not automatic failure. It is a dependency signal.

---

# 1. PURPOSE

Cost Allocation validates whether the selected recovery plan can be delivered by the business structure.

It combines:

```text
Recovery Summary strategy outputs
Labour identity and internal productive capacity
Asset identity and structural capacity
external / scalable delivery dependency indicators
```

Cost Allocation answers:

```text
Can this recovery plan actually be delivered?
What internal structure supports it?
What external dependency does it create?
```

Cost Allocation is not where the recovery strategy is chosen.

Recovery Summary chooses the plan.

Cost Allocation tests the plan.

---

# 2. CORE FIRST-PRINCIPLES RULE

There must be no hidden assumptions.

Cost Allocation must not silently assume:

```text
labour recovery = internal staff only
staff shortage = business failure
subcontract labour = internal labour
asset recovery = automatically deliverable
capacity exists without evidence
external delivery exists without being visible
```

Every dependency must be visible.

Every shortfall must be explained.

Every output must trace back to:

```text
Recovery Summary contract
Labour contract
Assets contract
local Cost Allocation structure
visible external-delivery assumption
```

---

Expanded system flow:


Cost Allocation sits after Recovery Summary because it needs the recovery plan first.

Do not build Cost Allocation before Recovery Summary has a clean output contract.

---

# 4. CLEAN ROLE SPLIT

```text
Business Summary = current reality
Recovery Summary = chosen strategy + visible consequence
Cost Allocation = delivery structure + dependency
Business Outcome = verdict
```

More detail:

```text
Business Summary tells us where the business is now.
Recovery Summary tells us what recovery plan has been chosen.
Cost Allocation tells us whether that plan is internally supported, externally dependent, strained, or unsupported.
Business Outcome tells us whether the model works.
```

---

# 5. PRODUCT ROLE

Cost Allocation is a structural and dependency validation layer.

It is not:

```text
Recovery Summary
Business Summary
Business Outcome
Business Modelling
Quote Engine
pricing engine
cost engine
```

Cost Allocation must not calculate business costs.

Cost Allocation must not choose recovery strategy.

Cost Allocation must not produce the final business verdict.

Cost Allocation must reveal what the selected recovery plan depends on.

---

# 6. CORE PRODUCT RULE

The locked product rule for the recovery chain is:

```text
You can choose anything, but you cannot hide from what it causes.
```

Recovery Summary shows the consequence of the chosen recovery plan.

Cost Allocation shows the delivery dependency created by that plan.

If the selected plan requires more capacity than the internal business currently has, Cost Allocation must show that clearly.

It must not hide the dependency.

It must not automatically call it failure.

# 10. EXTERNAL / SCALABLE DELIVERY PRINCIPLE

Cost Allocation must distinguish between:

```text
constrained internal capacity
scalable external delivery
```

Examples of constrained internal capacity:

```text
internal staff productive hours
owned assets
active operational groups
```

Examples of scalable external delivery:

```text
subcontract labour
labour hire
outsourced delivery
external plant hire
external supply
```

Subcontract labour may sit in Revenue / COGS as direct cost / COGS.

It must not be confused with internal Labour module capacity.

Cost Allocation must not treat lack of internal staff as automatic failure if the business can visibly use scalable delivery.

Correct interpretation:

```text
Internal labour capacity is insufficient; this recovery plan depends on external delivery.
```

Incorrect interpretation:

```text
You do not have enough staff, so the model fails.
```

# 14. NEW REQUIRED FIELD — allocation_status

Allowed values:

```js
"ready"
"ready_with_dependency"
"strained"
"not_supported"
```

---

## ready

Meaning:

```text
Recovery plan is supported by internal structure and capacity.
```

Use when:

```text
Recovery Summary is ready
structure is valid
required internal recovery is supported
no major external dependency is required
no blocking warnings exist
```

Display wording:

```text
Recovery plan is supported by current internal structure.
```

---

## ready_with_dependency

Meaning:

```text
Recovery plan can work, but depends on external delivery or scalable supply.
```

Use when:

```text
internal capacity is insufficient
external delivery is enabled or visibly assumed
shortfall is explainable as dependency, not failure
no blocking structural failure exists
```

This is a valid business state.

It must not be treated as failure.

Display wording:

```text
Recovery plan depends on external delivery capacity.
```

---

## strained

Meaning:

```text
Recovery plan may be possible, but creates heavy pressure or weak coverage.
```

Use when:

```text
coverage is low
internal capacity is heavily stretched
external dependency is high or unclear
warnings exist but no hard failure exists
```

Display wording:

```text
Recovery plan is possible but structurally strained.
```

---

## not_supported

Meaning:

```text
Recovery plan cannot currently be supported by internal structure or visible external dependency.
```

Use when:

```text
Recovery Summary is not ready
required structure is missing
required assets or labour are absent
external dependency is required but not enabled / visible
blocking warnings exist
```

Display wording:

```text
Recovery plan is not currently supported by the visible structure.
```

---

# 15. NEW REQUIRED FIELD — allocation_dependency_type

Allowed values:

```js
"none"
"internal_capacity"
"external_delivery"
"asset_structure"
"mixed"
"unknown"
```

Interpretation:

```text
none = supported internally
internal_capacity = limited by internal labour / internal productive capacity
external_delivery = relies on subcontract / labour hire / outsourced capacity
asset_structure = limited by missing or incomplete asset structure
mixed = more than one dependency type exists
unknown = missing data prevents classification
```

# 17. CAPACITY / DEPENDENCY LOGIC

Cost Allocation must evaluate:

```text
required recovery by component
internal capacity available
shortfall
external delivery availability / dependency
structure validity
coverage strength
```

Simple logic:

```text
If internal capacity supports the plan:
  allocation_status = ready

If internal capacity is short but external delivery is visible:
  allocation_status = ready_with_dependency

If internal capacity is short and external dependency is high or unclear:
  allocation_status = strained

If required structure/capacity is missing and no visible fallback exists:
  allocation_status = not_supported
```

---

# 18. INTERNAL CAPACITY SHORTFALL RULE

Internal capacity shortfall must be treated as a signal, not an automatic failure.

```text
Shortfall = required internal recovery / delivery pressure exceeds visible internal capacity.
```

If external delivery is enabled:

```text
shortfall becomes dependency
```

If external delivery is not enabled:

```text
shortfall becomes strain or not_supported depending on severity and structure
```

---

# 19. STRUCTURAL RULES BY RECOVERY MODEL

## labour_only

```text
asset links optional
operational groups optional
external delivery may be relevant
staff shortfall is dependency, not automatic failure
```

## asset_driven

```text
asset links required
operational groups required where applicable
missing asset structure may block support
external delivery does not replace missing owned-asset structure unless explicitly modelled later
```

## hybrid

```text
mixed structure allowed
partial links allowed
warnings required where coverage is low
external dependency can support labour shortfall if visible
asset structure still required where asset share is material
```

# 20. STRUCTURAL VALIDATION RULES

## Duplicate links

Not allowed:

```text
same active asset_id + staff_id pair
```

## Orphan detection

Show warnings for:

```text
active staff not linked where links are expected
active assets not linked where links are expected
assets not included in operational groups where groups are expected
```

## Group validity

A group is valid only if:

```text
all required_asset_ids exist and are active
required staff count can be met
```

## Coverage

Expose:

```js
staff_coverage_percent
asset_coverage_percent
group_coverage_percent
```

Coverage is a warning / strain signal.

Coverage does not automatically decide the final business verdict.

Business Outcome owns final verdict.

# 32. FINAL DEFINITION

Cost Allocation v3.8 is the structure, capacity, and dependency layer.

It consumes the selected recovery plan from Recovery Summary and tests whether that plan is supported by the visible business structure.

It separates internal constrained capacity from external scalable delivery.

It treats internal shortfall as a visible dependency signal, not automatic failure.

It does not decide the final verdict.

It prepares clean allocation evidence for Business Outcome.
