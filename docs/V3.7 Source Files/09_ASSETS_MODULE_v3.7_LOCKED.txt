## PURPOSE

Assets is the ownership-only asset cost engine.

It calculates the annual cost of owning or financing business assets.

It does not calculate running costs.

Assets answers:

**What does it cost the business to own, finance, lease, or hold active assets?**

It does not answer:

* what vehicle running costs are
* what Labour costs
* what General Overheads cost
* what Revenue / COGS produced
* how the business should recover cost
* what quote price should be used
* whether a quote should be accepted
* whether accepted quotes matched monthly actuals
* what cash-flow pressure exists

Those belong to other layers.

---

## PRODUCT ROLE

Assets is the ownership-only asset cost source of truth.

It is not a running-cost module.

It is not a pricing module.

It is not Quote Engine.

It is not Cash Flow.

It produces clean asset ownership cost outputs for Cost Summary.

Core idea:

**Assets owns asset ownership cost. General Overheads owns running cost. Cash Flow owns principal pressure later.**

---

## CLEAN ROLE SPLIT

Assets = ownership-only asset cost
General Overheads = vehicle/fleet running costs and shared operating costs
Cost Summary = total operating cost burden
Business Summary = current business mirror
Business Modelling = scenario benchmark
Quote Engine = quote-level leak detection
Cash Flow later = principal repayment and liquidity pressure
Feedback Loop later = accepted predictions vs monthly actuals

## ASSET FINANCE INTEREST RULE — FORWARD-LOOKING

Assets is the source of truth for asset finance interest.

Asset interest must be calculated from the Assets module, not from the P&L.

The P&L may contain historical or accountant-journaled interest values, but those values must not be treated as the source of truth for asset finance.

Assets must calculate asset interest using the current asset finance position.

The purpose is to estimate the asset finance interest the business needs to recover going forward, not to reconstruct the exact historical interest charged in the previous P&L period.

---

## ASSET INTEREST CALCULATION BASIS

Each financed asset should support enough finance inputs to estimate current annual asset interest:

* purchase_date
* finance_term_years
* interest_rate
* financed_amount or loan_amount

For each active financed asset:

1. Calculate elapsed time since purchase_date.
2. Calculate loan progress through the finance term.
3. Estimate remaining balance.
4. Calculate current annual interest from the estimated remaining balance.

Simple accepted model:

remaining_term_percent =
1 - finance_progress_percent

estimated_remaining_finance_balance =
loan_amount × remaining_term_percent

asset_interest_annual =
estimated_remaining_finance_balance × interest_rate

This is an approximation only.

The system does not need to build a full amortisation schedule in v3.7 unless a later locked finance schedule brief requires it.

---

## FORWARD-LOOKING COST RULE

Cost Summary is forward-looking.

Assets must therefore output the current / forward annual asset interest estimate, not the historical P&L interest amount.

The system must not calculate asset interest by looking backwards over the previous 12 months unless a future locked Cash Flow or finance schedule brief explicitly requires that.

Cost Summary should use the asset cost expected to apply going forward.

---

## P&L INTEREST RELATIONSHIP

If the P&L interest category includes asset finance, the P&L value is treated as a historical reported value only.

The Assets module still remains the authority for asset finance interest.

The system must not force Assets to reconcile exactly to the P&L interest value.

Correct interpretation:

* asset_interest_annual comes from Assets
* non_asset_interest / other_interest may remain in General Overheads
* any mismatch between P&L reported interest and modelled asset interest is informational only

Do not silently blend historical P&L asset interest with forward-looking asset interest.

Do not rebuild past asset interest merely to reconcile the P&L.

---

## CASH FLOW SEPARATION RULE

Principal repayments are real cash pressure but are not operating cost.

Principal must not feed total_asset_cost_annual.

Principal must not feed Cost Summary.

Assets may calculate or expose principal repayment information later for a Cash Flow layer, but it must remain separate from Cost Summary.

Interest belongs in asset cost.

Principal belongs in Cash Flow.

---

## OPTIONAL CASH FLOW OUTPUTS — FUTURE ONLY

Assets may later expose separate cash-flow support outputs such as:

* asset_principal_repayment_annual
* asset_principal_repayment_monthly
* asset_total_finance_payment_annual
* asset_total_finance_payment_monthly

These outputs must not feed total_asset_cost_annual unless a later locked brief explicitly changes the model.

They are for Cash Flow visibility only.

---

## READINESS RULE

assets_ready = true when one of the following is true:

1. Active assets exist and:

   * total_asset_cost_annual exists
   * ownership-only rules are followed
   * running_cost_annual does not feed total_asset_cost_annual
   * no vehicle running costs are included
   * no labour costs are included
   * asset finance interest is calculated from Assets when finance applies

OR:

2. No active assets exist and:

   * no_active_assets_confirmed = true
   * total_asset_cost_annual = 0

No active assets without explicit confirmation must block readiness.

Missing asset data is not confirmation.

A zero P&L asset benchmark is not confirmation.

---

## COST SUMMARY HANDOFF

Cost Summary must consume only:

* total_asset_cost_annual

Cost Summary must not consume:

* running_cost_annual
* raw asset finance inputs
* asset principal repayments
* vehicle running costs
* asset display categories
* P&L asset benchmark totals as calculation inputs

Cost Summary must not recalculate Assets.

Cost Summary must not recalculate asset interest.

---

## BUSINESS SUMMARY BOUNDARY

Business Summary does not consume Assets directly.

Business Summary consumes Assets only through Cost Summary:

Assets
→ total_asset_cost_annual
→ Cost Summary total_cost_burden
→ Business Summary operating cost burden

Business Summary must not recalculate asset cost.

---

## BUSINESS MODELLING BOUNDARY

Business Modelling must not mutate Assets.

Business Modelling may create scenario-only asset cost or operating burden assumptions, but those changes must not overwrite Assets.

Baseline Assets remains owned by this module.

Scenario asset assumptions remain owned by Business Modelling.

---

## QUOTE ENGINE BOUNDARY

Quote Engine must not consume Assets directly for baseline recovery.

Quote Engine consumes baseline/model recovery outputs from Cost Summary, Business Summary, or Business Modelling.

Quote Engine may later include quote-level direct cost packages such as hired plant or subcontracted equipment.

Those quote-level packages are not Assets module ownership costs.

Quote Engine must not:

* recalculate total_asset_cost_annual
* use asset ownership costs as quote line items
* mutate Assets
* treat principal repayment as quote operating cost
* use asset category subtotals as repair levers

Assets is business ownership structure, not quote logic.

---

## FEEDBACK LOOP / MONTHLY ACTUALS BOUNDARY

Monthly actuals may later show asset cost drift or finance cost differences.

The Feedback Loop may compare accepted quote predictions and monthly actuals at macro level.

However, Feedback Loop must not overwrite Assets assumptions automatically.

Any update to Assets from actuals must be explicit.

Assets remains the forward-looking asset ownership model.

Monthly actuals remain historical reality.

---

## DISPLAY INTENT

Assets should clearly show:

* active assets
* ownership-only annual cost
* finance interest where applicable
* lease cost where applicable
* depreciation / ownership cost where included
* total_asset_cost_annual
* no_active_assets_confirmed state where no assets exist

The page should make obvious:

**Vehicle running costs are not asset ownership costs.**

And:

**Principal repayments are cash-flow pressure, not operating cost.**

---

## USER-FACING STATEMENTS

Use these where appropriate:

**Assets captures ownership-only asset cost.**

**Vehicle running costs belong in General Overheads.**

**Principal repayments belong in Cash Flow later, not Cost Summary.**

**Quote Engine does not use asset ownership costs directly. It uses the business baseline created downstream.**

**Monthly actuals may show asset cost drift later, but they do not automatically overwrite Assets assumptions.**

## NOT ALLOWED

Assets must not:

* calculate recovery rates
* calculate pricing
* calculate productive capacity
* calculate quote gaps
* calculate quote repair levers
* perform feedback-loop analysis
* assign assets to staff
* allocate overheads to assets
* include vehicle running costs
* include labour costs
* include hired equipment / delivery costs
* include principal repayments in total_asset_cost_annual
* feed Quote Engine directly as baseline recovery
* overwrite assumptions from monthly actuals automatically
* force P&L interest to reconcile exactly to asset interest
