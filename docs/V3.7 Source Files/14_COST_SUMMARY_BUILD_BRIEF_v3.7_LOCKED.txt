## CORE PURPOSE

Build the Cost Summary page as a read-only aggregation layer.

Cost Summary defines:

**What the business costs to operate.**

It does not define:

* how the business recovers it
* what revenue was generated
* what direct costs / COGS were incurred
* what profit target should be applied
* what quote price should be used
* what cash-flow pressure exists
* whether a quote should be accepted
* whether accepted quotes matched monthly actuals

Those belong downstream.

## FORWARD-LOOKING BUILD RULE

Cost Summary must be built as a forward-looking operating cost page.

It must not try to recreate the previous P&L period.

It must use current / forward-looking outputs from upstream modules.

Past P&L values may support reconciliation and setup only.

They must not override Labour, Assets, or General Overheads output contracts.

---

## ASSET INTEREST BUILD RULE

Assets is the authority for asset finance interest.

Cost Summary must consume asset ownership cost from Assets.

Cost Summary must not calculate asset interest directly.

Cost Summary must not use P&L interest as the source of truth for asset finance.

If the P&L indicates that interest includes asset finance, that setting belongs to reconciliation / overhead interpretation only.

Cost Summary still consumes asset finance interest from Assets.

---

## CASH FLOW SEPARATION BUILD RULE

Principal repayments must not feed Cost Summary.

Principal repayments are cash-flow pressure, not operating cost.

Do not include principal in:

* total_asset_cost_annual
* total_business_cost_annual
* total_cost_burden
* required_recovery_rate

Future Cash Flow layer may consume principal-related outputs separately.

## CALCULATIONS

total_people_cost_annual =
total_labour_cost_annual

total_business_overheads =
total_general_overheads

total_business_cost_annual =
total_asset_cost_annual + total_business_overheads

total_cost_burden =
total_people_cost_annual

* total_asset_cost_annual
* total_business_overheads

required_revenue =
total_cost_burden

required_recovery_rate =
required_revenue / total_productive_output

If total_productive_output = 0:

required_recovery_rate = 0
warning required

---

## PRODUCTIVE OUTPUT RULE

Cost Summary must use Labour’s final productive output.

This must be the post-entitlement, post-productivity output.

Cost Summary must not use:

* paid hours
* raw working hours
* pre-productivity hours
* estimated hours created inside Cost Summary
* all staff hours if Labour has not classified those hours as productive output

If total_productive_output is missing or zero, Cost Summary must warn and set required_recovery_rate to 0.

---

## REQUIRED RECOVERY RATE WORDING

Required Recovery Rate must be visually clear and explicitly explained.

Use:

**This is not a labour charge-out rate.**

**This is the business break-even pressure per productive hour.**

**It shows what the business must recover across productive hours to carry its operating cost burden.**

Add supporting principle:

**All staff cost must be recovered. Only productive staff hours can recover it.**

## READINESS BEHAVIOUR

If model_ready = false:

* totals may display
* page must show not trusted state
* downstream modelling is blocked
* blocking_modules must be visible
* warning_modules must be visible

If model_ready = true and model_readiness_status = warning:

* totals may display as usable
* warnings remain visible

If model_ready = true and model_readiness_status = ready:

* totals are trusted

---

## WARNING DISPLAY RULE

Warnings must be explicit.

Do not show only:

“1 warning”

Show the actual issue.

Examples:

* Productive output is zero, so Required Recovery Rate is held at 0.
* Model Readiness is blocked by Labour.
* Assets readiness has warnings. Review before relying on downstream outputs.
* Principal repayments must not feed Cost Summary.
* Asset running costs must remain in General Overheads.

---

## RECONCILIATION

Cost Summary may display reconciliation status.

Cost Summary must not own reconciliation.

P&L benchmark totals must not drive Cost Summary calculations.

If mismatch exists:

* show warning / error state from Module Reconciliation
* do not silently ignore
* do not alter Cost Summary totals

Cost Summary must not attempt to reconcile historical P&L asset interest to forward-looking asset interest.

That conflict is informational only.

## PAGE PRINCIPLE

Cost Summary must feel like:

**Result first. Detail on demand.**

The user should immediately understand:

* what the business costs to run
* how many productive hours carry that cost
* what recovery rate is required
* that this is not a labour charge-out rate
* that all staff cost must be recovered by productive hours

The user should not need to inspect every detail to understand the result.

---

## BUSINESS SUMMARY BOUNDARY

Business Summary consumes Cost Summary outputs.

Business Summary compares Margin Pool against Total Cost Burden.

Cost Summary must not calculate Margin Pool or Net Position.

---

## BUSINESS MODELLING BOUNDARY

Business Modelling may create scenario copies of Cost Summary outputs through Business Summary.

Business Modelling must not mutate Cost Summary.

---

## QUOTE ENGINE BOUNDARY

Quote Engine consumes Cost Summary baseline outputs directly or through Business Summary / Business Modelling.

Quote Engine must not:

* recalculate Cost Summary
* alter required_recovery_rate
* alter total_cost_burden
* alter total_productive_output
* treat required_recovery_rate as a labour charge-out rate
* store quote snapshots in Cost Summary

---

## FEEDBACK LOOP / MONTHLY ACTUALS BOUNDARY

The Feedback Loop belongs later.

Monthly actuals must not overwrite Cost Summary automatically.

If actuals reveal drift, updates to source modules and Cost Summary baseline must be explicit.

## FINAL DEFINITION

Cost Summary Build v3.7 creates a read-only, forward-looking aggregation page that consumes trusted upstream module totals and the Model Readiness verdict.

It produces total operating cost burden, total productive output, and required recovery rate.

It does not perform reconciliation, pricing, quote testing, accepted quote storage, feedback-loop analysis, allocation, cash-flow modelling, or recovery strategy.
