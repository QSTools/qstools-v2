# Revenue Reality / Margin Stress Test Page
# Version: v1.0
# Purpose: Expose the gap between P&L GP and usable margin after labour is paid

============================================================
1. PAGE NAME
============================================================

Revenue Reality / Margin Stress Test

Recommended route:

app/revenue-reality/page.jsx

Alternative names if preferred:
- Revenue Reality
- Margin Stress Test
- Revenue / Margin Reality
- GP Stress Test

Do not call it Business Outcome.
Do not call it Revenue / COGS.
Do not merge it into Business Summary.

This is a separate baseline diagnostic page.

============================================================
2. PAGE PURPOSE
============================================================

This page sits between:

Revenue / COGS
Business Summary
Recovery Summary
Business Outcome

Its purpose is to expose the gap between:

1. Traditional P&L GP / Material Margin

and

2. Stress-tested margin after labour is paid

The page answers:

“What does my GP actually deliver after I pay labour?”

It is a baseline truth page, not a forecast, not a scenario model, and not a pricing recommendation page.

============================================================
3. CORE BUSINESS LOGIC
============================================================

Traditional P&L GP / Material Margin:

Revenue - COGS = Material Margin

Stress-tested Margin after Labour:

Material Margin - Total Labour Cost = Stress-Tested Margin after Labour

The page must make clear:

- Material Margin / GP comes from Revenue / COGS
- Labour Cost comes from the Labour module / Cost Summary / Business Summary
- Labour must be paid
- This is not assuming labour is recovered
- This is enforcing that labour is a real cost

============================================================
4. WHY THIS PAGE EXISTS
============================================================

Business Summary already shows the forward proof:

Revenue
minus COGS
minus Labour
equals Residual Margin after Labour

This new page shows the reverse proof:

P&L GP looks like X.
But after labour is paid, the usable margin is Y.

This exposes whether:

- material margin is propping up labour
- labour is consuming the GP
- the P&L GP is misleading
- the business has no margin left for assets, overheads, or profit
- the business is structurally relying on cross-subsidy

============================================================
5. WHAT THIS PAGE IS NOT
============================================================

This page is not:

- Revenue / COGS
- Business Summary
- Recovery Summary
- Cost Allocation
- Business Outcome
- a quote checker
- a pricing model
- a scenario planner
- a forecast
- a product catalogue
- a detailed cost allocation page

It should not change upstream calculations.

It should only consume outputs and present the stress-tested view.

============================================================
6. CRITICAL TERMINOLOGY
============================================================

Use these terms:

- P&L GP
- Material Margin
- Stress-tested Margin after Labour
- Post-Labour Margin
- Residual Margin after Labour
- Margin consumed by Labour
- Margin remaining after Labour

Do NOT call the stress-tested number:

- Real GP
- Negative GP
- Negative material margin
- Actual GP
- Accounting GP

Reason:

Accounting GP remains Revenue minus COGS.

The stress-tested number is an operational margin after labour is enforced.

Preferred title:

Stress-tested Margin after Labour

Preferred explanation:

“This is not accounting GP. It shows what your P&L GP actually leaves behind after labour is paid.”

============================================================
8. CORE OUTPUTS
============================================================

The page should calculate and expose:

1. P&L GP / Material Margin

margin_pool = revenue - cogs

2. Stress-tested Margin after Labour

stress_tested_margin_after_labour = margin_pool - total_labour_cost_annual

3. Labour Consumption of GP

labour_consumption_percent_of_gp = total_labour_cost_annual / margin_pool

4. Margin Remaining Percent

stress_tested_margin_percent_of_revenue = stress_tested_margin_after_labour / revenue

5. Margin Gap

If stress_tested_margin_after_labour < 0:

margin_gap_after_labour = absolute value of stress_tested_margin_after_labour

6. Status

Possible statuses:

- strong_margin_after_labour
- labour_consuming_margin
- labour_exceeds_margin
- no_margin_data
- no_labour_data

============================================================
9. CORE FORMULAS
============================================================

Revenue:

total_revenue

COGS:

total_direct_costs

Material Margin / P&L GP:

material_margin = total_revenue - total_direct_costs

Material Margin %:

material_margin_percent = material_margin / total_revenue

Total Labour Cost:

total_labour_cost_annual

Stress-tested Margin after Labour:

stress_tested_margin_after_labour = material_margin - total_labour_cost_annual

Labour Consumption of GP:

labour_consumption_percent = total_labour_cost_annual / material_margin

Stress-tested Margin % of Revenue:

stress_tested_margin_percent = stress_tested_margin_after_labour / total_revenue

If negative:

labour_shortfall_against_margin = abs(stress_tested_margin_after_labour)

============================================================
11. MODE-SPECIFIC WORDING
============================================================

The calculation can be the same, but wording changes by business type.

------------------------------------------------------------
Hours-Based Business
------------------------------------------------------------

Use language:

- Labour should recover labour.
- Materials should not prop up labour.
- This page shows whether GP is being consumed by labour.
- If the result is negative, labour is not being supported by the current margin structure.

Main question:

“Is material margin hiding a labour recovery problem?”

------------------------------------------------------------
Product / Unit-Based Business
------------------------------------------------------------

Use language:

- Margin after COGS carries the whole business.
- Labour is one of the costs paid from unit margin.
- This page shows how much product margin remains after labour.
- Assets, overheads, and profit still need to be paid after this.

Main question:

“After labour is paid, how much unit/product margin remains?”
