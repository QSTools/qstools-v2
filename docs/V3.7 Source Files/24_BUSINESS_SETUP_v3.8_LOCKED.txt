## PURPOSE

Business Setup is the business identity and mode foundation page.

It captures the minimum information required to define how QS Tools should interpret the business model.

Business Setup answers:

```text
What business is being modelled, and does it recover cost through hours or units?
```

It does not answer:

```text
what does the business cost to run?
what margin pool is being created?
is the business viable?
what recovery strategy should be used?
what should the user change?
what quote should be accepted?
what scenario should be modelled?
```

Those belong to later modules.

---

## PRODUCT ROLE

Business Setup is not a calculation page.

Business Setup is not a dashboard.

Business Setup is not Business Summary.

Business Setup is not Recovery Summary.

Business Setup is not Cost Allocation.

Business Setup is not Business Outcome.

Business Setup is the configuration layer that defines the global business context.

It tells the system:

```text
This is the business name, and this is the output driver model to use.
```

---

## SYSTEM POSITION

Current practical system flow:

```text
Business Setup
↓
P&L
↓
Revenue / COGS
↓
Labour / Assets / General Overheads
↓
Cost Summary
↓
Business Summary
↓
Recovery Summary
↓
Cost Allocation
↓
Business Outcome
↓
Business Modelling
↓
Quote Engine later
```

Simplified role chain:

```text
Business Setup = what mode the business uses
Revenue / COGS = what trading produces
Cost Summary = what the business costs
Business Summary = whether trading carries cost through the selected driver
Recovery Summary = how recovery is intended to happen
Cost Allocation = whether the structure can deliver the recovery plan
Business Outcome = whether the model works
```

## CORE ROLE

Business Setup owns:

```js
business_name
business_type
setup_completed
created_at
updated_at
```

The global business mode controls whether downstream pages interpret recovery through:

```text
productive hours
units sold
```

---

## CORE BUSINESS MODEL PRINCIPLE

QS Tools uses one universal business equation:

```text
Total Business Cost ÷ Output Driver = Required Recovery Per Output
```

The output driver is either:

```text
Hours
Units
```

This keeps the system universal without becoming industry-specific.


Where:

## STORAGE RULE

Business Setup must persist through localStorage during the current build phase.

Storage must not overwrite saved state with blank defaults before localStorage has loaded.

The hook must use a storage-loaded guard before automatic save effects run.

Required behaviour:

```text

user selects business type
user saves business profile
user navigates away
user returns to Business Setup
saved business name and selected business type remain visible
```

## BUSINESS TYPE DEFINITIONS

### Service / Labour-based business

Use when the business mainly sells:

```text
time
labour
services
charge-out hours
productive labour output
```

Examples:

```text
builders
contractors
trades
consultants
professional services
service businesses
```

Activity driver:

```text
productive_hours
```

Recovery display:

```text
$/hour
```

---

### Product / Unit-based business

Use when the business mainly sells:

```text
products
units
subscriptions
licences
users
manufactured outputs
productised services
```

Examples:

```text
retail
online stores
SaaS
manufacturing
subscriptions
product businesses
```

Activity driver:

```text
units_sold
```

Recovery display:

```text
$/unit
```

---

## MODE EFFECT

Business Setup controls downstream interpretation.

### labour_based

Downstream pages should interpret the recovery model through:

```text
productive hours
required recovery per hour
current margin per hour
hourly recovery gap
```

### product_based

Downstream pages should interpret the recovery model through:

```text
units sold
required recovery per unit
current margin per unit
unit recovery gap
```

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO REVENUE / COGS

Revenue / COGS consumes `business_type`.

If `business_type = labour_based`:

```text
Revenue / COGS behaves as the standard trading margin page
units_sold_annual is hidden
```

If `business_type = product_based`:

```text
Revenue / COGS shows units_sold_annual
units_sold_annual becomes the product activity driver used later by Business Summary
```

Business Setup must not calculate Revenue / COGS.

Business Setup must not mutate Revenue / COGS.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS SUMMARY

Business Summary consumes business type and activity driver values.

If `business_type = labour_based`:

```text
Business Summary shows per-hour recovery
```

If `business_type = product_based`:

```text
Business Summary shows per-unit recovery
```

Business Setup must not calculate Business Summary.

Business Setup must not mutate Business Summary.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO RECOVERY SUMMARY

Recovery Summary consumes downstream Business Summary outputs.

Business Setup does not define recovery strategy.

Business Setup only defines the activity-driver mode that allows later pages to interpret recovery correctly.

Recovery Summary owns how recovery is intended to happen.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO COST ALLOCATION

Cost Allocation does not consume Business Setup directly unless a later locked brief requires it.

Cost Allocation should receive the selected recovery and structure context from downstream recovery layers.

Business Setup must not create operational groups, asset links, staff links, delivery units, or allocation rules.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS OUTCOME

Business Outcome consumes downstream recovery, allocation, and summary outputs.

Business Setup does not decide whether the business model works.

Business Setup only defines whether the model is interpreted through hours or units.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO SETTINGS

During development, Business Setup remains visible in the main navigation.

Later, Business Setup should become part of Settings once the setup flow is finalised.

The user-facing message may state:

```text
You can change this later in Settings once the setup flow is finalised.
```


Business Setup is complete when:

```text
/business-setup exists
business_name can be entered
business_type can be selected
allowed business types are labour_based and product_based
selected business type is visually obvious
setup_completed is saved
saved profile persists after navigation and refresh
page remains editable during development
downstream modules can consume useBusinessSetup()
Revenue / COGS can read business_type
Business Summary can read the downstream mode-aware values
build passes
```

---

## FINAL DEFINITION

Business Setup is the business identity and mode foundation layer.

It defines whether QS Tools interprets the business through hours or units.

It does not calculate recovery.

It enables downstream pages to calculate recovery correctly.
