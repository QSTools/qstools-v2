## PURPOSE

Business Modelling is the controlled scenario layer of QS Tools.

It starts from the current Business Summary mirror and lets the user test controlled changes without altering the real model.

Business Modelling answers:

**What happens if we change the business assumptions?**

It does not answer:

* what is the current business position?
* what did the business sell?
* what does the business cost?
* what quote should be accepted?
* what cash-flow pressure exists?
* did a specific job perform correctly?

Those belong to other layers.

Business Modelling v3.6 already defined this page as the controlled scenario layer that starts from Business Summary, creates a locked baseline, and lets the user test scenario changes without mutating source modules. This v3.7 update preserves that role and clarifies how Business Modelling becomes the scenario benchmark source for Quote Engine.

---

## PRODUCT ROLE

Business Modelling is not the current business mirror.

Business Modelling is not a quote tool.

Business Modelling is not a cash-flow tool.

Business Modelling is the scenario benchmark layer.

It tells the user:

**This is what the business could look like if these controlled assumptions changed.**

The page should help answer:

**If my assumptions change, does the business still work?**

But it should do this at business-model level, not quote level.

---

## CLEAN ROLE SPLIT

Business Summary = what the business currently is
Business Modelling = what the business could become
Quote Engine = whether a quote supports that model
Monthly Actuals later = whether accepted quotes matched reality

Business Modelling is the bridge between current reality and quote testing.

## CORE ROLE

Business Modelling is the control layer.

It must create:

Baseline = locked current business
Scenario = editable copy of baseline
Delta = difference between baseline and scenario

The user must be able to adjust the scenario without changing the baseline or any source module.

Business Modelling should create clear model benchmarks that Quote Engine can later consume.

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

Model = Mirror → Then Modify

Business Modelling must always start from the current business state.

It must not start from generic assumptions such as:

* 20% margin
* arbitrary hourly rate
* guessed productivity
* template overheads
* assumed labour recovery
* assumed material margin

The first model is the current business.

Scenario changes must be deliberate and visible.

---

## WHAT THIS PAGE ANSWERS

Business Modelling answers:

* What happens if revenue changes?
* What happens if direct costs change?
* What happens if margin improves or worsens?
* What happens if productive hours increase or decrease?
* What happens if operating cost burden changes?
* What happens if the business performs better than baseline?
* What happens if the business is under pressure?
* What scenario benchmark should Quote Engine test against later?

---

## WHAT THIS PAGE DOES NOT ANSWER

Business Modelling does not answer:

* what is the current business position?
* should I accept a quote?
* what price should I charge?
* what quote changes close the gap?
* can I physically deliver the work?
* can I afford the cash flow?
* did this exact job perform correctly?

Those belong elsewhere:

Business Summary = current business position
Quote Engine = individual quote test
Production Capacity = delivery ability
Cash Flow = liquidity pressure
Monthly Actuals / Feedback = prediction vs reality over time

## BASELINE SNAPSHOT RULE

On first entry to Business Modelling, the system must create a baseline snapshot from Business Summary.

The baseline snapshot is read-only.

The baseline represents the current business as-is.

The baseline must not be edited by the user.

The baseline must not be overwritten automatically.

If upstream data changes, the existing baseline remains frozen until the user explicitly creates or refreshes the baseline.

Recommended control:

Create New Baseline From Current Business

or:

Refresh Baseline From Current Business

This action must be explicit.

The baseline should preserve enough data to compare future scenario changes against the original state.

## SCENARIO RULE

Scenario starts as a copy of baseline.

The user edits scenario values only.

Scenario changes must not mutate:

* P&L
* Labour
* Assets
* General Overheads
* Cost Summary
* Revenue / COGS
* Business Summary
* baseline_snapshot

Scenario must always be reversible.

Required reset action:

Reset Scenario To Baseline

## STANDARD SCENARIO STRUCTURE

Business Modelling should provide three model slots:

1. Baseline
2. Upside Scenario
3. Downside Scenario

These are not unlimited saved profiles in V1.

The system should avoid scenario clutter.

Recommended v3.7 structure:

* one locked Baseline
* one Upside Scenario
* one Downside Scenario
* one active selected model for downstream Quote Engine later

---

## BASELINE MODEL

Baseline is the current trusted Business Summary snapshot.

It answers:

**Does the business work as it currently is?**

Baseline is read-only unless explicitly refreshed.

Baseline is the default model for Quote Engine first build.

---

## UPSIDE MODEL

Upside is the target or improved model.

It answers:

**Does this business support where we want to go?**

Upside may test:

* higher revenue
* improved gross margin
* lower direct costs
* more productive hours
* better margin per productive hour
* lower operating cost pressure
* higher labour recovery

Upside must not invent assumptions.

Auto-fill only what the user has already explicitly defined elsewhere.

Everything else remains baseline until edited.

---

## DOWNSIDE MODEL

Downside is the pressure-test model.

It answers:

**Does this business survive if conditions get worse?**

Downside may test:

* lower revenue
* reduced gross margin
* higher direct costs
* lower productive hours
* higher operating cost pressure
* weaker recovery per hour

Downside must not automatically use the minimum recoverable rate as the downside labour rate.

Required recovery rate is a reference line.

It is not a scenario.

The user decides how much to stress the model.

---

## MINIMUM RECOVERABLE RATE RULE

required_recovery_rate from Cost Summary / Business Summary is a reference line.

It must not be treated as:

* a scenario
* a default downside value
* a pricing recommendation
* a target charge-out rate

It represents the operating cost floor per productive hour.

Scenarios are measured against it.

---

## EDITABLE LEVERS

Business Modelling must stay controlled.

Allowed editable scenario levers in v3.7 should be limited to:

1. Revenue adjustment
2. Material margin / direct cost adjustment
3. Labour charge-out or labour recovery adjustment
4. Productivity / productive output adjustment
5. Operating cost burden adjustment

Do not turn this into a spreadsheet.

Do not add excessive inputs.

Few levers, big impact.

---

## REVENUE LEVER

The revenue lever may adjust:

* scenario_total_revenue
* scenario_margin_pool
* scenario_net_position
* scenario_margin_per_productive_hour

It must not mutate P&L or Revenue / COGS.

It is a scenario-only adjustment.

---

## MATERIAL MARGIN / DIRECT COST LEVER

The material margin or direct cost lever may adjust:

* scenario_total_direct_costs
* scenario_margin_pool
* scenario_margin_per_productive_hour
* scenario_net_position

It must not mutate Revenue / COGS source data.

It must not reclassify P&L lines.

It is a scenario-only adjustment.

---

## LABOUR / RECOVERY LEVER

The labour or recovery lever may adjust:

* scenario_margin_per_productive_hour
* scenario_recovery_gap_per_hour
* scenario_required_recovery_rate where explicitly modelled as scenario assumption

It must not mutate the Labour module.

It must not change actual Labour cost.

It is a scenario-only adjustment.

Where available, Upside may prefill from explicit Labour target settings.

---

## PRODUCTIVITY LEVER

The productivity lever may adjust scenario productive output.

It must not mutate Labour source data.

It must not overwrite Labour’s total_productive_output.

It must be treated as a scenario assumption.

Extreme productivity assumptions should remain visible as assumptions.

---

## OPERATING COST LEVER

The operating cost lever may adjust scenario total_cost_burden.

It must not mutate Cost Summary or upstream modules.

It may represent:

* overhead reduction
* asset cost change
* operating cost pressure
* people cost pressure

It is a scenario-only adjustment.

## CORE SCENARIO FORMULAS

scenario_margin_pool =
scenario_total_revenue - scenario_total_direct_costs

scenario_net_position =
scenario_margin_pool - scenario_total_cost_burden

scenario_margin_per_productive_hour =
scenario_margin_pool / scenario_total_productive_output

scenario_recovery_gap_per_hour =
scenario_margin_per_productive_hour - scenario_required_recovery_rate

scenario_delta_annual =
scenario_net_position - baseline_snapshot.net_position

scenario_delta_per_hour =
scenario_recovery_gap_per_hour - baseline_snapshot.recovery_gap_per_hour

If scenario_total_productive_output = 0:

scenario_margin_per_productive_hour = 0
scenario_recovery_gap_per_hour = 0
warning required.

## LIVE DELTA RULE

Business Modelling must show the live difference between baseline and scenario.

Display both:

* annual change
* per-hour change

Recommended display:

Change vs Current:

Per hour: +$X/hr
Annual: +$Y/year

Hourly change should be prioritised because it connects directly to how the business prices and operates.

Annual change should be shown for scale.

Baseline must remain visible while the user edits scenarios.

The user must not need to remember baseline numbers or flick back to Business Summary.

Recommended structure:

* Baseline card: read-only, muted
* Scenario card: editable
* Delta card: live difference

The user should always see:

* what is real
* what is being tested
* what changed

## SCENARIO COMPARISON DISPLAY

Business Modelling should show Baseline, Upside, and Downside clearly.

Suggested display:

Baseline = current model
Upside = target model
Downside = pressure model

For each model show:

* net position
* required recovery rate
* margin per productive hour
* recovery gap per hour
* annual delta vs baseline
* per-hour delta vs baseline

---

## SELECTED MODEL RULE

Business Modelling may later expose one selected model for Quote Engine.

Selected model may be:

* baseline
* upside
* downside
* active scenario

Business Modelling must always show which model is selected.

Business Modelling must not silently switch selected model.

Quote Engine must not mutate Business Modelling scenarios.

---

## READINESS RULE

modelling_ready = true when:

* Business Summary is ready or usable with warnings
* baseline_snapshot exists
* active scenario exists
* no blocking upstream model readiness issues exist

If Business Summary is not trusted:

* Business Modelling may display preview state only
* scenario outputs must not be treated as final
* baseline must be marked untrusted

---

## TRUST RULE

Business Modelling must never hide the trust state of its baseline.

If the baseline comes from a warning state, the scenario must show that it is based on a warning model.

If the baseline comes from an untrusted state, the scenario must be marked preview only.

Quote Engine later must inherit the selected model trust state.

---

## WARNING REQUIREMENTS

Warnings must be explicit.

Minimum warnings:

* no_business_summary_baseline
* baseline_not_trusted
* scenario_missing
* scenario_productive_output_zero
* scenario_required_recovery_rate_zero
* scenario_assumption_extreme
* selected_model_not_trusted
* upstream_business_summary_warning

Warning wording examples:

* Business Summary is not ready, so the baseline is preview only.
* Scenario productive output is zero, so per-hour outputs are held at 0.
* Selected model has warnings and should not be used as a trusted Quote Engine benchmark.

---

## CASH FLOW BOUNDARY

Business Modelling does not model principal repayments in v3.7.

Principal belongs to the future Cash Flow layer.

Business Modelling may later consume Cash Flow outputs when a Cash Flow brief is locked.

Until then, principal must not alter:

* scenario_total_cost_burden
* scenario_required_recovery_rate
* scenario_net_position
* scenario_recovery_gap_per_hour

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO BUSINESS SUMMARY

Business Modelling consumes Business Summary.

Business Summary is factual.

Business Modelling is hypothetical.

Business Modelling must not change Business Summary.

Business Summary answers:

**What is happening?**

Business Modelling answers:

**What happens if something changes?**

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO QUOTE ENGINE

Quote Engine may consume selected Business Modelling outputs as quote benchmark assumptions.

Business Modelling does not price quotes.

Business Modelling does not decide quote acceptance.

Business Modelling creates scenario benchmarks that Quote Engine can later test individual quotes against.

Boundary statement:

**Business Modelling creates the benchmark. Quote Engine tests the quote against it.**

Quote Engine may later show:

* quote works against baseline
* quote does not meet upside
* quote survives downside

This comparison depends on Business Modelling exposing clean scenario outputs.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO MONTHLY ACTUALS / FEEDBACK LOOP

Monthly Actuals / Feedback belongs later.

Business Modelling may later be updated or refreshed based on monthly actuals, but only through explicit user action or a locked feedback-loop brief.

Monthly actuals must not silently overwrite baseline snapshots or scenario assumptions.

Future loop:

accepted quotes in a month
vs
monthly P&L actuals
vs
business baseline / model

Business Modelling provides the scenario benchmarks.

It does not perform feedback analysis in V1.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO CASH FLOW

Cash Flow later may consume scenario assumptions to show payment pressure.

Cash Flow remains separate from Cost Summary and Business Modelling operating-cost logic.

Business Modelling must not treat cash-flow pressure as operating cost.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO PRODUCTION CAPACITY

Production Capacity belongs later.

Business Modelling may provide scenario productive output assumptions, but it does not validate delivery capacity.

Production Capacity answers:

**Can the business actually deliver this work?**

Business Modelling answers:

**What happens if the business assumptions change?**
