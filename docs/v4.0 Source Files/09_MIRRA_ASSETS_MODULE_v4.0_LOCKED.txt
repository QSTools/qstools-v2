# 1. PURPOSE

The Assets module defines the business asset cost base and productive asset recovery requirement.

It answers:

What assets does the business rely on?
Which assets are productive?
Which assets are non-productive?
What do those assets cost annually?
Which asset-related running costs genuinely belong to productive assets?
How many hours are productive assets actually used?
What hourly recovery rate is required for each productive asset?
Are asset-related cost pools over-assigned, under-assigned, or leaking?
Which productive asset values must pass into Asset Reality?

Assets is a source module.

It does not decide operating group assignment.

It does not pair operators to machines.

It does not define shared assets.

It does not distribute assets across labour groups.

It does not decide whether the recovery model works.

It provides the asset truth that downstream modules consume.

# 4. CORE PRINCIPLE

Assets are real cost.

Mirra must not assume assets are recovered just because revenue or margin exists.

Asset cost must be explicitly visible.

Asset utilisation must be explicit.

Asset recovery must be calculated from asset cost and asset utilisation hours.

Running costs assigned to productive assets must be transferred cleanly from the source pool.

No downstream module may invent asset hours, asset cost, or asset recovery rate.

No downstream module may count the same asset cost twice.

# 6. PRODUCTIVE VS NON-PRODUCTIVE ASSET RULE

Assets must clearly separate:

- productive assets
- non-productive assets

Productive assets are used to deliver work, produce output, or support chargeable activity.

Non-productive assets support the business but do not directly create productive output.

Examples of productive assets:

- excavator used on jobs
- concrete pump used to produce m3 output
- truck used in delivery
- ute used by site crew where required for delivery
- productive plant
- production machinery
- tools or equipment used to produce chargeable work

Examples of non-productive assets:

- personal car
- commute-only ute
- admin vehicle
- office printer
- admin computer
- office furniture
- non-production fitout

Productive assets enter:

productive_asset_rows
total_productive_asset_burden
Asset Reality
Cost Allocation Productive Asset Pool
asset recovery calculations

Non-productive assets remain visible in Assets but do not enter Productive Asset Pool or Asset Reality unless explicitly reclassified.

---

# 7. NON-PRODUCTIVE ASSET RULE

Non-productive assets must not be treated as productive recovery burden.

If an asset does not directly produce work or support chargeable activity, it should not be included in productive asset recovery.

These costs may remain:

- in General Overheads
- in non-productive asset visibility
- in review
- in future cash flow / business support analysis

But they must not flow into:

- Asset Reality productive asset test
- Productive Asset Pool
- productive asset recovery rate
- productive asset burden
- Rate Builder productive asset rate

unless the user explicitly changes the asset to productive.

# 8. WHAT BELONGS IN ASSETS

Assets may include:

- owned vehicles
- leased vehicles
- financed vehicles
- plant
- machinery
- equipment
- trailers
- tools where material enough
- production equipment
- delivery equipment
- asset finance interest where treated as asset-owned
- lease cost where tied to asset use
- depreciation or replacement allowance where enabled
- asset insurance where explicitly treated as asset-owned
- registration where explicitly treated as asset-owned
- asset-specific ownership costs
- productive asset running cost transfers where source pools are assigned to productive assets

The key rule:

Assets owns asset ownership/recovery cost and the productive-asset portion of running cost pools that are explicitly transferred into Assets.

---

# 9. WHAT DOES NOT BELONG IN ASSETS

Assets must not include:

- employee labour cost
- productive labour hours
- subcontractor COGS
- material COGS
- product COGS
- general rent unless asset-specific
- general overheads not linked to asset ownership or productive asset running cost transfer
- fuel unless explicitly modelled as an asset source pool transfer
- repairs unless explicitly modelled as an asset source pool transfer
- servicing unless explicitly modelled as an asset source pool transfer
- GST/tax payable
- principal repayments as operating cost
- owner drawings
- dividends
- income tax
- revenue
- profit target

Fuel, repairs, registration, servicing, tyres, insurance, and consumables may be overheads or asset running cost transfer pools depending setup.

Do not double count them.

---

# 10. OWNERSHIP COST VS RUNNING COST RULE

Assets owns asset ownership cost.

Running costs may sit in General Overheads unless explicitly mapped as asset source pools.

Ownership costs may include:

- finance interest
- lease cost
- depreciation / replacement allowance
- ownership insurance where selected
- registration where selected
- other fixed ownership costs

Running costs may include:

- fuel
- repairs
- maintenance
- servicing
- tyres
- operating consumables
- running-related insurance
- registration / compliance

If running costs are left in General Overheads, they are recovered later through overhead distribution in Cost Allocation.

If running costs are assigned into Assets as productive asset running cost transfers, Assets must apply source-pool guardrails.

No cost can be counted in both General Overheads and Assets without a clear transfer and reconciliation.

---

# 11. PRODUCTIVE ASSET RUNNING COST TRANSFER RULE

Assets may consume asset-related running cost pools only for the portion that genuinely belongs to productive assets.

Examples of asset-related running cost pools:

- asset-related fuel pool
- asset-related insurance pool
- asset-related repairs / maintenance pool
- asset-related registration / compliance pool
- asset-related consumables pool
- asset finance / interest pool where asset-related
- user-created asset-related source pool

These source pools may originate in General Overheads or another traceable source.

The purpose of assigning these pools inside Assets is not to force the whole pool into Assets.

The purpose is to identify the portion of the pool that belongs to productive assets so productive asset recovery is accurate.

---

# 12. PRODUCTIVE ASSET RUNNING COST TRANSFER LOGIC

For each asset-related source pool:

available_pool_amount =
total source pool available

assigned_to_productive_assets =
sum assigned to productive assets

remaining_pool_amount =
available_pool_amount - assigned_to_productive_assets

The assigned portion moves into the productive asset burden.

The remaining portion stays in General Overheads.

Formula:

remaining_pool_amount =
available_pool_amount - assigned_to_productive_assets

over_allocated_amount =
max(0, assigned_to_productive_assets - available_pool_amount)

If assigned_to_productive_assets exceeds available_pool_amount:

allocation_status = over_allocated
asset_pool_guardrail_status = blocked

---

# 13. REMAINING BALANCE RULE

The remaining balance of any running cost pool does not need to be manually moved somewhere else.

It stays in General Overheads.

It is later recovered through overhead distribution in Cost Allocation.

Do not force the remaining balance into Assets.

Do not force 100% assignment of running cost pools to productive assets.

Do not zero out overhead pools just to make Assets appear fully assigned.

The system balances at the total source-pool level, not by forcing every pool into Assets.

---

# 14. NO DOUBLE COUNTING RULE

A transferred running cost amount must not be counted twice.

If $70,000 of fuel is transferred to productive assets, then the General Overheads pool used downstream must be reduced by $70,000 or must clearly mark that $70,000 as transferred to Assets.

Correct:

Fuel source pool = $100,000
Transferred to productive assets = $70,000
Remaining overhead fuel = $30,000

Incorrect:

Assets include $70,000 fuel
General Overheads still include full $100,000 fuel
Total model counts $170,000 fuel

That is a leak.

---

# 15. GENERAL OVERHEADS TRANSFER RELATIONSHIP

General Overheads owns the original overhead source pool.

Assets may consume a portion of an asset-related overhead pool only through explicit transfer.

Assets must expose the transfer amount so General Overheads / Cost Summary / Cost Allocation can avoid double counting.

Required transfer variables:

asset_running_cost_transfers[]
total_asset_running_cost_transferred
remaining_overhead_after_asset_transfers

Each transfer row should include:

{
  transfer_id,
  source_pool_id,
  source_pool_name,
  source_module,
  available_pool_amount,
  assigned_to_productive_assets,
  remaining_pool_amount,
  over_allocated_amount,
  allocation_status
}

General Overheads remains the owner of the original source pool.

Assets owns the assigned productive-asset portion once transferred.

Cost Summary consumes the net result so the same source cost is not counted twice.

---

# 16. COST SUMMARY BALANCE RULE

Cost Summary must remain balanced.

The correct total operating cost burden is:

Labour
+ Assets
+ General Overheads
= Total Cost Burden

Where Assets may include:

- asset ownership cost
- asset finance / lease / depreciation / replacement cost
- transferred productive asset running costs

And General Overheads must exclude or mark as transferred:

- running cost amounts transferred into Assets

If asset running cost transfers exist, Cost Summary must use:

net_general_overheads =
gross_general_overheads - total_asset_running_cost_transferred

or an equivalent no-double-counting method.

Do not count transferred productive asset running costs in both Assets and General Overheads.

---

# 17. PRINCIPAL REPAYMENT RULE

Principal repayment is not baseline operating recovery cost.

Principal repayment belongs to future Cash Flow / finance-pressure analysis unless a future active brief explicitly changes this.

Asset finance interest may be operating cost.

Principal must not be included in total_asset_cost_annual.

If users enter loan repayment totals, the system must separate:

- interest
- principal

Only interest may be included in operating cost unless a future brief says otherwise.

# 21. UTILISATION RULE

Every productive asset should have utilisation hours.

Required variables:

utilisation_hours_per_week
utilisation_hours_annual
default_utilisation_hours_per_week

Default:

default_utilisation_hours_per_week = 40

Formula:

utilisation_hours_annual =
utilisation_hours_per_week × 48

Use 48 weeks.

Do not use 52 weeks unless a later active brief changes the rule.

If utilisation_hours_per_week is missing, default to 40 only if the user has not set a value.

Default must be visible.

Do not hide default assumptions.

---

# 22. ASSET REALITY UTILISATION RULE

The utilisation_hours_per_week entered in the Assets module is the required denominator for Asset Reality.

Asset Reality must use:

asset utilisation hours

not:

labour hours

not:

business productive hours

not:

Cost Allocation shared percentages

Reason:

Asset Reality tests whether each productive asset is economically viable at its actual entered usage.

Example:

If a digger is only used 10–15 hours per week, the recovery test must be based on 10–15 hours per week.

Using labour hours would falsely lower the asset recovery rate and hide underutilisation.

---

# 23. SHARED ASSET RULE

Shared assets do not exist in the Assets module.

An asset may later be shared across operating groups in Cost Allocation.

That is not an Assets module concept.

Assets records:

- what the asset is
- whether it is productive
- what it costs
- how many hours per week it is used by the business

Cost Allocation later records:

- which groups use the asset
- shared percentages
- assigned asset burden

Therefore:

Assets must not create shared asset percentages.

Asset Reality must not consume shared asset percentages.

---

# 24. ZERO UTILISATION GUARDRAIL

If an active productive asset has annual cost but zero utilisation hours:

required_asset_recovery_rate = 0
asset_recovery_rate_status = invalid_zero_utilisation_hours

Warning:

This asset has annual cost but no utilisation hours. Enter utilisation hours before using this asset recovery rate.

This warning should block trusted Asset Reality and Rate Builder use for that asset.

Do not divide by zero.

Do not substitute labour hours.

Do not substitute business productive hours.

Asset utilisation belongs to Assets.

---

# 25. ASSET RECOVERY RATE

Assets must calculate:

required_asset_recovery_rate

Formula:

required_asset_recovery_rate =
total_asset_cost_annual / utilisation_hours_annual

This is the asset’s own required recovery rate.

It is not the group recovery rate.

It is not the final charge-out rate.

It is not the Cost Allocation rate.

Asset Reality may consume this rate as the core asset stress-test value.

Cost Allocation may later assign this asset or a share of its burden to operational groups.

Rate Builder may use the asset recovery rate to show required asset rate.

# 29. ASSET SOURCE-POOL GUARDRAIL PURPOSE

Assets must include guardrails where asset-related source pools are allocated across assets.

This applies when costs such as:

- fuel
- insurance
- repairs and maintenance
- registration
- servicing
- tyres
- consumables
- depreciation
- finance / interest
- lease cost
- equipment hire where treated as asset cost
- other user-created asset cost pools

are being assigned from an available source pool to individual assets.

The system must prevent over-allocation.

The system must also preserve the remaining balance as overhead unless explicitly assigned elsewhere.

---

# 30. SOURCE-POOL GUARDRAIL FORMULA

For each asset cost pool:

assigned_pool_amount =
sum of all asset allocations for that cost type

remaining_pool_amount =
available_pool_amount - assigned_pool_amount

over_allocated_amount =
max(0, assigned_pool_amount - available_pool_amount)

Required variables:

available_pool_amount
assigned_pool_amount
remaining_pool_amount
over_allocated_amount
allocation_status

Do not silently cap.

Do not auto-distribute.

Do not auto-balance.

Do not force 100% assignment.

---

# 32. ASSET POOL STATUS RULES

balanced:
assigned_pool_amount equals available_pool_amount within rounding tolerance.

under_allocated:
assigned_pool_amount is less than available_pool_amount.

over_allocated:
assigned_pool_amount is greater than available_pool_amount.

no_source_pool:
available_pool_amount is zero or missing but user has assigned costs.

review_required:
source data is incomplete, uncertain, or mapping is unclear.

Rounding tolerance:

absolute difference <= 1.00

Anything above tolerance is a real variance.

Under-allocated is not a failure.

It means the remaining amount stays in General Overheads unless another source brief explicitly says otherwise.

# 37. ASSETS READINESS CONDITIONS

Assets is ready when:

- asset rows exist or user confirms no assets
- active assets have valid cost values
- productive assets have utilisation hours
- required_asset_recovery_rate can be calculated or invalid state is clearly flagged
- total_asset_cost_annual can be calculated
- asset source pools are not over-allocated
- transferred running costs do not exceed source pools
- transferred running costs are not double counted in General Overheads
- no blocking warning exists

Assets is not ready when:

- no asset data exists and assets are expected
- active assets have invalid cost
- productive assets have cost but zero utilisation
- source-pool over-allocation exists
- transferred asset running costs exceed available pool
- total_asset_cost_annual cannot be calculated
- saved state is invalid

- total_asset_running_cost_transferred
- assets_status
- assets_model_trust_state

Module Reconciliation compares Assets output to relevant P&L benchmark values.

Module Reconciliation does not change Assets.

# 43. OUTPUT TO COST SUMMARY

Cost Summary should receive:

- total_asset_cost_annual
- total_asset_running_cost_transferred
- assets_status
- assets_model_trust_state

Cost Summary uses total_asset_cost_annual as the asset component of total_cost_burden.

If asset running cost transfers exist, Cost Summary must avoid double counting by using the net General Overheads value after transferred running costs are removed or clearly marked as transferred.

Cost Summary must not recalculate Assets.

---

# 44. OUTPUT TO GENERAL OVERHEADS

General Overheads should receive or expose alongside Assets:

- total_asset_running_cost_transferred
- remaining_overhead_after_asset_transfers
- asset_running_cost_transfers[]

This allows General Overheads / Cost Summary / Cost Allocation to avoid double counting transferred asset running cost pools.

General Overheads remains the source owner of the original overhead pool.

Assets owns the transferred productive asset portion.

# 46. OUTPUT TO COST ALLOCATION

Cost Allocation should receive:

- productive_asset_rows
- total_productive_asset_burden
- productive_asset_count
- asset_pool_guardrail_status
- assets_model_trust_state

Cost Allocation uses these to create:

- Productive Asset Pool
- asset assignment options
- shared asset allocation
- asset pull-out calculations
- asset reconciliation

Cost Allocation must not create asset source cost.

Cost Allocation must not assign more than 100% of an asset.

Cost Allocation must respect asset_pool_guardrail_status.

If asset_pool_guardrail_status = blocked:

Cost Allocation must also show blocked or review state.

Recovery Summary tests whether assets are recovered.

This panel should show:

- utilisation hours per week
- annual utilisation hours

- warning if zero

Annual utilisation is calculated as weekly utilisation hours × 48 weeks.

This utilisation feeds Asset Reality.

This panel should show:

- asset annual cost
- annual utilisation hours
- required asset recovery rate
- rate status

Formula display:

asset annual cost / annual utilisation hours = required asset recovery rate

This panel does not assign the asset to a labour group.

Assignment belongs in Cost Allocation.

This rate feeds Asset Reality.

# 53. DO NOT CHANGE

Do not change:

- Labour source calculations
- General Overheads source calculations except to support transfer visibility where required
- Revenue / COGS logic
- Cost Summary aggregation except to consume Assets contract and avoid double counting
- Cost Allocation assignment logic from inside Assets
- Recovery Summary tests
- Rate Builder rate calculations
- Business Outcome verdict logic

Do not pair assets with operators in Assets.

Do not assign assets to operational groups in Assets.

Do not create shared asset percentages in Assets.

Do not distribute shared asset percentages in Assets.

Do not include labour cost in Assets.

Do not use labour hours for asset recovery rate.

Do not include COGS in Assets.

Do not include principal repayments in operating asset cost.

Do not silently duplicate costs between Assets and General Overheads.

Do not force all running cost pools into Assets.

Do not force non-productive assets into productive recovery.

# 57. FINAL PRINCIPLE

Assets is the asset cost, productive asset, utilisation, and productive asset running cost transfer source.

It creates the true asset recovery requirement.

It does not assign assets to work groups.

It does not create shared asset allocations.

It does not decide recovery strategy.

It does not build rates.

It does not force all overhead pools into Assets.

It transfers only the portion of running cost pools that genuinely belongs to productive assets.

Anything not consumed by productive assets remains overhead.

It gives Asset Reality the asset utilisation truth needed to test whether assets can justify themselves.

No invented utilisation.
No labour-hour asset denominator.
No zero-hour division.
No duplicated overhead cost.
No forced running cost assignment.
No non-productive asset leakage into productive recovery.
No over-allocated source pools.
No hidden asset burden.
No leaks.
