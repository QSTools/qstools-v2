PRIMARY RULE:
Asset Reality tests whether productive assets can economically justify themselves at their entered utilisation. It uses asset-level utilisation from the Assets module, not labour hours and not Cost Allocation shared percentages.

---

# 1. PURPOSE

Asset Reality is the second stress-test layer.

It answers:

Are productive assets recovering themselves?
What does each productive asset need to earn per hour to justify its cost?
Are assets underutilised?
Is asset cost pressure being hidden inside the business?
Which productive assets may be creating recovery pressure before Cost Allocation begins?

Asset Reality is a diagnostic section.

It is not a standalone source module.

It is not a new top-level page unless future navigation changes require it.

It should sit on the Revenue Reality page as a separate section or tab.

---

# 2. PAGE LOCATION

Asset Reality should be added to the existing Revenue Reality page.

Recommended page structure:

Revenue Reality Page

Section 1:
Material → Labour Stress Test

Section 2:
Asset Reality / Asset Recovery Stress Test

Do not create a separate top-level page unless a future brief changes this.

The user should be able to see:

1. Does margin survive Labour?
2. Can productive assets justify themselves at entered utilisation?

These are related diagnostics but must remain separate calculations.

---

# 3. CORE PRINCIPLE

Asset Reality treats each productive asset as a standalone economic unit.

It asks:

Based on this asset’s cost and actual entered utilisation, what hourly recovery rate does it need?

This is not a shared asset allocation test.

This is not a labour/operator pairing test.

This is not Cost Allocation.

This is not Rate Builder.

It is a pressure signal.

---

# 4. SECOND FAILURE CHECKPOINT

Revenue Reality is the first failure checkpoint:

Does margin survive Labour?

Asset Reality is the second failure checkpoint:

Can productive assets justify themselves at entered utilisation?

If Revenue Reality fails:

The business may already be under pressure before assets are considered.

If Asset Reality fails:

The business may have an asset utilisation / asset ownership / asset recovery problem.

If both pass but final result fails:

The issue may sit in overheads, allocation, pricing structure, or final recovery model.

Asset Reality must preserve this diagnostic separation.

---

# 5. SHARED ASSET RULE

Shared assets do not exist in the Assets module.

Shared usage is created later in Cost Allocation.

Therefore Asset Reality must not use shared asset percentages.

Asset Reality must not ask:

Who uses the asset?

Asset Reality asks:

How much is the asset used in total, and does that usage justify the cost?

Assets records:

- what the asset is
- whether it is productive
- what it costs
- how many hours per week it is used by the business

Cost Allocation later records:

- which groups use the asset
- shared percentages
- assigned asset burden

Do not mix these concepts.

---

# 6. COST ALLOCATION DEPENDENCY RULE

Cost Allocation is not required for Asset Reality.

Asset Reality depends on:

- Assets module
- Business Setup mode context
- Model Readiness trust state

Asset Reality does not depend on:

- Cost Allocation groups
- group assignments
- shared asset percentages
- operator/machine pairing
- overhead distribution
- Recovery Summary
- Rate Builder

Cost Allocation comes later.

Asset Reality answers:

Is the asset economically viable at entered utilisation?

Cost Allocation answers:

How is that asset assigned across operational groups?

# 13. PRIMARY ASSET TEST

For each productive asset:

asset_utilisation_hours_annual =
utilisation_hours_per_week × 48

required_asset_recovery_rate =
total_asset_cost_annual / asset_utilisation_hours_annual

If utilisation_hours_annual = 0:

required_asset_recovery_rate = 0
asset_reality_status = blocked or review_required depending source state

Do not divide by zero.

Do not replace asset utilisation with labour hours.

Do not replace asset utilisation with business productive hours.

---

# 14. WHY ASSET UTILISATION IS USED

Asset Reality is testing asset viability.

Therefore the denominator must be how much the asset is actually used.

Example:

A digger is used 10–15 hours per week.

Using total labour hours would make the asset look cheaper than it really is.

Correct:

Digger annual cost
÷ Digger annual utilisation hours
= Digger required recovery rate

This exposes underutilisation.

---

# 15. PRODUCTIVE VS NON-PRODUCTIVE ASSETS

Only productive assets enter the Asset Reality recovery test.

Non-productive assets remain visible but do not enter productive asset recovery.

Examples of non-productive assets:

- personal car
- commute-only ute
- admin vehicle
- office equipment
- non-production equipment

Non-productive asset costs may remain visible as business support cost, but they do not enter productive asset recovery unless reclassified in Assets.

# 19. TOTAL PRODUCTIVE ASSET UTILISATION HOURS

Asset Reality calculates:

total_productive_asset_utilisation_hours =
sum utilisation_hours_annual for active productive assets

If no active productive assets exist:

total_productive_asset_utilisation_hours = 0
asset_reality_status = review_required or not_ready depending setup

If productive assets exist but utilisation hours total zero:

asset_reality_status = blocked

---

# 20. WEIGHTED ASSET RECOVERY RATE

Asset Reality may calculate a weighted required asset recovery rate for the productive asset pool.

Formula:

weighted_required_asset_recovery_rate =
total_productive_asset_burden / total_productive_asset_utilisation_hours

Where:

total_productive_asset_utilisation_hours =
sum utilisation_hours_annual for active productive assets

If total_productive_asset_utilisation_hours = 0:

weighted_required_asset_recovery_rate = 0
status = blocked or review_required

This is a pool-level diagnostic only.

It is not a final charge-out rate.

It is not a Cost Allocation rate.

It is not a Rate Builder output.

# 25. ASSET REALITY STATUS VALUES

Allowed asset_reality_status values:

ready
ready_with_warnings
review_required
blocked
preview_only
asset_pressure_detected
not_ready

Status rules:

ready:
Assets source is trusted and productive assets have valid cost/utilisation.

ready_with_warnings:
Calculations are usable but warnings remain visible.

asset_pressure_detected:
One or more productive assets may require high recovery due to cost or low utilisation.

review_required:
Values calculate but user review is required.

blocked:
Assets source is blocked, utilisation is invalid, or asset pool guardrail is blocked.

preview_only:
Values display but are not trusted final output.

not_ready:
Required asset data is missing.

Asset pressure is not a data failure.

# 27. READINESS CONDITIONS

Asset Reality is ready when:

- Assets module exists
- productive assets exist or user confirms no productive assets
- active productive assets have valid annual cost
- active productive assets have valid utilisation hours
- asset_pool_guardrail_status is not blocked
- Model Readiness is not blocked
- no blocking Asset Reality warning exists

Asset Reality may be ready_with_warnings when:

- asset pressure is detected
- low utilisation is detected
- high required recovery rate is detected
- non-productive assets are excluded
- upstream source data carries warning state

Asset Reality is blocked when:

- Assets source is missing
- Assets module is blocked
- asset_pool_guardrail_status is blocked
- productive asset has cost but zero utilisation
- productive asset cost is invalid
- total_productive_asset_utilisation_hours is zero and productive assets exist
- Model Readiness blocks asset-dependent output

# 29. WARNING MEANINGS

## asset_reality_zero_utilisation

Use when a productive asset has annual cost but zero utilisation.

Blocking:

true.

Meaning:

Required asset recovery rate cannot be calculated because asset utilisation is zero.

## asset_reality_low_utilisation

Use when an asset has low entered utilisation compared with expected use.

Blocking:

false unless threshold rules are later defined.

Meaning:

This asset may need a high recovery rate because it is not used enough.

## asset_reality_high_required_recovery_rate

Use when required asset recovery rate is high.

Blocking:

false by default.

Meaning:

This asset may create pricing pressure.

## asset_reality_asset_pool_blocked

Use when asset_pool_guardrail_status = blocked.

Blocking:

true.

## asset_reality_non_productive_assets_excluded

Use when non-productive assets exist and are excluded from productive asset recovery.

Blocking:

false.
