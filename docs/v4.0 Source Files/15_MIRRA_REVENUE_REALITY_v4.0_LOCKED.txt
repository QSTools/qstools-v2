PRIMARY RULE:
Revenue Reality tests one specific failure point: whether margin after COGS can survive Labour. It must not include Assets or Overheads in the main stress-test box.

---

# 1. PURPOSE

Revenue Reality is the first commercial stress-test layer.

It answers:

Does the margin created after COGS actually survive Labour?

This is the first failure checkpoint in the business model.

It exposes whether the business is already under pressure before Assets, Overheads, Cost Allocation, Recovery Summary, Rate Builder, or Business Outcome are considered.

Revenue Reality does not show the full business result.

Business Summary shows the full current business chain.

Revenue Reality isolates the first point where the model can fail.

---

# 2. CORE TEST

Revenue Reality tests:

Revenue
- COGS
= Margin Pool

Margin Pool
- Labour
= Margin After Labour

This is the same early chain used in Business Summary, but Revenue Reality isolates the Labour pressure point.

Business Summary explains the full chain.

Revenue Reality tests the first breakpoint.

---

# 3. WHAT THIS PAGE MUST NOT DO

Revenue Reality must not include Assets or Overheads in the primary stress-test box.

It must not calculate:

- Asset recovery
- Asset utilisation pressure
- Overhead distribution
- Cost Allocation groups
- Recovery Summary tests
- Rate Builder rates
- Business Outcome verdict

Assets are tested separately by Asset Reality.

Overheads are handled later through Cost Allocation, Recovery Summary, Rate Builder, and Business Outcome.

This page isolates:

Materials / COGS → Labour

---

# 4. WHY ASSETS ARE NOT INCLUDED HERE

Assets are a separate recovery driver.

If Assets are included in the same box as Labour, the diagnostic becomes muddy.

The system must be able to answer separately:

- Is Labour breaking the model?
- Are Assets breaking the model?
- Are Overheads breaking the model?
- Is allocation / recovery strategy the issue?

Therefore Revenue Reality tests Labour only.

Asset Reality tests Assets separately.

# 7. CORE PRINCIPLE

Revenue Reality tests margin pressure.

It does not decide recovery strategy.

It answers:

Is there enough margin after COGS to carry Labour?

If not, the business is under pressure before Assets and Overheads are even considered.

This is not a data failure.

It is a commercial reality signal.

This box is the main diagnostic on the page.

# 15. PRIMARY STRESS TEST FORMULA

Revenue Reality primary formula:

margin_after_labour =
margin_pool - labour_cost_for_stress_test

Where:

labour_cost_for_stress_test =
total_people_cost_annual

or compatibility equivalent:

total_labour_cost_annual

Preferred:

total_people_cost_annual

because it matches Cost Summary people-cost burden.

---

# 16. LABOUR CONSUMPTION OF MARGIN

Revenue Reality calculates:

labour_consumption_of_margin_percent =
labour_cost_for_stress_test / margin_pool

If margin_pool = 0:

labour_consumption_of_margin_percent = 0
status = review_required or blocked depending source state

Do not divide by zero.

Interpretation:

below 100%:
Labour is covered by margin and leaves margin after Labour.

equal to 100%:
Labour consumes the entire margin pool.

above 100%:
Labour exceeds the available margin pool.

---

# 17. MARGIN AFTER LABOUR PERCENT OF REVENUE

Revenue Reality calculates:

margin_after_labour_percent_of_revenue =
margin_after_labour / total_revenue

If total_revenue = 0:

margin_after_labour_percent_of_revenue = 0

Do not divide by zero.

This shows how much of revenue remains after COGS and Labour.

---

# 18. SURPLUS / SHORTFALL FORMULAS

Revenue Reality may expose display helpers:

margin_surplus_after_labour =
max(0, margin_pool - labour_cost_for_stress_test)

labour_shortfall_against_margin =
max(0, labour_cost_for_stress_test - margin_pool)

These must reconcile to:

margin_after_labour =
margin_pool - labour_cost_for_stress_test

If the reconciliation does not hold, show review_required or blocked depending severity.

---

# 19. LABOUR MARGIN GAP

Revenue Reality calculates:

labour_margin_gap =
margin_pool - labour_cost_for_stress_test

This is the same value as margin_after_labour.

It may be retained as a compatibility/display alias.

Preferred active variable:

margin_after_labour

Compatibility alias:

labour_margin_gap

---

# 20. PER-DRIVER FORMULAS

Revenue Reality calculates mode-aware per-driver values.

If activity_driver_value > 0:

current_margin_per_driver =
margin_pool / activity_driver_value

labour_cost_per_driver =
labour_cost_for_stress_test / activity_driver_value

margin_after_labour_per_driver =
margin_after_labour / activity_driver_value

labour_margin_gap_per_driver =
margin_after_labour_per_driver

If activity_driver_value = 0:

all per-driver values = 0
status = review_required or blocked depending source state

Do not divide by zero.

Do not invent driver values.

---

# 21. MODE-AWARE DRIVER RULE

If business_type = labour_based:

activity_driver_type = hours
activity_driver_label = productive hours
activity_driver_value = total_productive_output or total_productive_labour_hours

Revenue Reality shows:

- margin per productive hour
- labour cost per productive hour
- margin after Labour per productive hour

If business_type = product_based:

activity_driver_type = units
activity_driver_label = annual units
activity_driver_value = total_derived_units_annual

Revenue Reality shows:

- margin per unit
- labour cost per unit
- margin after Labour per unit

Do not use productive hours as the primary driver in product/unit mode.

Do not use units as the primary driver in labour-based mode.

---

# 22. HOURS-BASED INTERPRETATION

In hours-based mode, Revenue Reality should explain:

- how much material margin the business creates
- how much Labour consumes
- whether enough remains after Labour
- margin after Labour per productive hour
- labour cost per productive hour
- current margin per productive hour

Core interpretation:

COGS creates material margin.

Labour must be covered by that margin or recovered separately through the operating model.

If Labour consumes the margin pool, the business is under pressure before Assets and Overheads.

Revenue Reality must not claim that materials or COGS build the hourly rate.

---

# 23. PRODUCT / UNIT-BASED INTERPRETATION

In product/unit mode, Revenue Reality should explain:

- how much unit margin exists after COGS
- how much labour burden is consumed per unit
- margin after Labour per unit
- whether the unit margin can carry Labour before Assets and Overheads

Core interpretation:

COGS is consumed by the product.

Margin after COGS must carry Labour, Assets, Overheads, and Profit.

Revenue Reality tests the Labour part of that burden first.

Revenue Reality must not build the full unit cost stack.

Rate Builder owns fully burdened unit rate.

---

Downstream modules should consume the Revenue Reality diagnostic output rather than recalculating the stress-test separately.

# 25. RELATIONSHIP TO ASSET REALITY

Revenue Reality and Asset Reality sit on the same page or diagnostic layer but test different drivers.

Revenue Reality tests:

Does margin survive Labour?

Asset Reality tests:

Can productive assets justify themselves at entered utilisation?

Revenue Reality must not include Asset Reality calculations in the main Labour stress-test box.

Asset Reality must not change Revenue Reality calculations.

Together they form the first two diagnostic layers before Cost Allocation.

# 26. STATUS VALUES

Allowed revenue_reality_status values:

ready
ready_with_warnings
review_required
blocked
preview_only
at_risk
negative_margin_after_labour
not_ready

Status rules:

ready:
Inputs are trusted and margin after Labour is positive.

ready_with_warnings:
Inputs are trusted but warnings remain visible.

review_required:
Values calculate but review is required.

blocked:
Required source values are missing/invalid or Model Readiness blocks trusted output.

preview_only:
Values display but are not trusted final output.

at_risk:
Margin after Labour is low or under pressure but not negative.

negative_margin_after_labour:
Labour exceeds margin pool but source data is trusted.

not_ready:
Required inputs are missing.

Negative margin after Labour is not a data readiness failure.

revenue_reality_model_trust_state = preview_only

If model_trust_state = warning:
revenue_reality_model_trust_state = warning

If model_trust_state = ready and calculations are valid:
revenue_reality_model_trust_state = ready

# 28. READINESS CONDITIONS

Revenue Reality is ready when:

- Revenue / COGS totals exist
- margin_pool is numeric
- labour cost for stress test exists
- activity driver exists where per-driver output is shown
- Model Readiness is not blocked
- no blocking Revenue Reality warning exists

Revenue Reality may be ready_with_warnings when:

- margin after Labour is negative
- Labour consumes most of margin pool
- COG detail is preview-only but high-level P&L totals exist
- upstream model has warning state
- activity driver is available but needs review

Revenue Reality is blocked when:

- Revenue / COGS is missing
- margin_pool cannot be calculated
- Labour cost is missing
- selected activity driver is required but missing
- Model Readiness is blocked
- COG reconciliation is blocked for COG-dependent output

# 30. WARNING MEANINGS

## revenue_reality_labour_exceeds_margin

Use when:

labour_cost_for_stress_test > margin_pool

Blocking:

false if source data is trusted.

Meaning:

Labour exceeds the available margin pool. This is a commercial pressure result, not a data failure.

## revenue_reality_low_margin_after_labour

Use when margin after Labour is positive but low.

Blocking:

false.

Meaning:

The business has little margin left after Labour before Assets and Overheads.

## revenue_reality_cog_reconciliation_blocked

Use when COG reconciliation is blocked or leak_detected and Revenue Reality depends on COG totals.

Blocking:

true for trusted output.

## revenue_reality_zero_activity_driver

Use when per-driver output is required but activity driver is zero.

Blocking:

true for per-driver values.

## revenue_reality_margin_pool_negative

Use when margin_pool is negative.

Blocking:

false if source data is trusted.

Meaning:

COGS exceeds revenue before Labour is considered.
