# Mirra v4.0 — Handover Brief

## Core Concept Locked, Evidence System Direction, Brief Alignment Audit, and P&L Rebuild Start Point

---

# 1. Current Context

This chat moved Mirra from a module/page build into a clearer product architecture.

The core product idea has now been defined as:

Mirra is a closed-loop business recovery system.

It uses monthly accounting truth to build and test the business model, uses that model to create rate targets and quote rules, checks jobs against the same model, compares actual monthly performance back to the model, and creates feedback for controlled model improvement.

The key new document created was:

MIRRA_CORE_CLOSED_LOOP_RECOVERY_MODEL_v1.0_LOCKED.txt

This should now be treated as a core product architecture document, not a normal module brief.

---

# 2. Core Product Definition

The central Mirra loop is:

Monthly P&L
↓
Business recovery model
↓
Required labour / asset / overhead / COG recovery
↓
Rate targets
↓
Quote checker
↓
Work orders / purchase orders / time / invoicing
↓
Job performance
↓
Monthly P&L comparison
↓
Model feedback
↓
Approved model improvement
↓
Future quote targets

This loop is the product.

---

# 3. Product Positioning Locked

Mirra should not be positioned as only:

- accounting software
- estimating software
- job costing software
- cost allocation software
- FP&A software
- project management software
- dashboard reporting
- ERP software

The better positioning is:

A closed-loop business recovery system for small operators.

Alternative phrasing:

An operator-facing recovery and quote validation engine.

A business recovery and rate validation system for trade, construction, and service businesses.

A system that turns accounting data into required rates, quote targets, job checks, monthly feedback, and controlled model improvement.

---

# 4. Strategic Difference

Other systems may connect:

- quotes
- jobs
- purchase orders
- invoices
- time
- budgets
- reports

But Mirra’s strategic difference is the recovery loop.

Mirra asks:

Did this job prove the business recovery model?

And:

Did the monthly P&L prove that the recovery model is improving or failing?

The difference is the connection between job-level performance and whole-business recovery logic.

---

# 5. Important Product Insight

The most important product statement from this chat is:

Mirra measures every job against the business recovery model, then measures the whole business through the monthly P&L to prove whether the model is improving or failing.

This creates two connected lenses:

Job lens:
Did this job recover the model?

Business lens:
Did the whole business recover as expected this month?

Both use the same recovery logic.

# 12. Brief Alignment Conflicts to Watch For

The audit should flag older or conflicting ideas such as:

- Cost Allocation owns recovery model
- Cost Allocation calculates recovery
- Recovery Summary asks user to choose recovery method
- Business Outcome feeds Quote Checker directly
- Employee Overheads as a separate active module
- Cost Summary consumes Cost Allocation
- Cost Summary consumes Recovery Summary
- Quote Checker uses unselected scenarios

Required v4.0 concepts:

- Business Modelling sits between Business Outcome and Quote Checker
- Cost Allocation is structure only
- Recovery Summary is automatic / mode-dependent
- Rate Builder owns charge-out rate construction
- Business Outcome diagnoses only
- Business Modelling selects the target model
- Quote Checker enforces selected target model

# 16. P&L Rebuild Start Point

The P&L rebuild should not start by editing downstream modules.

Start with:

Business Setup / P&L source integrity.

Check:

- business mode availability
- P&L import totals
- revenue grouping
- COG grouping
- operating expense grouping
- other/unallocated reassignment
- custom categories
- source trace availability
- warnings/trust state
- downstream output contract

Known issue:

In P&L “other/unallocated”, sometimes when an item is reassigned it moves correctly, and other times it remains visible in the old category.

That must be fixed.

Reassignment must move the item, not duplicate it.

No account/category row may exist in both unallocated and assigned category after reassignment.
