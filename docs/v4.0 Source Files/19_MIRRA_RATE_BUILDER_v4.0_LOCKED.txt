PRIMARY RULE:
Rate Builder is the first layer where upstream pressure is converted into actual charge-out rates. It builds sellable rates from the allocated structure, but it does not create source costs, assign costs, test recovery again, or issue the final business outcome.

---

# 1. PURPOSE

Rate Builder answers:

What do I need to charge?

It takes the pressure identified upstream and converts it into real-world rates.

It should show:

- labour rate
- asset / machine rate
- machine + operator rate
- full crew rate
- unit/product rate where valid
- minimum recoverable rate
- margin-added sell rate
- current rate gap
- under-recovery warning

Rate Builder is where the business finally sees:

- what each component costs
- what overhead burden sits on it
- what minimum recovery is required
- what sell rate is needed

# 3. CORE PRINCIPLE

Rate Builder is mode-guided, not mode-restricted.

Business mode comes from Business Setup.

Rate Builder must not ask the user to choose the business mode again.

However, real businesses can sell both hours and products.

Therefore:

- business mode sets the default behaviour
- individual rates may still have their own rate driver

Example:

Labour-based business:
Default = hourly rates
Allowed exceptions = product/unit rates if manually or structurally valid

Product-based business:
Default = unit/product rates
Allowed exceptions = dayworks / hourly rates

---

# 4. MODE RULE

Business Setup owns the mode.

Rate Builder consumes:

- business_type
- is_labour_based
- is_product_based

If labour-based:

Default Rate Builder behaviour is hourly / labour / asset / crew based.

If product-based:

Default Rate Builder behaviour is unit / m² / m³ / product based.

If mixed rates exist:

Rate Builder may support mixed rate rows.

But Rate Builder must not change the business mode.

---

# 5. IMPORTANT MODE DISTINCTION

The system mode describes how the business primarily operates.

The rate driver describes what is being sold.

Therefore:

System mode = business default  
Rate driver = individual sellable rate

Examples:

A labour-based business may sell:
- labour per hour
- digger + operator per hour
- full crew per day
- occasional product/unit items only if manually defined

A product-based business may sell:
- block laying per m²
- concrete pumping per m³
- coffee per unit
- dayworks per hour as an exception

---

# 6. RATE BUILDER DOES NOT REPLACE SOURCE MODULES

Rate Builder must not create:

- labour cost
- asset cost
- overhead cost
- COG cost
- source revenue
- source margin
- productive hours
- asset utilisation
- Cost Allocation groups

It consumes these from upstream modules.

Rate Builder must preserve traceability.

---

# 7. WHAT RATE BUILDER UNRAVELS

Rate Builder is the first page that unpacks upstream pressure into chargeable rates.

Revenue Reality says:

Margin after Labour is tight or negative.

Asset Reality says:

Assets require a recovery rate.

Recovery Summary says:

This structure needs to recover a certain amount.

Rate Builder converts that into:

- labour needs to be $X/hr
- digger + operator needs to be $Y/hr
- crew needs to be $Z/hr
- product needs to be $A/unit where valid

This is the first practical pricing layer.

In product-based mode, COG detail may support top-down unit rate analysis if the product/unit is the primary business driver.

---

# 13. COG DETAIL RULE BY MODE

## Labour-Based Business

Revenue / COG detail is used for:

- margin visibility
- material markup context
- supplier spend concentration
- supplier negotiation insight
- future SOQ/job layer preparation

It is not used to automatically build product/unit rates.

Reason:

Labour-based businesses do not have enough quantity linkage to derive true product cost from supplier totals.

## Product-Based Business

Revenue / COG detail may be used for:

- unit margin
- unit COG
- top-down cost per unit
- product/unit rate validation

Only where the unit is the primary business driver.

---

# 14. TOP-DOWN RATE DERIVATION RULE

Top-down rate derivation is valid only when the selected driver is the primary business driver.

Examples:

Product-based block laying business:

Revenue ÷ m² = revenue per m²  
COG ÷ m² = COG per m²  
Margin ÷ m² = margin per m²

This works because m² is the primary driver.

Labour-based business:

Revenue ÷ m² is not valid unless m² is tracked as a real production driver.

Therefore product/unit rates in a labour-based business require:

- manual component definition
- or future SOQ / job / PO data

Do not infer product/unit rates from supplier totals alone.

---

# 15. LABOUR-BASED PRODUCT RATE LIMITATION

For a labour-based business, true product/unit rates cannot be built from annual COG supplier totals alone.

To build a true product/unit rate, the system needs:

- schedule of quantities
- job-level quantities
- material consumption
- labour consumption
- subcontractor consumption
- PO/job cost mapping

This belongs to the future SOQ / Job Tracking / PO layer.

Until then, product/unit rates in labour mode must be:

- manual
- template-based
- or marked not_available / preview_only

Do not create false precision.

---

# 16. PRODUCT-BASED DAYWORKS RULE

Product-based businesses may still sell hours.

Example:

A square-metre installation business may also charge dayworks.

This is valid.

Rate Builder should allow an hourly rate row inside product mode.

But the system mode remains product-based.

Hourly rate is an exception rate, not a new business mode.

---

# 17. SELLABLE RATE VS WORKING GROUP RULE

A Cost Allocation working group is not always a sellable rate.

Example:

Site Works Group contains:

- 2 labourers
- 1 operator
- 1 digger

But the business may sell:

- digger + operator
- labourer only
- full crew
- dayworks
- m³ / m² product rate

Therefore Rate Builder must allow rates to be built from:

- full group
- subset of group
- individual labour component
- asset + operator combination
- unit/product driver where valid

---

# 18. SUBSET RATE RULE

Rate Builder must allow component selection.

Example:

Cost Allocation group:

Site Works:
- 2 labourers
- 1 operator
- 1 digger

Rate Builder rate:

Digger + Operator Rate:
- include operator
- include digger
- include proportional overhead
- exclude 2 labourers

This produces the real charge-out rate for the machine and operator.

# 19. COMPONENT TYPES

Allowed rate component types:

- labour
- asset
- overhead
- COG/material
- subcontractor
- manual adjustment
- margin/profit

Each component must be traceable.

No hidden cost layer.

No duplicated cost layer.

---

# 20. LABOUR COMPONENT

Labour component uses:

- assigned labour cost
- assigned labour hours
- weighted labour rate from Labour
- Cost Allocation assignment

It can produce:

- labourer hourly rate
- operator hourly rate
- crew labour rate

Non-productive labour must not appear here directly.

Non-productive labour is recovered through overheads.

---

# 21. ASSET COMPONENT

Asset component uses:

- assigned asset burden
- assigned asset utilisation hours
- required asset recovery rate
- asset cost from Assets
- assignment from Cost Allocation

It can produce:

- digger hourly recovery
- pump hourly recovery
- truck hourly recovery
- equipment cost component

Asset running costs already transferred into Assets must not be added again through overheads.

---

# 22. OVERHEAD COMPONENT

Overhead component uses:

- assigned overhead from Cost Allocation
- selected overhead allocation basis

Possible allocation basis:

- labour hours
- asset hours
- selected component cost
- group cost share
- equal split
- manual visible override

Overhead must be included in the rate unless explicitly excluded and flagged.

---

# 23. COG / MATERIAL COMPONENT

COG/material component rules depend on mode.

In product mode:

COG/material may be included as unit cost where unit driver is valid.

In labour mode:

COG/material must not be automatically included in a product/unit rate unless manually defined or supplied by future SOQ/job data.

COG detail may still be shown as material markup context.

---

# 24. MATERIAL MARKUP CONTEXT

Rate Builder may show light material markup context.

This should not become the main analysis section.

The main analysis belongs in Business Outcome.

Rate Builder may show:

- blended material markup %
- total COG
- gross margin
- note that detailed supplier markup is not individually known

Important:

There is one blended material markup.

The system cannot assign a true markup per supplier from total COG data alone.

Supplier/category detail shows contribution to the blended result, not separate supplier markups.

---

# 25. SUPPLIER CONTRIBUTION CONTEXT

Supplier/category COG detail may show:

- top supplier spend
- top category spend
- percentage of total COG
- concentration of spend

This is useful context.

But in Rate Builder it should remain light.

Full supplier impact and modelling belongs in Business Outcome.

---

# 26. BUSINESS OUTCOME HANDOFF FOR SUPPLIER MODELLING

Rate Builder should hand off supplier/material sensitivity to Business Outcome.

Business Outcome should model:

- supplier price increase
- supplier price decrease
- total COG movement
- blended material markup change
- margin impact
- recovery impact
- required rate impact

Example:

If concrete supplier cost reduces by 1%, show:

- COG reduction
- margin improvement
- rate pressure reduction

This is Business Outcome modelling, not Rate Builder construction.

# 28. MINIMUM RECOVERABLE RATE

Rate Builder owns minimum recoverable rate.

Formula:

selected total cost ÷ selected driver

Selected total cost may include:

- labour component
- asset component
- overhead component
- COG/material component where valid
- manual visible adjustment

This is cost-only.

It is not the sell rate.

---

# 29. FULLY BURDENED COST RATE

Fully burdened cost rate includes all selected cost layers before profit.

For many use cases this equals minimum recoverable rate.

It must clearly show:

- labour
- asset
- overhead
- COG/material where valid

---

# 30. MARGIN / MARKUP

Rate Builder may apply:

- margin %
- markup %
- fixed profit per driver
- target sell rate

Margin and markup must not be confused.

Margin:

sell rate = cost rate ÷ (1 - margin %)

Markup:

sell rate = cost rate × (1 + markup %)

The active method must be visible.

---

# 31. SUGGESTED SELL RATE

Suggested sell rate is:

fully burdened cost rate
+ selected profit/margin method

Rate Builder should show:

- cost-only rate
- profit amount
- final sell rate

This is the practical number the user can charge.

---

# 32. CURRENT / MARKET / TARGET RATE COMPARISON

- current charge-out rate
- target rate

- imported price list later

# 49. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

Rate Builder v4.0 is accepted when:

1. It consumes Business Setup mode.
2. It is mode-guided, not mode-restricted.
3. It does not allow business mode to be redefined.
4. It supports labour-based default hourly rates.
5. It supports product-based default unit rates.
6. It supports exception rates.
7. It supports full-group rates.
8. It supports subset rates.
9. It can build digger + operator separately from a larger group.
10. It consumes Cost Allocation structure.
11. It consumes Recovery Summary requirements.
12. It consumes Revenue Reality context.
13. It consumes Asset Reality context.
14. It calculates minimum recoverable rate.
15. It calculates fully burdened cost rate.
16. It applies margin or markup.
17. It calculates suggested sell rate.
18. It compares current / target / market rates where supplied.
19. It does not invent market rates.
20. It does not derive labour-based product rates from COG totals alone.
21. It requires SOQ/manual component data for labour-mode product rates.
22. It supports top-down unit analysis only where unit is primary business driver.
23. It shows blended material markup context.
24. It does not calculate supplier-level markups as if they are true individual markups.
25. It hands supplier/material modelling to Business Outcome.
26. It produces Quote Checker handoff values.
27. It does not issue final Business Outcome verdict.
28. Build passes.

# 50. FINAL PRINCIPLE

Rate Builder is where pressure becomes pricing.

It turns:

structure
+ recovery requirement
+ component costs
+ margin target

into:

minimum recoverable rates
and
sellable charge-out rates

It is mode-guided.

It supports real-world exceptions.

It does not fake product rates from insufficient data.

It keeps product/unit pricing honest.

It gives Business Outcome the rates and levers needed to model fixes.

It gives Quote Checker the rates needed to enforce alignment.

No source mutation.
No duplicated costs.
No fake unit rates.
No invented market rates.
No hidden margin.
No final verdict.
No leaks.
