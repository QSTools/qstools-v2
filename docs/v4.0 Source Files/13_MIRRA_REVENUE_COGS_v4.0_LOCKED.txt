# 1. PURPOSE

Revenue / COGS defines the commercial selling layer of the business.

It answers:

What does the business sell?
What revenue did the business generate?
What direct COGS / Cost of Sales were consumed?
What margin pool was created?
Is the business hours-based or product/unit-based?
If product/unit-based, what commercial units does it sell?
Does detailed COG activity reconcile back to the P&L Cost of Sales?
Which suppliers make up COG spend?
Which suppliers or source names need cleanup?
Which COG categories and optional subcategories should later feed product/unit rates or margin visibility?

Revenue / COGS is not the final pricing module.

It prepares reconciled commercial and COG source data for downstream recovery and rate logic.

# 4. CORE PRINCIPLE

Revenue / COGS must preserve the accounting source and add operational interpretation without hiding the original source.

P&L owns raw accounting lines.

Revenue / COGS owns operational COG assignment, supplier summary, supplier cleanup, optional COG subcategory structure, and COG detail reconciliation.

The P&L says what the accounting Cost of Sales total is.

Revenue / COGS proves whether the imported COG detail ties back to that total.

If it does not tie back, downstream use must be blocked or preview-only.

# 5. MODE-DEPENDENT COGS RULE

COGS treatment depends on business mode.

## Hours-based / service-based mode

COGS creates material margin.

Revenue - COGS = Material Margin

In this mode:

- COGS is primarily observational.
- COGS is used for margin visibility, leak detection, and high-level cost-driver review.
- COGS is not automatically part of hourly recovery burden.
- Labour should recover labour.
- Assets should recover assets.
- Materials should cover themselves and create margin.
- Material margin must be visible.
- COG margin must not hide labour or asset under-recovery.

Detailed supplier invoice line-level COG structure is not required to build labour-based rates.

Reason:

Xero and similar accounting systems usually hold the supplier invoice as a high-level transaction. Detailed line items are often inside attached PDFs, not structured transaction data.

Detailed costing for labour-based quotes should come from a structured quoting / estimating system later, not from accounting exports.

## Product / unit-based mode

COGS is consumed by the product/unit.

Revenue - COGS = Margin Pool

That margin pool must recover:

- labour
- assets
- overheads
- profit

In this mode:

- COGS is operational.
- COGS can become part of product/unit cost stack logic.
- Unit margin must be tested against cost burden.
- Rate Builder must include COG per unit in the unit cost stack where user has provided enough mapping.
- COG categories and optional subcategories become powerful inputs for product/unit pricing.

Example:

Coffee price may need:

- coffee beans
- milk
- cups / packaging
- direct labour burden
- overhead burden
- margin

COG category/subcategory structure supports that later.

# 6. LABOUR MODE VS PRODUCT MODE COG DETAIL RULE

The same Revenue / COGS structure must support both simple and detailed use.

## Labour mode

COG is a margin and review layer.

Use COG for:

- margin pool
- material margin
- supplier spend visibility
- supplier/category cleanup
- leak detection
- rough cost-driver analysis

Do not require COG-to-task or COG-to-product mapping.

Do not attempt to build labour-based rates from Xero COG transaction detail.

## Product/unit mode

COG may become part of the product costing layer.

Use COG for:

- unit COG cost
- product/unit input grouping
- margin per unit
- cost stack preparation
- Rate Builder input where mapped

Do not infer product consumption without user input.

# 19. COG CONTROL IMPORT SOURCE RULE

The correct COG control source is:

Account Transactions
or
General Ledger-style transaction report

The report must show account-level transaction movements that reconcile to P&L Cost of Sales.

Do not use Payable Invoice Detail as the COG control source.

Payable Invoice Detail creates timing issues because invoice due dates, payment timing, and creditor terms may not match P&L recognition timing.

Account Transactions aligns better because it reflects the accounting movement in the COG accounts.

# 21. DEBIT / CREDIT CALCULATION RULE

COG reconciliation must use:

net_total =
debit_total - credit_total

Debit/Credit must not be dropped as calculation fields.

Reason:

Gross may appear positive, negative, bracketed, or differently formatted depending accounting system, export style, source type, journals, or credit note treatment.

Debit/Credit is the safest accounting basis.

Gross and GST must be stored, but not used for net COG reconciliation.

Credit notes are handled naturally through the Debit - Credit calculation.

Examples:

Payable Invoice:
Debit = 1,000
Credit = 0
Net = 1,000

Payable Credit Note:
Debit = 0
Credit = 300
Net = -300

Net COG movement:
1,000 - 300 = 700

No special credit note adjustment is required beyond correct Debit - Credit handling.

---

# 22. GROSS AND GST RULE

Gross is GST-inclusive.

GST is not operating COG.

Gross and GST are stored for:

- audit
- traceability
- future GST-inclusive reporting
- future checking
- source review

Gross must not be used as the COG net value.

GST must not be included in operating COG.

If the importer detects gross being used as net value, create warning:

cog_gross_used_warning

# 27. SUPPLIER NAME NORMALISATION RULE

Revenue / COGS must include a supplier name normalisation layer for COG transaction imports.

Accounting exports may contain supplier/contact names with extra notes, job descriptions, trade descriptions, payment notes, or correction comments.

Examples:

K & C Building
K & C Building - Blockwork
K & C Building - Cutting
K & C Building - Concrete Laying
K & C Building - Less part payment from other invoice
K & C Building - Paul entered the amount that was agreed

These should be grouped under a clean supplier name where appropriate:

The original imported supplier/contact/description text must always be preserved.

The cleaned supplier name is used for supplier spend summary, category assignment, and downstream reporting.

The raw supplier/contact text is retained for audit and drill-down.

# 34. SUPPLIER SUMMARY CALCULATION

For each clean supplier:

total_net_cost =
sum net_total for all transactions mapped to clean_supplier_name

total_gross_cost =
sum gross_total for all transactions mapped to clean_supplier_name

total_gst =
sum gst_total for all transactions mapped to clean_supplier_name

transaction_count =
count transactions mapped to clean_supplier_name

percent_of_total_cog =
total_net_cost / total_cog_transaction_net_total

Source types:

source_types =
unique list of transaction source values for that supplier

Example:

source_types = Payable Invoice, Payable Credit Note

# 38. SUPPLIER SUMMARY NO-LEAK RULE

Supplier name normalisation and categorisation must not change total COG value.

Before normalisation:

sum transaction net_total = COG transaction total

After normalisation:

sum supplier_summary total_net_cost = COG transaction total

After supplier category assignment:

sum category totals = supplier_summary total_net_cost

These totals must match.

If they do not match:

supplier_summary_status = blocked
warning = supplier_summary_total_mismatch

Normalisation changes grouping only.

Categorisation changes reporting only.

Neither can change accounting value.

# 41. COG RECONCILIATION

Revenue / COGS owns COG reconciliation.

Required variables:

pnl_cog_total =
pnl_total_cogs

account_transactions_net_total - pnl_cog_total

# 42. COG RECONCILIATION STATUS RULES

Allowed cog_reconciliation_status values:

balanced
rounding_only
variance_review
leak_detected
blocked
not_available

Rules:

balanced:
difference = 0.00

rounding_only:
absolute difference <= 1.00

leak_detected:
absolute difference > 1.00 for control-source COG import

blocked:
required source is missing, period mismatch exists, import failed, or reconciliation cannot be calculated

not_available:
no Account Transactions import exists yet

variance_review:
only allowed if a future reviewed variance workflow is explicitly created

Default rule:

If Account Transactions COG import exists and difference is above tolerance:

cog_reconciliation_status = leak_detected

Do not describe material variance as acceptable because the percentage is small.

# 43. PERIOD MATCH RULE

P&L period and Account Transactions COG period must match.

If periods do not match:

cog_reconciliation_status = blocked

Warning:

COG Account Transactions period does not match the P&L period. Re-export the report for the same period before using COG downstream.

Do not silently month-shift.

Do not assume invoice timing correction.

Do not auto-adjust dates.

The user may investigate timing outside the model, but the control import must match the P&L period.

# 46. PAYABLE INVOICE DETAIL RULE

Payable Invoice Detail must not be used as the COG control import.

It can mismatch the P&L due to:

- invoice due dates
- creditor terms
- payment timing
- spend money vs payable invoices
- cash purchases
- deposits
- journals
- WIP adjustments
- timing differences

It may be useful for future supplier spend analysis, but not as the accounting COG control report.

Required warning if detected:

cog_payable_invoice_detail_rejected

Message:

Payable Invoice Detail is not the COG control source. Use Account Transactions for the selected COG accounts and matching P&L period.

# 48. XERO ACCOUNT TRANSACTIONS EXPORT STEPS

Instruction panel text:

1. In Xero, open the Profit and Loss report for the same financial period you are using in Mirra.

2. Click into one of the Cost of Sales totals to open the Account Transactions view.

3. In the Account field, select all Cost of Sales / COG accounts that are included in your P&L Cost of Sales total.

4. Set the report date range to exactly match the P&L period.

5. Add or confirm the required columns:
   - Date
   - Source
   - Description
   - Debit
   - Credit
   - Gross
   - GST

6. Include Contact / supplier if Xero allows it in the exported report. If Contact is not available, Mirra may use Description as the raw supplier/source text where appropriate.

7. Click Update.

8. Use Save As to save the report as a custom report.

9. Name it something clear, such as:
   Account Transactions COG

10. Set the custom report as the default if Xero allows this.

11. Export the report to Excel.

12. Save it in the same folder as your P&L export where possible.

13. Import that Excel file into Revenue / COGS.

14. Check the reconciliation panel.

15. The Account Transactions COG total should reconcile to the P&L Cost of Sales total.

If it does not reconcile, do not use COG downstream until the variance is resolved.

# 59. LABOUR MODE COG DETAIL LIMITATION

Xero commonly stores high-level supplier invoice values, not detailed supplier invoice line items.

Quote Engine / estimating system / structured quote data

Not:

Xero high-level COG transaction export

COG categories/subcategories are useful for visibility, margin analysis, and review, but not required for rate building.

- COG assignment is incomplete where required downstream

# 95. FINAL PRINCIPLE

Revenue / COGS defines the commercial selling layer.

It preserves what the P&L says.

It reconciles COG detail to the accounting COG total.

It lets the user assign operational COG categories and optional subcategories without changing source accounting names.

It creates a supplier spend summary so the user can see where COG money actually goes.

It cleans supplier names for reporting while preserving raw source text.

It supports simple high-level categorisation by default and deeper detail only when useful.

It decides whether the business is dealing with COG as material margin or product/unit consumption based on Business Setup mode.

It does not build the final recovery model.

It does not build rates.

It does not issue the business verdict.

No assumptions.
No Payable Invoice Detail as COG control.
No silent period shifting.
No GST-inclusive gross used as net COG.
No dropped Debit/Credit calculation basis.
No forced line-by-line categorisation.
No overwritten supplier source text.
No hidden COG categories.
No required over-detail.
No unreconciled COG downstream.
No leaks.
