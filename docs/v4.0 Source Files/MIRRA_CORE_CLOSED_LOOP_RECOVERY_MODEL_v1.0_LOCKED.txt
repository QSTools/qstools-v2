# Mirra — Core Closed-Loop Recovery Model Brief

## v1.0 — PRODUCT CONCEPT, BUSINESS MODEL LOOP, MONTHLY P&L FEEDBACK, QUOTE CHECKING, JOB ACTUALS, MODEL FEEDBACK, AND CONTINUOUS BUSINESS IMPROVEMENT

STATUS: CORE PRODUCT ARCHITECTURE BRIEF
VERSION: v1.0
FILE NUMBER: CORE-01

PRODUCT NAME:
Mirra

FORMER NAME:
QS Tools

PRIMARY RULE:
Mirra is a closed-loop business recovery system. It converts accounting data into a recovery model, uses that model to set required rates and quote targets, checks quotes against those targets, measures job and invoicing actuals against the same recovery logic, and feeds monthly performance back into future model improvement.

---

# 1. PURPOSE

This brief defines the core product idea behind Mirra.

It is not a page brief.

It is not an implementation brief.

It is not a module contract brief.

It is the central product architecture statement.

Mirra exists to answer:

Is the business model working?

Are quotes aligned to that model?

Are jobs proving or disproving the model?

Is monthly P&L performance improving?

What needs to change next?

---

# 2. CORE PRODUCT IDEA

Mirra connects four layers that are usually separated:

1. Monthly accounting reality
2. Business recovery model
3. Quote / job execution
4. Performance feedback

The core loop is:

Monthly P&L
↓
Business recovery model
↓
Required labour / asset / overhead / COG recovery
↓
Rate targets
↓
Quote checker
↓
Work orders / purchase orders / time / invoicing
↓
Job performance
↓
Monthly P&L comparison
↓
Model feedback
↓
Approved model improvement
↓
Future quote targets

This loop is the product.

---

# 3. WHAT MAKES MIRRA DIFFERENT

Mirra is not just:

- accounting software
- estimating software
- job costing software
- cost allocation software
- FP&A software
- project management software
- dashboard reporting

Mirra combines parts of these ideas into one operator-facing recovery workflow.

Most tools answer one or more of these:

Did this job make money?

What is the project budget?

What does the P&L say?

What is the forecast?

What are the job actuals?

What is the current margin?

Mirra asks:

Does every job, quote, invoice, labour hour, asset hour, supplier cost, and monthly P&L result align with the business recovery model?

---

# 4. CORE LOOP STATEMENT

Mirra measures every job against the business recovery model, then measures the whole business through the monthly P&L to prove whether the model is improving or failing.

This creates two connected lenses:

Job lens:
Did this job recover the model?

Business lens:
Did the whole business recover as expected this month?

Both lenses use the same recovery logic.

---

# 5. MONTHLY P&L FEEDBACK LOOP

Mirra imports or receives monthly P&L data.

Monthly P&L data is not reporting only.

It is model recalibration evidence.

Each monthly P&L cycle should update or test:

- total revenue
- COGS
- gross margin
- labour cost
- asset cost
- overhead cost
- margin after labour
- asset recovery pressure
- overhead recovery pressure
- blended material markup
- net position
- business model drift

Monthly P&L answers:

Is the business still balanced?

Are actual costs moving away from the model?

Are margins improving or deteriorating?

Are rate targets still valid?

Is the selected business model still working?

---

# 6. JOB-LEVEL RECOVERY TEST

Each job should be tested against the same business recovery model.

A job should be measured by asking:

- did labour recover labour?
- did assets recover assets?
- did overhead burden recover?
- did COG / material markup hold?
- did subcontractor allowance hold?
- did quoted rates match the target model?
- did actual delivery prove or disprove the quote?
- did the job improve or weaken the business model?

This is stronger than basic job profit.

The question is not only:

Did this job make money?

The question is:

Did this job recover the business correctly?

---

# 7. BUSINESS-LEVEL RECOVERY TEST

The monthly business view should ask:

- did total labour recover?
- did productive assets recover?
- did overheads recover?
- did supplier / COG pressure move the margin?
- did material markup hold?
- did quoted work follow the target model?
- did invoiced work align with required recovery?
- did the overall business move closer to the selected model?

This turns the P&L into a recovery scorecard.

---

# 8. QUOTE CHECKING ROLE

Quote Checker enforces the selected business model before work starts.

It checks whether a quote aligns with:

- minimum recoverable rates
- suggested sell rates
- required labour recovery
- required asset recovery
- overhead recovery burden
- material / COG assumptions
- selected margin targets
- selected markup targets
- selected Business Modelling scenario

Quote Checker asks:

Does this quote follow the model we selected?

Quote Checker does not decide the model.

Business Modelling selects the model.

Quote Checker enforces it.

---

# 9. INVOICING / JOB ACTUALS ROLE

The invoicing and job actual layer proves whether the model held in reality.

It should connect:

- quote lines
- work orders
- purchase orders
- supplier invoices
- time records
- asset usage
- job invoices
- job actual costs
- job profitability

It should then compare actual performance back to the model.

This creates evidence for future improvement.

---

# 10. MODEL FEEDBACK ROLE

Model Feedback converts actual performance into review signals.

It should identify:

- rates that are too low
- supplier costs that are increasing
- material categories causing pressure
- labour productivity problems
- asset under-recovery
- overhead under-recovery
- quotes that passed but jobs failed
- quotes that failed but still made money
- repeated issues
- positive signals where the model worked

Feedback is evidence.

It is not automatic correction.

---

# 11. MODEL REVIEW ROLE

Model Review controls whether feedback becomes a model change.

No model should change silently.

Model Review should allow the user to:

- approve rate changes
- reject one-off issues
- defer decisions
- create new target models
- approve new quote checker target sets
- preserve version history
- roll back if required

Approval is decision.

Feedback is evidence.

# 18. MONTHLY CONTROL FUNCTIONS

Mirra’s monthly loop has three control functions.

## 1. Keep the business balanced

Monthly P&L tests whether the model is drifting.

Examples:

- labour cost increased
- COGS increased
- asset costs changed
- overheads moved
- revenue dropped
- margin compressed

## 2. Track actual performance

Job, invoicing, time, PO, and supplier actuals show whether work performed against the model.

Examples:

- labour did not recover
- asset recovery missed
- overhead burden was not covered
- COG margin failed
- supplier cost pressure increased

## 3. Align the system to the business model

The system compares:

Model target
vs
quoted work
vs
invoiced work
vs
actual P&L

Then the model can be reviewed and improved.

# 24. FINAL PRINCIPLE

Mirra is a closed-loop recovery model.

It uses monthly accounting truth to build and test the business model.

It uses that model to create rate targets and quote rules.

It checks jobs against the same model.

It compares actual monthly performance back to the model.

It creates feedback for controlled model improvement.

That loop is the product.

No fake precision.
No hidden assumptions.
No disconnected job costing.
No monthly P&L without model comparison.
No quote checking without selected target model.
No model change without review.
No source mutation.
No leaks.
