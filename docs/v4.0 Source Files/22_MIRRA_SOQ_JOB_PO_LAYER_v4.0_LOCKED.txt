# 3. CORE PRINCIPLE

P&L data tells the system what happened financially.

SOQ / Job / PO data tells the system what happened physically.

Both are required for full unit-rate truth.

Without SOQ / job-level data, the system can know:

- total revenue
- total COG
- gross margin
- labour burden
- asset burden
- overhead burden
- required recovery rates

But it cannot know:

- blocks per m²
- concrete per m³
- labour hours per m²
- machine hours per task
- material consumption per quote item
- true unit cost per job

The SOQ / Job / PO Layer provides that missing physical link.

# 5. WHY THIS LAYER IS REQUIRED

For product/unit costing, annual supplier totals are not enough.

Example:

Supplier spend:
Concrete purchases = $191,000

This tells the system total spend.

It does not tell the system:

- which jobs used the concrete
- how many m³ were poured
- which quote lines consumed it
- whether the cost relates to block fill, slab, footings, or site works
- what the cost per quoted unit was

SOQ / Job / PO data creates that link.

---

# 6. LABOUR-BASED BUSINESS RULE

For labour-based businesses, product/unit rates cannot be derived from P&L / COG totals alone.

Product/unit rates become valid only when this layer supplies:

- schedule quantity
- material quantity
- labour quantity
- asset quantity
- subcontractor quantity
- cost mapping

Until then:

Rate Builder may build hourly, crew, machine, and recovery-based rates.

True product/unit rates remain manual, preview-only, or not_available.

---

# 7. PRODUCT-BASED BUSINESS RULE

For product-based businesses, top-down unit rates may be valid when the business unit is the primary driver.

Example:

Block supply/install business:

Revenue ÷ m² = revenue per m²  
COG ÷ m² = COG per m²  
Margin ÷ m² = margin per m²

However, SOQ / Job / PO data still improves accuracy by proving:

- actual material consumption
- actual labour consumption
- actual subcontractor cost
- actual job margin
- actual unit profitability

---

# 8. QUOTE CHECKER RELATIONSHIP

Quote Checker checks the quote against the approved model.

SOQ / Job / PO later checks:

- what was quoted
- what was ordered
- what was delivered
- what was invoiced
- what was actually consumed
- what profit was actually achieved

Quote Checker is pre-job validation.

SOQ / Job / PO is job execution and actual-cost validation.

---

# 9. RATE BUILDER RELATIONSHIP

Rate Builder creates approved model rates.

SOQ / Job / PO later validates whether those rates were correct.

If actual jobs show:

- labour takes longer than modelled
- materials cost more than assumed
- assets are underused
- subcontractors cost more than expected
- waste is higher than assumed

then this layer feeds evidence back to:

- Rate Builder
- Business Outcome
- future model updates

---

# 10. BUSINESS OUTCOME RELATIONSHIP

Business Outcome models what should change.

SOQ / Job / PO proves whether those changes work in real jobs.

Example:

Business Outcome target:

Increase digger + operator rate to $X/hr.

SOQ / Job / PO checks:

- quoted rate used
- actual hours used
- actual asset cost recovered
- job margin achieved

This creates a feedback loop.

---

# 11. REVENUE / COGS RELATIONSHIP

Revenue / COGS provides supplier/category financial detail.

SOQ / Job / PO provides job-level consumption detail.

Together they allow:

- supplier spend to be mapped to job items
- COG categories to be linked to quote tasks
- material spend to be converted into cost per unit
- supplier pricing to be tested against actual job profitability

Revenue / COGS alone is not enough.

SOQ / Job / PO creates the physical bridge.

# 22. UNIT COST VALIDATION

This layer can calculate true unit cost when quantity exists.

Formula:

actual_unit_cost =
actual_total_cost / actual_quantity

Where actual_total_cost may include:

- material cost
- labour cost
- asset cost
- subcontractor cost
- allocated overhead recovery

If actual_quantity = 0:

actual_unit_cost = 0
status = review_required or blocked

Do not divide by zero.

---

# 23. PRODUCT RATE VALIDATION

For product/unit lines:

actual_unit_margin =
quoted_unit_rate - actual_unit_cost

actual_unit_margin_percent =
actual_unit_margin / quoted_unit_rate

This allows the system to validate:

- whether the quoted unit rate was correct
- whether the product line recovered cost
- whether material assumptions were accurate
- whether labour assumptions were accurate
- whether the rate should be updated

# 25. FEEDBACK LOOP TO RATE BUILDER

- material cost above assumption
- labour hours above assumption

- overhead recovery missed
- job profitable
- job under-recovered

But this layer must not automatically overwrite approved rates.

- category
- quantity

- status

# 28. STATUS VALUES

Allowed soq_job_po_status values:

ready
ready_with_warnings
review_required
blocked
preview_only
not_ready

Status rules:

ready:
Job/quote/PO data is linked and usable.

ready_with_warnings:
Usable but warnings remain.

review_required:
Data exists but mapping or quantity review is needed.

blocked:
Required job, quote, quantity, or cost data is invalid.

preview_only:
Data displays but is not trusted.

not_ready:
Required data is missing.
