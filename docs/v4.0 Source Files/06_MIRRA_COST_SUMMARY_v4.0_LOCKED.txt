Cost Summary aggregates trusted operating cost burden from source modules. It does not interpret revenue, assign operating structure, test recovery, build rates, or create the business verdict.

---

# 1. PURPOSE

The Cost Summary module defines the business operating cost burden.

It answers:

What does the business cost to operate before recovery strategy, rate setting, or outcome diagnosis?

Cost Summary aggregates the trusted annual cost outputs from:

- Labour
- Assets
- General Overheads

It creates the baseline cost burden that later modules use to test recovery, build rates, and diagnose the business model.

Cost Summary does not decide whether the business model works.

It provides the cost base.

---

# 2. MODULE ROLE

Cost Summary owns:

- aggregated people cost
- aggregated asset cost
- aggregated general overhead cost
- total business cost burden
- baseline productive output
- baseline required recovery rate per productive hour
- cost source composition
- cost summary readiness
- cost summary warnings
- cost burden trust state

Cost Summary does not own:

- revenue
- COGS
- margin pool
- P&L classification
- operational COG assignment
- COG Account Transactions reconciliation
- staff identity
- asset identity
- overhead category source truth
- cost allocation groups
- overhead distribution
- recovery strategy
- required unit recovery
- Rate Builder rates
- Business Outcome verdict
- Business Modelling scenarios
- quote checks

# 4. CORE PRINCIPLE

Cost Summary is an aggregation layer.

It must answer:

How much cost must the business recover?

It must not answer:

- how should the business recover it?
- which labour group recovers it?
- which asset group recovers it?
- what unit rate should be charged?
- is the business viable?
- what should the owner change?
- what quote should be accepted?

Those questions belong downstream.

# 6. COGS EXCLUSION RULE

COGS / Cost of Sales must not be included in Cost Summary operating cost burden.

Reason:

COGS is treated differently depending on business mode.

## Hours-based mode

COGS creates material margin.

It is not part of the hourly business operating cost burden by default.

## Product / unit-based mode

COGS is consumed by the product/unit.

It belongs in unit cost stack logic through Revenue / COGS and Rate Builder.

Therefore Cost Summary must not add COGS into:

total_cost_burden

or:

total_business_cost_annual

COGS must remain outside Cost Summary.

---

# 7. REVENUE EXCLUSION RULE

Revenue must not be included in Cost Summary.

Revenue belongs to Revenue / COGS and Business Summary.

Cost Summary may later be displayed beside revenue context, but it does not own revenue.

Cost Summary is cost-only.

# 10. PRIMARY FORMULA

Cost Summary primary formula:

total_cost_burden =
total_people_cost_annual
+ total_asset_cost_annual
+ total_business_overheads

Where:

total_people_cost_annual =
Labour source output

total_asset_cost_annual =
Assets source output

total_business_overheads =
General Overheads source output

No COGS.

No revenue.

No profit target unless a later v4.0 brief explicitly adds target profit to Business Modelling or Rate Builder.

---

# 11. TOTAL BUSINESS COST ANNUAL

total_business_cost_annual should equal:

total_cost_burden

unless the current codebase requires both names for compatibility.

Preferred v4.0 interpretation:

total_cost_burden =
primary contract variable

total_business_cost_annual =
alias / compatibility variable where existing downstream modules expect it

Do not create divergent meanings.

If both exist, they must reconcile exactly.

---

# 12. REQUIRED REVENUE VARIABLE

required_revenue may remain as a compatibility variable if already used.

In v4.0, be careful with wording.

Cost Summary does not calculate commercial revenue target in the full sense.

It calculates the cost burden that must be recovered.

Preferred wording:

required_revenue =
total_cost_burden

or:

minimum_recovery_required =
total_cost_burden

If required_revenue exists for compatibility, its meaning must be:

the minimum annual recovery required to cover operating cost burden before profit target

It must not imply sales revenue, invoice revenue, or market revenue.

Revenue / COGS owns actual revenue.

---

# 13. BASELINE PRODUCTIVE OUTPUT

Cost Summary consumes:

total_productive_output

from Labour.

This represents annual productive hours for the current labour setup.

In labour/hour-based mode, this is the denominator for baseline required recovery per productive hour.

In product/unit-based mode, Cost Summary may still calculate this baseline productive-hour pressure, but Business Summary and Recovery Summary must use product/unit driver where appropriate.

Cost Summary does not own product/unit output.

Revenue / COGS owns unit driver rows and annual unit counts.

---

# 14. REQUIRED RECOVERY RATE FORMULA

Cost Summary calculates:

required_recovery_rate =
total_cost_burden / total_productive_output

This is the baseline hourly cost pressure.

If total_productive_output is zero:

required_recovery_rate = 0

and:

cost_summary_status = blocked

or:

cost_summary_status = not_ready

depending source state.

Do not divide by zero.

Do not invent productive hours.

Do not use units sold as a Cost Summary denominator.

Product/unit recovery per unit is handled downstream in Business Summary, Recovery Summary, and Rate Builder.

# 18. MODEL READINESS INHERITANCE

Cost Summary must inherit Model Readiness.

If model_trust_state = blocked:

cost_summary_model_trust_state = blocked

If model_trust_state = preview_only:

cost_summary_model_trust_state = preview_only

If model_trust_state = warning:

cost_summary_model_trust_state = warning

If all source modules are ready and model_trust_state = ready:

cost_summary_model_trust_state = ready

Cost Summary must not override a blocked upstream trust state.

---

# 19. COST SUMMARY READY CONDITIONS

Cost Summary is ready when:

- Labour source exists
- Assets source exists
- General Overheads source exists
- total_people_cost_annual is numeric
- total_asset_cost_annual is numeric
- total_business_overheads is numeric
- total_cost_burden can be calculated
- total_productive_output is numeric
- required_recovery_rate can be calculated or clearly marked zero because productive output is zero
- Model Readiness is not blocked
- no blocking source module warning exists

Cost Summary is not ready when:

- Labour source is missing
- Assets source is missing
- General Overheads source is missing
- any required source amount is invalid
- total_cost_burden cannot be calculated
- source module is blocked
- Model Readiness is blocked
- productive output is missing where required

# 20. STATUS VALUES

Allowed cost_summary_status values:

ready
ready_with_warnings
review_required
blocked
preview_only
not_ready

Status rules:

ready:
All required source modules are ready, totals are numeric, trust state ready.

ready_with_warnings:
Cost Summary can calculate, but warnings from source modules or reconciliation remain.

review_required:
Cost Summary can calculate, but user review is required before relying downstream.

blocked:
Cost Summary cannot be trusted due to invalid/missing source values or blocked upstream trust state.

preview_only:
Cost Summary can display but must not be used as final trusted output because upstream data is incomplete or manually estimated.

not_ready:
Cost Summary does not have sufficient data to calculate.

# 23. WARNING MEANINGS

## cost_summary_missing_labour

Use when Labour source output is not available.

Blocking:

true.

## cost_summary_missing_assets

Use when Assets source output is not available.

Blocking:

true unless Assets are explicitly confirmed not applicable.

## cost_summary_missing_general_overheads

Use when General Overheads source output is not available.

Blocking:

true unless overheads are explicitly confirmed as zero.

## cost_summary_productive_output_zero

Use when total_productive_output is zero.

Blocking:

true for hourly recovery rate.

Message should explain:

Required recovery per productive hour cannot be calculated because productive output is zero.

## cost_summary_total_cost_zero

Use when total_cost_burden is zero.

Blocking:

review_required unless confirmed.

## cost_summary_upstream_blocked

Use when Model Readiness or a source module is blocked.

Blocking:

true.

## cost_summary_cogs_excluded_notice

Use as info if needed.

Message should explain:

COGS is not included in Cost Summary because COGS is handled in Revenue / COGS and Rate Builder according to business mode.

Blocking:

false.

2. Preserve current working totals if they are correct.
3. Confirm Labour source value is consumed, not recalculated.
4. Confirm Assets source value is consumed, not recalculated.
5. Confirm General Overheads source value is consumed, not recalculated.
6. Confirm COGS is not included.
7. Confirm revenue is not included.
8. Confirm total_cost_burden formula.
9. Confirm required_recovery_rate formula.
10. Confirm zero productive output guardrail.
11. Add source composition output if missing.
12. Add trust-state inheritance if missing.
13. Add structured warnings if missing.
14. Add reconciliation context if missing.
15. Run build.
16. Confirm downstream Business Summary still receives the expected values.

# 42. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

The Cost Summary v4.0 build is accepted when:

1. Cost Summary consumes Labour, Assets, and General Overheads only.
2. Cost Summary does not include COGS.
3. Cost Summary does not include Revenue.
4. total_people_cost_annual is traceable to Labour.
5. total_asset_cost_annual is traceable to Assets.
6. total_business_overheads is traceable to General Overheads.
7. total_cost_burden equals people + assets + overheads.
8. total_business_cost_annual does not diverge from total_cost_burden.
9. required_recovery_rate equals total_cost_burden / total_productive_output.
10. Zero productive output is guarded and does not divide by zero.
11. Source composition is visible.
12. Model Readiness trust state is inherited.
13. Module Reconciliation context is visible.
14. Cost Summary warnings are structured.
15. Downstream modules receive stable outputs.
16. Cost Summary does not create recovery strategy.
17. Cost Summary does not create rates.
18. Cost Summary does not create business verdict.
19. Build passes.
20. No hidden leaks are introduced.

# 43. FINAL PRINCIPLE

Cost Summary defines the operating cost burden.

It is the cost base.

It does not decide recovery.

It does not interpret COGS.

It does not assign structure.

It does not build rates.

It does not diagnose the business.

It answers one question:

What does this business cost to run?

No leaks.
