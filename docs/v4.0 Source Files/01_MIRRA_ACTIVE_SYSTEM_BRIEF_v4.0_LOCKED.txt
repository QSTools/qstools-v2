# 3. HIGHEST-LEVEL PRINCIPLE

Mirra is built on:

First principles for everything.
Simple in hindsight. Invisible beforehand.

The system must reveal what was previously hidden in the business.

Every cost must belong to exactly one owner.

Every important output must be traceable, explainable, and defensible.

If a number cannot be defended, it should not be trusted.

Core product question:

Do your assumptions about how your business runs match reality?

---

# 4. CORE SYSTEM PHILOSOPHY

Mirra uses historical accounting data to build a forward-looking commercial model.

The P&L shows what happened.

Mirra uses that information to classify, reconcile, and model what the business needs to recover going forward.

Past accounting data is input evidence.

Forward-looking commercial cost is the decision basis.

Business Setup defines whether the business is primarily interpreted through:

productive hours
or
units sold

This allows Mirra to support both:

labour / service businesses
and
product / unit businesses

without changing the first-principles model.

---

# 5. CORE MODE-AWARE PRINCIPLE

Mirra uses one universal recovery equation:

Total Business Cost ÷ Activity Driver = Required Recovery Per Output

The activity driver is selected by Business Setup and applied downstream.

labour_based  → productive hours → $/hour
product_based → units sold       → $/unit

This is not an industry category.

It is the primary output driver used by the system to interpret recovery pressure.

Mode is system-level.

Individual rates may still have different drivers later in Rate Builder.

# 11. RATE BUILDER NEW ROLE

Rate Builder is the first layer where upstream pressure becomes pricing.

It consumes:

Cost Allocation structure
Recovery Summary recovery requirements

Asset Reality pressure

It builds:

minimum recoverable rates

component rates
full-group rates
subset rates
machine + operator rates
labour rates
crew rates
unit rates where valid
Quote Checker handoff values

Rate Builder is mode-guided, not mode-restricted.

Labour-based business:
default rate style = hourly / labour / asset / crew

Product-based business:
default rate style = unit / product

Exception rates are allowed.

Example:

A labour-based business may still manually build product/unit rates where valid.
A product-based business may still sell dayworks/hourly rates.

Primary rule:

Rate Builder turns recovery pressure into charge-out rates.

It does not create source costs, assign costs, test recovery again, or issue the final business outcome.

# 12. LABOUR-BASED PRODUCT RATE LIMITATION

For labour-based businesses, true product/unit rates cannot be derived from annual supplier COG totals alone.

To build true product/unit rates, Mirra needs:

schedule of quantities
job-level quantities
material consumption
labour consumption
asset consumption
subcontractor consumption
PO/job cost mapping

Until SOQ / Job / PO exists, product/unit rates in labour mode must be:

manual
template-based
preview-only
or not_available

Do not fake product rates from supplier totals.

Do not infer physical quantity consumption from high-level COG detail.

---

# 13. PRODUCT-BASED TOP-DOWN UNIT RULE

For product-based businesses, top-down unit analysis may be valid when the unit is the primary business driver.

Example:

Revenue ÷ m² = revenue per m²
COG ÷ m² = COG per m²
Margin ÷ m² = margin per m²

This works only where the unit is the primary driver and the business setup supports it.

Top-down unit derivation is not valid merely because a unit label exists.

---

# 14. REVENUE / COGS DETAIL RULE

Revenue / COGS has two levels of value:

1. P&L / financial truth
2. supplier/category detail

For labour-based businesses:

Revenue / COGS detail is used for:

margin visibility
material markup context
supplier spend concentration
category spend concentration
supplier negotiation insight
future SOQ/job layer preparation

It is not used as a direct product/unit rate construction input unless manual or SOQ data exists.

For product-based businesses:

Revenue / COGS detail may support unit margin and unit COG analysis where the product/unit driver is valid.

---

# 15. MATERIAL MARKUP RULE

Mirra may calculate one blended material markup.

There is one blended material markup at the model level.

The system must not pretend there is a true supplier-level markup unless source data supports that.

Supplier/category detail shows contribution to the blended result.

Correct interpretation:

material markup = one blended result
supplier spend = contribution / influence on that result
category spend = contribution / influence on that result

Supplier/category sensitivity belongs in Business Modelling.
