# 1. PURPOSE

Business Setup defines the basic identity and recovery-mode foundation for the Mirra model.

It answers:

What business is being modelled?
Does the business recover cost mainly through hours or through product/unit margin?
Is the setup complete enough for mode-aware downstream modules?

Business Setup is the starting point for mode-aware labels, calculations, and downstream interpretation.

It does not calculate:

- revenue
- COGS
- labour cost
- asset cost
- overheads
- total cost burden
- recovery strategy
- Cost Allocation
- Rate Builder outputs
- Business Outcome verdicts

---

# 2. MODULE ROLE

Business Setup owns:

- business name
- business type
- commercial mode selection
- hours-based vs product/unit-based flag
- setup completed flag
- setup timestamps
- basic setup readiness
- setup warnings

Business Setup does not own:

- P&L data
- revenue lines
- COGS lines
- margin pool
- unit driver rows
- labour data
- asset data
- overhead data
- cost summary
- recovery tests
- rate models
- outcome diagnosis
- scenario modelling
- quote logic

# 4. CORE PRINCIPLE

Business Setup must remain simple.

It should not try to solve the business.

It only identifies the business and sets the commercial recovery mode.

The selected mode affects downstream logic, but the calculations happen downstream.

---

# 5. BUSINESS MODE PRINCIPLE

Mirra v4.0 supports two primary active business modes:

1. Hours-based / service-based
2. Product / unit-based

These are not rigid industry categories.

They describe how the business mainly recovers cost.

---

# 6. HOURS-BASED / SERVICE-BASED MODE

Use this mode when the business mainly sells:

- time
- labour
- services
- productive hours
- charge-out hours
- labour + plant services
- asset-supported work

Examples:

- construction contractor
- builder
- tradie
- earthworks contractor
- consultant
- mechanic
- service business
- labour hire
- contractor with productive crews

Primary recovery driver:

productive hours

Primary downstream display:

$/hour

Core recovery rule:

Labour recovers labour.
Assets recover assets.
Materials cover themselves and create margin.

---

# 7. PRODUCT / UNIT-BASED MODE

Use this mode when the business mainly sells:

- products
- units
- m²
- m³
- tonnes
- each
- subscriptions
- transactions
- productised services
- repeatable outputs

Examples:

- concrete pumping by m³
- concrete placing by m²
- café
- retailer
- manufacturer
- subscription business
- productised service business

Primary recovery driver:

units / selected commercial unit

Primary downstream display:

$/unit

Core recovery rule:

COGS is consumed by the product.
Revenue minus COGS creates margin.
That margin must recover labour, assets, overheads, and profit.

# 10. BUSINESS NAME

business_name stores the name of the business being modelled.

Rules:

- business_name may be user-entered
- business_name may be blank during initial setup
- if blank, show review_required
- do not use business_name as database/company identity later unless future company system brief says so
- business_name is display identity only in this module

---

# 11. BUSINESS TYPE

business_type stores the active commercial recovery mode.

Allowed current values:

labour_based
product_based

Rules:

- business_type must be selected before downstream mode-aware modules are trusted
- business_type should be editable during development/build phase
- changing business_type should not delete existing downstream data
- changing business_type may change downstream labels and readiness warnings
- changing business_type should not silently reclassify revenue, COGS, labour, assets, or overheads

---

# 12. DERIVED MODE FLAGS

Business Setup owns derived booleans:

is_labour_based
is_product_based

Formula:

is_labour_based =
business_type === "labour_based"

is_product_based =
business_type === "product_based"

These flags are consumed downstream for display logic and mode-specific calculation paths.

Do not manually store conflicting values.

If business_type changes, flags must update.

---

# 13. SETUP COMPLETED FLAG

Business Setup owns:

setup_completed

setup_completed should be true only when:

- business_name is present or intentionally skipped
- business_type is selected
- no blocking setup warning exists

setup_completed does not mean the whole Mirra model is ready.

It only means Business Setup itself is complete.

Model Readiness decides overall model trust.

---

# 14. TIMESTAMPS

Business Setup owns:

created_at
updated_at

Rules:

- created_at should be set when setup record is first created
- updated_at should change when setup values change
- timestamps are useful for future audit/source trace
- timestamps do not determine trust by themselves

---

# 16. BUSINESS SETUP READY CONDITIONS

Business Setup is ready when:

- business_type is selected
- business_type is valid
- setup_completed is true
- no blocking setup warning exists

Business Setup is review_required when:

- business_name is missing but business_type exists
- business mode may need confirmation
- user changed mode after downstream data exists

Business Setup is blocked when:

- business_type is missing
- business_type is invalid
- setup state is corrupted

Business Setup is not_ready when:

- no setup data exists

---

# 17. MODE CHANGE RULE

Changing business_type is allowed during the active build phase.

But changing business_type must not silently rewrite downstream source data.

If business_type changes after downstream modules contain data, show warning:

Business mode changed. Review Revenue / COGS, Business Summary, Recovery Summary, Rate Builder, and Business Outcome because mode-dependent interpretation may change.

Affected downstream modules:

- Revenue / COGS
- Business Summary
- Revenue Reality
- Cost Allocation
- Recovery Summary
- Rate Builder
- Business Outcome
- Business Modelling later
- Quote Engine later

Do not delete downstream data.

Do not auto-reclassify COGS.

Do not auto-create unit driver rows unless Revenue / COGS owns that behaviour.

---

# 18. MODE-AWARE DOWNSTREAM RULE

Business Setup mode affects downstream labels and logic.

Examples:

If business_type = labour_based:

Business Summary should show:

- productive hours
- required recovery per hour
- current margin per hour
- recovery gap per hour

Recovery Summary should test:

- labour recovery
- asset recovery
- material margin

Rate Builder should show:

- hourly rates
- asset rates
- material margin

If business_type = product_based:

Business Summary should show:

- annual units
- required recovery per unit
- current margin per unit
- recovery gap per unit

Recovery Summary should test:

- unit margin
- required units
- required margin per unit

Rate Builder should show:

- unit cost stack
- required unit rate
- fully burdened unit margin

---

# 19. NO CALCULATION RULE

Business Setup must not calculate:

- total_revenue
- total_direct_costs
- margin_pool
- gross_margin_percent
- total_labour_cost_annual
- total_asset_cost_annual
- total_general_overheads
- total_cost_burden
- net_position
- recovery_gap
- required_rate
- business_outcome_status

Those belong downstream.

---

# 20. NO ASSUMPTION RULE

Business Setup must not infer the business mode from:

- business name
- industry
- P&L account names
- uploaded file name
- user location
- prior conversations
- revenue labels
- COGS labels

The user must choose the mode.

Mirra may explain the choices.

Mirra may suggest what each means.

But the user decides.

---

# 21. WARNING OBJECT

Business Setup warnings must use the standard shape:

{
  warning_id,
  source_module,
  severity,
  message,
  affected_variable,
  blocking,
  review_action
}

Required warning IDs:

business_setup_missing
business_type_missing
business_type_invalid
business_name_missing
business_mode_changed
business_mode_requires_review

---

# 22. WARNING MEANINGS

## business_setup_missing

Use when no setup record exists.

Blocking:

true.

## business_type_missing

Use when business_type has not been selected.

Blocking:

true.

## business_type_invalid

Use when business_type is not an allowed value.

Blocking:

true.

## business_name_missing

Use when business_name is blank.

Blocking:

false unless name is required by future company profile logic.

## business_mode_changed

Use when the user changes mode after downstream data exists.

Blocking:

false by default, but affected downstream modules should show review_required.

## business_mode_requires_review

Use when mode-dependent downstream pages need confirmation after mode selection or mode change.

Blocking:

false unless the affected downstream page requires explicit setup.

Product / Unit-based business

---

# 35. MODE EXPLANATION PANEL

This panel should explain:

## Hours-based / Service-based

Use this when your business mainly sells time, labour, productive capacity, or services.

In this mode:

Labour recovers labour.
Assets recover assets.
Materials cover themselves and create margin.

## Product / Unit-based

Use this when your business mainly sells units, products, m², m³, tonnes, subscriptions, or repeatable outputs.

In this mode:

COGS is consumed by the product.
Margin after COGS must recover labour, assets, overheads, and profit.

---

# 36. DOWNSTREAM IMPACT PANEL

This panel should explain what the selected mode affects.

Mode affects:

- Revenue / COGS setup
- Business Summary labels
- Recovery Summary tests
- Rate Builder formulas
- Business Outcome wording

Mode does not change:

- P&L source data
- Labour source data
- Assets source data
- General Overheads source data

---

# 37. WARNING / REVIEW PANEL

This panel should show:

- missing mode
- invalid mode
- missing business name
- mode changed after downstream data exists
- downstream review required

No warning should be hidden.

---

# 38. HELP PANEL

The help panel should explain:

- Business Setup chooses how the business mainly recovers cost
- it does not calculate the model
- the user can change mode during setup
- changing mode may require downstream review
- downstream modules calculate the financial result
- the user decides the mode

# 43. FINAL PRINCIPLE

Business Setup chooses the recovery mode.

It identifies the business.

It does not solve the business.

It does not calculate performance.

It does not create source truth for other modules.

It sets the mode so downstream modules can calculate correctly.

User chooses the mode.
Mirra explains the consequence.

No assumptions.
No hidden mode inference.
No downstream source mutation.
No leaks.
