PRIMARY RULE:
Labour owns people cost and productive capacity. No downstream module may infer labour rates, staff types, or productive hours.

---

# 1. PURPOSE

The Labour module defines the business people-cost base and productive labour capacity.

It answers:

Who works in the business?
What do they cost?
Are they productive or non-productive?
How many productive hours do they create?
What is the true weighted cost rate of productive labour?
What is the weighted productive rate by staff type?

Labour is a source module.

It does not decide recovery strategy.

It does not allocate labour to operating groups.

It does not price jobs.

It does not calculate Business Outcome.

---

# 2. MODULE ROLE

Labour owns:

- staff identity
- staff type identity
- productive/non-productive classification
- employment cost
- wage/salary cost
- employer obligations where included
- productive hours
- non-productive hours
- total productive labour cost
- total productive labour hours
- weighted all-productive labour rate
- weighted productive staff type rates
- labour readiness
- labour warnings
- labour trust state

Labour does not own:

- subcontractor COGS
- materials
- revenue
- COGS
- asset utilisation
- asset recovery
- asset/labour pairing
- operational groups
- overhead distribution
- Cost Allocation groups
- Recovery Summary tests
- Rate Builder rates
- Business Outcome verdicts

# 4. CORE PRINCIPLE

Labour is real cost.

Labour must be paid.

Mirra must not assume labour is recovered just because revenue exists.

Labour cost must be calculated from explicit Labour module data.

Do not use P&L labour as the operational labour source.

Do not use charge-out rate as labour cost.

Do not use target rate as labour cost.

Do not infer productivity.

Do not infer staff type from staff name.

---

# 5. STAFF IDENTITY RULE

Every staff row must have a stable identity.

Required fields:

staff_id
staff_name
staff_type_id
staff_type_name
is_productive
is_active

Rules:

- staff_id must be stable.
- staff_name is display only.
- staff_type_id must be stable.
- staff_type_name is display only.
- staff type must be explicitly assigned.
- if staff type is missing, send to review.
- do not use staff_name as ID.
- do not infer staff type from staff_name.

---

# 6. STAFF TYPE RULE

Staff type is required for productive staff type rate calculations.

Example staff types:

- Working Director
- Foreman
- Leading Hand
- Operator
- Labourer
- Apprentice
- Admin
- Office Manager
- Sales
- Management
- User-created staff type

Staff type must be user-controlled.

Do not hardcode only construction staff types.

The system may provide starter examples, but the user must be able to create or rename staff types.

---

# 7. PRODUCTIVE VS NON-PRODUCTIVE RULE

Labour must clearly separate:

- productive staff
- non-productive staff

Productive staff contribute productive hours.

Non-productive staff do not contribute productive labour hours.

Non-productive staff still have cost.

Non-productive staff cost remains part of the business cost burden where applicable.

Productive staff type rate outputs include productive staff only.

Do not remove non-productive staff from the Labour module.

Do not treat non-productive hours as productive capacity.

---

# 8. WHAT BELONGS IN LABOUR

Labour may include:

- wages
- salaries
- owner/director wage where treated as labour
- employer contributions
- payroll on-costs
- leave allowance
- ACC / workers compensation where relevant
- KiwiSaver / superannuation employer cost where relevant
- payroll tax where relevant
- productive staff cost
- non-productive staff cost
- admin staff cost where employee cost
- management staff cost where employee cost

This list is not exhaustive.

Regional cost rules may vary later.

For v4.0, the key rule is:

People cost belongs in Labour unless explicitly classified elsewhere by a future active brief.

---

# 9. WHAT DOES NOT BELONG IN LABOUR

Labour must not include:

- subcontractor invoices unless they are treated as internal labour by explicit user setup
- material COGS
- product COGS
- asset ownership cost
- asset finance
- fuel
- repairs
- insurance
- rent
- software
- admin overheads not directly staff employment cost
- GST/tax payable
- income tax
- drawings/dividends not treated as wage
- profit target

Subcontractor COGS normally belongs in Revenue / COGS.

Overhead staff-related expenses that are not direct employment cost belong in General Overheads.

# 11. LABOUR COST VARIABLES

Labour owns:

annual_base_cost
annual_on_costs
annual_labour_cost
total_labour_cost_annual
total_people_cost_annual
total_productive_labour_cost
total_non_productive_labour_cost

Formula:

annual_labour_cost =
annual_base_cost + annual_on_costs

total_labour_cost_annual =
sum annual_labour_cost for active staff

total_people_cost_annual =
total_labour_cost_annual

unless compatibility requires separate naming.

If both total_labour_cost_annual and total_people_cost_annual exist, they must reconcile exactly unless explicitly briefed otherwise.

---

# 12. PRODUCTIVE HOURS VARIABLES

Labour owns:

productive_hours_annual
non_productive_hours_annual
total_hours_annual
total_productive_labour_hours
total_productive_output

Formula:

total_productive_labour_hours =
sum productive_hours_annual for active productive staff

total_productive_output =
total_productive_labour_hours

In v4.0, total_productive_output remains the compatibility variable used by Cost Summary.

Do not use units sold as total_productive_output.

Units sold belong in Revenue / COGS.

---

# 13. ALL-PRODUCTIVE WEIGHTED LABOUR RATE

Labour must calculate:

weighted_all_productive_labour_rate

Formula:

weighted_all_productive_labour_rate =
total_productive_labour_cost / total_productive_labour_hours

If total_productive_labour_hours = 0:

weighted_all_productive_labour_rate = 0

and:

rate_status = invalid_zero_productive_hours

Do not divide by zero.

Do not substitute total hours.

Do not substitute expected hours.

Do not guess.

---

# 14. PRODUCTIVE STAFF TYPE RATES

Labour must output:

productive_staff_type_rates[]

This is a core v4.0 output.

It gives downstream modules a realistic weighted rate for each productive staff type.

This is required for:

- Cost Allocation
- Recovery Summary
- Rate Builder
- future crew mix builder
- future Quote Engine

---

# 15. PRODUCTIVE STAFF TYPE RATE ROW CONTRACT

Each productive_staff_type_rates row must include:

{
  staff_type_id,
  staff_type_name,
  staff_count,
  total_annual_cost,
  total_productive_hours,
  weighted_productive_hourly_rate,
  productive_share_percent,
  is_productive,
  rate_status,
  source
}

source should be:

labour_module

---

# 16. PRODUCTIVE STAFF TYPE RATE FORMULA

For each productive staff type:

total_annual_cost =
sum annual_labour_cost for active productive staff assigned to that staff type

total_productive_hours =
sum productive_hours_annual for active productive staff assigned to that staff type

weighted_productive_hourly_rate =
total_annual_cost / total_productive_hours

productive_share_percent =
total_productive_hours / total_productive_labour_hours

This is not a simple average of individual hourly rates.

It is weighted by productive hours.

---

# 17. STAFF TYPE RATE EXAMPLE

Example staff:

Operator A:
annual cost = $95,000
productive hours = 1,600

Operator B:
annual cost = $85,000
productive hours = 1,500

Operator C:
annual cost = $75,000
productive hours = 1,300

Operator total annual cost:

$95,000 + $85,000 + $75,000 = $255,000

Operator total productive hours:

1,600 + 1,500 + 1,300 = 4,400

Operator weighted productive hourly rate:

$255,000 / 4,400 = $57.95/hr

This rate belongs in Labour.

Rate Builder may consume it.

Rate Builder must not recreate it.

---

# 18. PRODUCTIVE STAFF TYPE RATE STATUS

Allowed rate_status values:

ready
invalid_zero_productive_hours
review_zero_cost
review_unassigned_staff_type

Status rules:

ready:
staff type has productive staff, annual cost, and productive hours.

invalid_zero_productive_hours:
staff type has annual cost but zero productive hours.

review_zero_cost:
staff type has productive hours but zero annual cost.

review_unassigned_staff_type:
productive staff exists without staff type assignment.

---

# 19. ZERO-HOUR GUARDRAIL

If a productive staff type has cost but zero productive hours:

weighted_productive_hourly_rate = 0
rate_status = invalid_zero_productive_hours

Warning:

This staff type has annual labour cost but no productive hours. Assign productive hours before using this rate.

This warning should be blocking for downstream rate use.

---

# 20. ZERO-COST GUARDRAIL

If a productive staff type has productive hours but zero annual cost:

weighted_productive_hourly_rate = 0
rate_status = review_zero_cost

Warning:

This staff type has productive hours but no annual labour cost. Review labour cost setup.

This warning should require review.

---

# 21. UNASSIGNED STAFF TYPE GUARDRAIL

If productive staff has no staff_type_id:

place into review bucket:

staff_type_id = unassigned_productive_review
staff_type_name = Unassigned / Review required

Expose:

unassigned_productive_staff[]
unassigned_productive_staff_cost
unassigned_productive_staff_hours

Warning:

Productive staff have no staff type assigned. Assign staff type before using staff type rates.

This prevents hidden exclusions.

# 23. LABOUR AND P&L RECONCILIATION

Labour does not force itself to match the P&L.

Module Reconciliation compares Labour module output to P&L labour benchmark.

Labour must expose values needed by Module Reconciliation.

Relevant Labour outputs:

total_labour_cost_annual
total_people_cost_annual
labour_ready
labour_status
labour_model_trust_state

If P&L labour differs from Labour module, show variance in Module Reconciliation.

Do not overwrite Labour with P&L.

Do not overwrite P&L with Labour.

Always variance.

Never silent overwrite.

---

# 24. LABOUR READINESS CONDITIONS

Labour is ready when:

- at least one active staff row exists or user has explicitly confirmed no staff
- all active staff have valid annual_labour_cost
- productive staff have valid productive_hours_annual
- productive staff have staff_type_id
- productive totals can be calculated
- weighted rates can be calculated or invalid states are clearly flagged
- no blocking warnings exist

Labour is not ready when:

- no labour data exists
- active staff have invalid cost
- productive staff have missing productive hours
- productive staff have missing staff type
- total_productive_labour_hours is zero where productive labour is required
- total_labour_cost_annual cannot be calculated
- saved state is invalid

---

# 25. LABOUR STATUS VALUES

Allowed labour_status values:

ready
ready_with_warnings
review_required
blocked
preview_only
not_ready

Status rules:

ready:
Labour data is complete, staff type rates are valid, no blocking warnings.

ready_with_warnings:
Labour can be used, but non-blocking warnings remain.

review_required:
Labour can calculate, but user review is required before relying downstream.

blocked:
Labour cannot be trusted due to invalid cost/hours/staff type setup.

preview_only:
Labour can display estimated/manual data but should not be trusted as final.

not_ready:
No valid Labour source exists.

---

# 26. LABOUR TRUST STATE

Labour should expose:

labour_model_trust_state

Allowed values:

ready
warning
blocked
preview_only

Mapping:

labour_status = ready
→ ready

labour_status = ready_with_warnings or review_required
→ warning

labour_status = blocked or not_ready
→ blocked

labour_status = preview_only
→ preview_only

Downstream modules must inherit this trust state.

# 28. WARNING MEANINGS

## no_labour_data

Use when no staff rows exist.

Blocking:

true unless user explicitly confirms no labour.

## labour_invalid_staff_cost

Use when annual_labour_cost is invalid.

Blocking:

true.

## labour_missing_staff_type

Use when staff type is missing for productive staff.

Blocking:

true for staff type rates.

## labour_missing_productive_hours

Use when productive staff has missing productive hours.

Blocking:

true for recovery/rate calculations.

## labour_zero_productive_hours

Use when total productive hours is zero.

Blocking:

true for hourly recovery.

## productive_staff_type_zero_hours

Use when a staff type has cost but no productive hours.

Blocking:

true for that staff type rate.

## productive_staff_type_zero_cost

Use when a staff type has hours but no cost.

Blocking:

review_required.

## labour_reconciliation_variance

Use when Module Reconciliation reports Labour vs P&L variance.

Blocking:

false unless model trust state blocks downstream use.
