PRIMARY RULE:
General Overheads owns the overhead source pool. Cost Allocation may distribute that pool, but no downstream module may silently create, duplicate, or over-allocate overhead burden.

---

# 1. PURPOSE

The General Overheads module defines the business overhead cost pool.

It answers:

What does the business cost to keep running before direct production recovery is tested?

General Overheads captures, classifies, totals, and exposes the operating overhead burden that is not owned by Labour, Assets, or Revenue / COGS.

This module provides the traceable overhead source pool for:

- Cost Summary
- Module Reconciliation
- Business Summary
- Cost Allocation
- Recovery Summary
- Rate Builder
- Business Outcome

---

# 2. MODULE ROLE

General Overheads owns:

- general overhead source pool
- net general overhead total
- overhead category totals
- overhead source classification
- overhead readiness
- overhead warnings
- asset finance exclusion where selected
- overhead benchmark comparison inputs
- overhead source trace for downstream allocation

General Overheads does not own:

- employee wage cost
- employer obligations included in Labour
- productive labour hours
- productive labour rates
- asset ownership cost
- asset utilisation
- asset recovery rates
- COGS
- materials
- subcontract COGS
- revenue
- cost allocation groups
- overhead distribution method
- final recovery tests
- Rate Builder outputs
- Business Outcome verdict

---

---

# 4. CORE PRINCIPLE

General Overheads must remain a source module.

It defines the available overhead pool.

It does not decide the recovery model.

It does not decide how overhead is distributed across labour, assets, products, or units.

That distribution belongs to Cost Allocation.

General Overheads must always preserve:

- source amount
- source category
- P&L benchmark relationship
- exclusions
- warnings
- downstream trust state

---

# 5. EMPLOYEE OVERHEADS RULE

There is no separate active Employee Overheads module in Mirra v4.0.

Employee overhead-type costs are included in General Overheads through the overheads pathway.

Examples may include:

- staff training
- recruitment
- uniforms
- staff amenities
- staff welfare
- employment-related overheads that are not direct wage/on-cost calculations

Direct labour cost, wages, salaries, leave, ACC, employer obligations, and productive labour cost belong in Labour.

Do not recreate Employee Overheads as a separate module.

Do not send employee overheads directly to Cost Summary outside the General Overheads pathway.

# 9. P&L RELATIONSHIP

The P&L provides overhead benchmark evidence.

General Overheads should compare its total to relevant P&L operating expense benchmarks through Module Reconciliation.

General Overheads should not silently force its total to equal the P&L.

Example:

P&L General Overheads benchmark:
$310,000

General Overheads module:
$300,000

Variance:
$10,000

Mirra must show the variance.

Do not overwrite.

Do not hide.

Do not call the variance an error unless proven.

# 13. TOTAL GENERAL OVERHEADS

total_general_overheads is the main downstream source total.

Formula:

total_general_overheads =
sum of active general_overhead_categories annual_amount
after exclusions and adjustments defined in this module

If the module uses both gross and net forms:

gross_general_overheads_benchmark =
P&L/general overhead benchmark before exclusions

net_general_overheads =
overhead total after valid exclusions

Preferred downstream output:

total_general_overheads

Cost Summary consumes:

total_general_overheads

Cost Allocation consumes:

general_overheads_pool_amount

---

# 14. OVERHEAD SOURCE POOL

General Overheads must expose an overhead source pool for Cost Allocation.

Required variables:

general_overheads_pool_amount
general_overheads_pool_source_module
general_overheads_pool_source_name

Default values:

general_overheads_pool_amount = total_general_overheads
general_overheads_pool_source_module = general_overheads
general_overheads_pool_source_name = General Overheads

Cost Allocation may distribute this pool.

General Overheads does not distribute it.

---

# 15. ASSET FINANCE EXCLUSION RULE

Asset finance interest may appear in P&L operating expenses.

If asset finance interest is selected as asset-related and reviewed through the Assets pathway, General Overheads must be able to exclude it from the overhead pool to avoid double counting.

Required variables:

asset_finance_interest_excluded
asset_finance_interest_excluded_amount
adjusted_general_overheads_benchmark

Formula:

adjusted_general_overheads_benchmark =
gross_general_overheads_benchmark - asset_finance_interest_excluded_amount

Rules:

- exclusion must be explicit
- exclusion must be visible
- exclusion must not delete the original P&L line
- excluded amount must be available to Module Reconciliation
- exclusion must prevent double counting
- exclusion must not hide a P&L variance

---

# 16. ASSET RUNNING COST DISTINCTION

Asset ownership cost belongs in Assets.

Asset running costs may belong in General Overheads unless they are explicitly modelled as asset source pools.

Examples of running costs:

- fuel
- repairs
- servicing
- tyres
- registration
- insurance
- maintenance

These may be overheads in one business and asset-specific pools in another.

Mirra must not assume.

If running costs are assigned to Assets later, source pool guardrails must prevent over-allocation.

If running costs remain in General Overheads, Cost Allocation may distribute them only through explicit overhead allocation.

Do not double count them in both Assets and General Overheads.

---

# 17. PRINCIPAL REPAYMENT RULE

Principal repayments are not operating overhead cost.

Principal repayments belong to cash flow / financing analysis, not baseline operating recovery, unless a future active brief explicitly changes this.

Interest may be operating cost or asset finance review depending classification.

Principal must not be included in total_general_overheads.

---

# 18. TAX / GST RULE

GST collected or paid is not an overhead cost.

Income tax is not an operating overhead cost for baseline recovery.

Tax timing may matter for future cash flow, but it does not belong in General Overheads operating burden.

Do not include GST or income tax in total_general_overheads.

---

# 19. OWNER DRAWINGS / DIVIDENDS RULE

Owner drawings and dividends are not General Overheads.

If owner wages or director salary are part of Labour, they belong in Labour.

If owner return/profit target is modelled later, it belongs in Business Modelling or Rate Builder target margin, not General Overheads.

Do not hide drawings as overheads.

---

# 20. REVIEW REQUIRED RULE

Any overhead line that cannot be confidently classified should be assigned:

review_required = true

The module should show it clearly.

Review required items should not disappear.

If the item is material and unresolved, General Overheads status should become review_required or blocked depending severity.

---

# 21. GENERAL OVERHEADS READINESS

General Overheads is ready when:

- overhead categories exist
- active categories have numeric amounts
- review-required material items are resolved or explicitly accepted as review
- excluded asset finance amount is visible where selected
- total_general_overheads can be calculated
- source pool can be exposed
- no blocking warning exists

General Overheads is not ready when:

- no overhead source exists
- active overhead categories have invalid amounts
- source classification is incomplete
- total_general_overheads cannot be calculated
- material review-required lines remain unresolved
- source pool is negative due to invalid exclusion
- data shape is invalid

---

# 22. STATUS VALUES

Allowed general_overheads_status values:

ready
ready_with_warnings
review_required
blocked
not_ready

Status mapping:

ready:
Overhead source pool is complete and trusted.

ready_with_warnings:
Overhead source pool can be used, but non-blocking warnings remain.

review_required:
Some categories or exclusions require review before relying downstream.

blocked:
Overhead pool cannot be trusted or used downstream.

not_ready:
No valid overhead data exists.

# 25. WARNING MEANINGS

## no_general_overheads_data

Use when there are no overhead categories or valid overhead source records.

Blocking:

true unless the user explicitly confirms zero overheads.

## general_overheads_total_zero

Use when overhead total is zero.

Blocking:

false if intentionally zero, true if source missing.

## general_overhead_invalid_amount

Use when an active overhead line has non-numeric or invalid amount.

Blocking:

true.

## general_overhead_review_required

Use when a material overhead line needs review.

Blocking:

depends on materiality.

## asset_finance_exclusion_exceeds_benchmark

Use when excluded asset finance amount is greater than gross benchmark.

Blocking:

true.

## possible_duplicate_asset_cost

Use when a category appears to duplicate asset ownership costs.

Blocking:

review_required.

## possible_duplicate_labour_cost

Use when a category appears to duplicate Labour module wage/on-cost values.

Blocking:

review_required.

## possible_cogs_in_overheads

Use when a category appears to be direct COGS/materials.

Blocking:

review_required.

general_overheads_benchmark_total or adjusted_general_overheads_benchmark

General Overheads

module_amount:
total_general_overheads

variance_amount:
module_amount - source_amount

variance_percent:
variance_amount / source_amount

Default tolerance:

1.00

Status rules:

- balanced if variance = 0.00
- rounding_only if absolute variance <= 1.00
- variance_review if above tolerance but non-blocking
- leak_detected if variance may create hidden unreconciled overhead burden
- blocked if source is invalid or over-exclusion exists

# 48. FINAL PRINCIPLE

General Overheads defines the overhead source pool.

It does not decide how overhead is recovered.

It does not distribute overheads.

It does not replace Labour or Assets.

It provides a traceable overhead burden that downstream modules must allocate, test, and price without exceeding the source pool.

No assumptions.
No hidden overheads.
No duplicate asset cost.
No duplicate labour cost.
No silent exclusions.
No over-allocation.
No leaks.
