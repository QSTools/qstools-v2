# QS Tools — Handover Brief

## Business Setup Page + Two-Mode System + Universal Business Tool Architecture

### Draft v1.0 — Build Handover

---

## STATUS

This is the current handover brief for the next build session.

This brief captures the most important architectural breakthrough so far:

> QS Tools is no longer only a construction/business recovery tool.
> It is a universal business diagnostic engine based on cost recovery through an output driver.

The two core output drivers are:

```text
Hours → Labour / service-based businesses
Units → Product / unit-based businesses
```

Everything else is a variation of those two models.

---

# 1. THE CORE BREAKTHROUGH

## Universal business principle

Every business has the same core problem:

```text
Am I recovering my true business cost through the output I sell?
```

The output may be:

* hours
* units
* products
* subscriptions
* users
* custom builds
* manufactured items
* retail sales

But the model is the same:

```text
Total Business Cost ÷ Activity Driver = Required Recovery Per Output
```

## The two core modes

### 1. Labour-based / service-based

Used when the business primarily sells time, labour, or service output.

Examples:

* builder
* contractor
* consultant
* trade business
* professional services
* labour-driven construction business

Activity driver:

```text
productive_hours
```

Output:

```text
required_recovery_per_hour
```

---

### 2. Product-based / unit-based

Used when the business primarily sells units, products, subscriptions, or productised outputs.

Examples:

* retail store
* online store
* SaaS product
* manufacturer
* productised service
* custom product builder
* subscription business

Activity driver:

```text
units_sold
```

Output:

```text
required_recovery_per_unit
```

---

# 2. CRITICAL NAMING DECISION

Use language that is simple for business owners.

Recommended setup choices:

```text
Service / Labour-based business
Product / Unit-based business
```

Avoid over-specific labels such as:

* retail
* manufacturing
* SaaS
* construction-only

Reason:

Those are industries or business shapes, not the core economic driver.

The setup page should determine how the business sells output, not try to classify every industry.

# 5. REVENUE / COG PAGE — MODE ADAPTATION BRIEF

## Purpose

The Revenue / COG page already represents how the business generates revenue and what direct costs are attached to that revenue.

This is the correct place to add the retail/product unit driver.

Do NOT create a separate Retail Mode page.

---

## Core decision

If the business is product-based, Revenue / COG must show:

```js
units_sold_annual
```

If the business is labour-based, this field must not appear.

---

## Required mode behaviour

### If business_type = labour_based

Revenue / COG page behaves as currently designed.

Do not show units sold.

Do not ask for jobs per year.

Do not add construction-specific job count logic.

Labour-based businesses use productive hours from the Labour / Cost Summary pathway.

---

### If business_type = product_based

Show:

```js
units_sold_annual
```

Label options:

```text
Units sold per year
Annual units sold
Expected units sold per year
```

Recommended label:

```text
Units sold per year
```

Helper text:

```text
Use the total number of products, units, subscriptions, or productised outputs sold in a year.
```

Examples in helper text:

```text
Products sold, subscriptions, users, licences, custom units, manufactured units.
```

---

## What units_sold_annual means

This is the product-based activity driver.

It is NOT:

* revenue
* COGS
* cost per unit
* margin per unit

It is only the denominator used to spread total business cost across product output.

---

## Calculation principle

Do not ask the user for required cost per unit.

The system calculates it.

Formula:

```text
required_recovery_per_unit = total_business_cost / units_sold_annual
```

Where `total_business_cost` comes from the existing Cost Summary / Business Summary flow.

---

## Important correction

Do not confuse:

```text
COGS per unit
```

with:

```text
required recovery per unit
```

COGS is direct product cost.

Required recovery per unit is total business cost spread across units.

Both are useful, but they are not the same thing.

---

## Revenue / COG output additions

If product-based, Revenue / COG should expose:

```js
units_sold_annual
```

Optional if already available or later required:

```js
average_sell_price_per_unit
average_cog_per_unit
average_gross_margin_per_unit
annual_gross_margin
```

Only add optional fields if they already match the current Revenue / COG design.

For the immediate mode unlock, the required field is:

```js
units_sold_annual
```

# 6. BUSINESS SUMMARY — MODE ADAPTATION BRIEF

## Purpose

Business Summary must become mode-aware.

It currently assumes a labour/hour model.

It must now support:

```text
$/hour for labour-based businesses
$/unit for product-based businesses
```

---

## Core rule

Use one shared concept:

```js
activity_driver
```

Do not create separate downstream formulas everywhere for hours and units.

---

## Activity driver behaviour

### If business_type = labour_based

```js
activity_driver_type = "hours"
activity_driver_label = "productive hours"
activity_driver_value = total_productive_output
required_recovery_per_driver = total_cost_burden / total_productive_output
```

Display as:

```text
Required recovery per hour
```

---

### If business_type = product_based

```js
activity_driver_type = "units"
activity_driver_label = "units sold"
activity_driver_value = units_sold_annual
required_recovery_per_driver = total_cost_burden / units_sold_annual
```

Display as:

```text
Required recovery per unit
```

---

## Shared formula

```text
required_recovery_per_driver = total_cost_burden / activity_driver_value
```

Where:

```text
activity_driver_value = productive hours OR units sold
```

---

## Required Business Summary outputs

Business Summary should expose a mode-aware output contract similar to:

```js
{
  business_type,
  activity_driver_type,
  activity_driver_label,
  activity_driver_value,
  total_cost_burden,
  required_recovery_per_driver,
  required_recovery_label,
  required_recovery_unit_label
}
```

Example labels:

### Labour-based

```js
required_recovery_label = "Required recovery per hour"
required_recovery_unit_label = "$/hour"
```

### Product-based

```js
required_recovery_label = "Required recovery per unit"
required_recovery_unit_label = "$/unit"
```

---

## Required warnings

### Labour-based warning

If `total_productive_output` is zero or missing:

```text
No productive hours available. Required recovery per hour cannot be calculated.
```

### Product-based warning

If `units_sold_annual` is zero or missing:

```text
Units sold are required to calculate required recovery per unit.
```

---

## UI behaviour

Business Summary should adapt labels and headline cards based on mode.

### Labour-based display

Show:

* total cost burden
* productive hours
* required recovery per hour

### Product-based display

Show:

* total cost burden
* units sold per year
* required recovery per unit

---

## Do not duplicate the page

Do not create separate Business Summary pages.

One Business Summary page must adapt based on `business_type`.


### Recovery Summary

Examples:

Will adapt diagnosis language.

Examples:

* labour-based: labour recovery shortfall

### Business Modelling

Will adapt levers and labels.

Examples:

* labour-based: productivity, labour rate, labour sales
* product-based: units sold, product margin, price per unit

---

## Pages not affected by mode

These should remain unchanged as cost/source modules:

* P&L
* General Overheads
* Labour
* Assets
* Cost Summary

Reason:

Mode affects how cost is distributed and interpreted, not how cost is collected.

# 8. FUTURE BUILD — UNIVERSAL BUSINESS TOOL BRIEF

## The universal business model

QS Tools should now be positioned internally as a universal business diagnostic tool.

It works because every business can be reduced to:

```text
Cost Base
+
Output Driver
+
Recovery Strategy
+
Delivery Structure
+
Outcome Diagnosis
```

---

## Core equation

```text
Total Business Cost ÷ Output Driver = Required Recovery Per Output
```

Where output driver is:

```text
hours
or
units
```

---

## Why this applies widely

### Construction / service

```text
Total cost ÷ productive hours = required recovery per hour
```

### Retail

```text
Total cost ÷ units sold = required recovery per unit
```

### SaaS

```text
Total cost ÷ subscriptions/users = required recovery per user
```

### Manufacturing

```text
Total cost ÷ units produced/sold = required recovery per unit
```

### Productised service

```text
Total cost ÷ productised outputs = required recovery per output
```

---

## Do not dilute the tool

Do not add industry-specific engines.

Do not create separate logic for:

* retail
* manufacturing
* SaaS
* construction
* consulting

Instead, keep the architecture:

```text
Business type = labour-based or product-based
Activity driver = hours or units
Structure relevance = none / light / active
```

This keeps the tool universal without becoming bloated.

# 9. PRODUCT PRICING CHECK — FUTURE EXTENSION

The existing quote-checking idea can be generalised.

It should become a pricing validation engine.

## Labour-based check

Question:

```text
Is this quote priced correctly?
```

Uses:

* hours
* labour rate
* required recovery per hour

---

## Product-based check

Question:

```text
Is this product priced correctly?
```

Uses:

* sell price per unit
* COGS per unit
* expected units sold
* required recovery per unit

---

## Required product check levels

The product check should work at two levels:

### 1. Per-unit check

Shows:

* sell price per unit
* COGS per unit
* gross margin per unit
* required recovery per unit
* per-unit shortfall or surplus

### 2. Volume check

Shows:

* expected units sold
* total gross margin
* required recovery contribution
* total shortfall or surplus

Reason:

A product can look profitable per unit but still fail if volume is too low.

# 11. COST ALLOCATION — FUTURE POSITION

Cost Allocation must come after Recovery Summary.

Purpose:

```text
Test whether the business structure can deliver the recovery plan.
```

Important:

Cost Allocation should not be hidden purely because the business is product-based.

Instead, Cost Allocation should be active only when production/delivery structure matters.

Examples:

### Product-based pure retail / SaaS

* cost allocation passive/minimal
* no asset/labour production structure required

### Product-based manufacturing

* cost allocation active
* assets/labour produce the units

### Labour-based construction / service

* cost allocation active if labour/assets structure matters

* viable / marginal / not viable

* cause
* lever
* expected effect

Core diagnostic chain:

```text
Outcome → Driver → Cause → Lever → Expected Effect
```
