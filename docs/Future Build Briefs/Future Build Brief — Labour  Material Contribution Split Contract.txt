# Future Build Brief — Labour / Material Contribution Split Contract

## Purpose

This future build defines the data contract required to show the true split between:

```text
Labour contribution
Material contribution
Other contribution
```

inside Business Summary.

This should not be built yet as a calculation layer.

For now, Business Summary can include future-ready hierarchy nodes, but the true labour/material split must remain marked as pending until quote, job, invoice, and claimed labour-hour data are connected.

---

# Core principle

Business Summary can currently show:

```text
Revenue
- COG / Direct Costs
= Gross Profit / Margin Pool

Gross Profit / Margin Pool
- Operating Cost Baseline
= Surplus / Deficit
```

But Business Summary cannot yet safely prove:

```text
How much of the margin pool came from labour
How much came from materials
How much came from other sources
```

because the system does not yet know:

```text
How many labour hours were actually claimed / invoiced
How many labour hours were actually used
How the quote split revenue between labour and materials
```

So the contribution split must be future-ready only.

---

# Business Summary current wording

Use this pending message:

```text
Full labour and material margin split becomes available once claimed labour hours and quote/job data are connected.
```

Alternative shorter version:

```text
Contribution split pending. Requires claimed labour hours and quote/job data.
```

---

# Business Summary future hierarchy

Business Summary should eventually support:

```text
Business Result
→ Revenue Generated
→ COG / Direct Costs
→ Gross Profit / Margin Pool
→ Operating Cost Baseline
→ Surplus / Deficit
```

Then under:

```text
Gross Profit / Margin Pool
```

show:

```text
Labour Contribution
Material Contribution
Other Contribution
```

For now, these nodes can exist as pending nodes only.

Do not calculate or display final values until the data contract below is available.

---

# Future data contract

## Claimed / invoiced labour fields

These describe what the business actually billed or claimed for labour.

```js
claimed_labour_hours
claimed_labour_revenue
claimed_labour_rate
```

Definitions:

```text
claimed_labour_hours = labour hours charged / claimed to the customer
claimed_labour_revenue = labour revenue charged to the customer
claimed_labour_rate = claimed_labour_revenue / claimed_labour_hours
```

Important distinction:

```text
claimed_labour_hours are not the same as recovery_hours.
```

Recovery hours are business capacity.

Claimed labour hours are what was actually charged or recovered through invoices / quotes.

---

## Actual labour used fields

These describe the labour actually consumed by the job or business activity.

```js
actual_labour_hours_used
actual_labour_cost
```

Definitions:

```text
actual_labour_hours_used = labour hours actually consumed by the work
actual_labour_cost = cost of labour used on the work
```

This may come from:

```text
timesheets
job tracking
quote actuals
invoice processor
manual job inputs
```

---

## Labour contribution fields

These describe what labour actually contributed.

```js
labour_contribution
labour_contribution_percent
labour_recovery_gap
```

Future formulas:

```text
labour_contribution =
claimed_labour_revenue - actual_labour_cost
```

```text
labour_contribution_percent =
labour_contribution / claimed_labour_revenue
```

```text
labour_recovery_gap =
claimed_labour_revenue - required_labour_recovery
```

Do not calculate these until claimed labour and actual labour data are available.

---

## Material contribution fields

These describe direct material contribution.

```js
material_revenue
material_cost
material_contribution
material_contribution_percent
```

Future formulas:

```text
material_contribution =
material_revenue - material_cost
```

```text
material_contribution_percent =
material_contribution / material_revenue
```

This is safe once material revenue and material cost are cleanly available.

Do not infer material revenue from total revenue unless the Revenue / COG or quote layer explicitly provides it.

---

## Other contribution fields

These describe contribution from revenue streams that are not clearly labour or material.

```js
other_revenue
other_cost
other_contribution
other_contribution_percent
```

Future formulas:

```text
other_contribution =
other_revenue - other_cost
```

```text
other_contribution_percent =
other_contribution / other_revenue
```

Examples may include:

```text
hire income
admin fees
delivery charges
service fees
miscellaneous revenue
```

---

# Future allocated margin fields

These are the full-model outputs once the system can allocate the recovery gap properly.

```js
allocated_labour_margin
allocated_material_margin
allocated_other_margin
```

Percent fields:

```js
allocated_labour_margin_percent
allocated_material_margin_percent
allocated_other_margin_percent
```

These should only be calculated when the full quote/job/time loop exists.

They should not be guessed from P&L totals alone.

---

# Availability flag

Business Summary should eventually receive:

```js
contribution_split_available
```

Definition:

```text
true only when claimed labour hours, claimed labour revenue, material revenue, material cost, and quote/job data are connected strongly enough to calculate contribution split safely.
```

Until then:

```js
contribution_split_available = false
```

When false, Business Summary should display pending messaging, not fake values.

---

# Important modelling rule

Do not force the entire business gap onto materials.

A low Business Summary result may be caused by:

```text
labour under-recovery
material margin
overhead load
non-productive labour
productivity loss
quote leakage
a combination of all of the above
```

Business Summary should detect the gap.

Business Modelling should test the levers.

Quote / Job Checker should prove the result against real work.

---

# Future workflow

The future full loop should be:

```text
Cost Summary
→ Required recovery baseline

Business Summary
→ Actual performance and margin pool

Business Modelling
→ Test which levers close the gap

Quote / Job Checker
→ Check whether jobs meet the required recovery

Quote / Job / Time Actuals
→ Measure claimed labour, actual labour, material cost, and real contribution split
```

---

# Final definition

The future Labour / Material Contribution Split build should answer:

```text
How much margin is really coming from labour?
How much margin is really coming from materials?
Are we under-claiming labour, under-marking materials, or both?
```

Until the data exists, Business Summary must show the structure as pending, not calculated.
