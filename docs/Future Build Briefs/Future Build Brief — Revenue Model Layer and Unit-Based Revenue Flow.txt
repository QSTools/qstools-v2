# Future Build Brief — Revenue Model Layer and Unit-Based Revenue Flow

STATUS: FUTURE BUILD BRIEF
PURPOSE: Define the missing Revenue Model layer and clarify how unit-based / rate-based / labour-based revenue should flow into Business Summary and Recovery Outcome.

---

## 1. WHY THIS BRIEF EXISTS

During the Revenue / COGS, Business Summary, and Recovery Summary mode-awareness work, we identified that the system is still missing one major layer:

Revenue Model

The current system can now show:
- labour-mode recovery pressure
- product/unit-mode recovery pressure
- margin per unit
- required units to break even
- unit surplus / shortfall

But the system does not yet formally define how the business earns money.

Revenue / COGS currently gives the macro P&L view:
- Revenue
- COGS / Direct Costs
- Margin Pool

But it should not become a detailed pricing engine.

Therefore, a separate Revenue Model layer is required.

---

## 2. CURRENT SYSTEM POSITION

Current core flow:

Cost Summary
→ Recovery Summary
→ Cost Allocation
→ Business Summary / Recovery Outcome

Future required flow:

Cost Summary
→ Cost Allocation
→ Revenue Model
→ Business Summary
→ Recovery Summary / Recovery Outcome

Important clarification:
The exact page order can be finalised later, but conceptually the system needs both:
- cost structure
- revenue structure

before it can make a proper outcome judgement.

---

## 3. CORE PRINCIPLE

Cost Allocation defines:

What does it cost to deliver the unit?

Revenue Model defines:

What do we charge for the unit?

Business Summary / Outcome compares:

Does what we charge cover what it costs?

---

## 4. REVENUE MODEL DEFINITION

Revenue Model = how the business turns output into money.

It must support multiple revenue mechanisms:

1. Labour-driven revenue
   - hours × $/hour

2. Unit-driven revenue
   - units × $/unit
   - m² × $/m²
   - m³ × $/m³
   - transactions × average sale

3. Fixed / lump-sum revenue
   - total revenue entered directly

4. Hybrid revenue
   - multiple revenue streams combined

---

## 5. BUSINESS SETUP MODE CHANGE

The current “product-based” naming is too narrow.

Replace or reinterpret as:

Primary Revenue Driver

Options:
- Labour-driven
- Unit-driven
- Mixed / Hybrid

Where:

Labour-driven =
Revenue mainly comes from productive labour hours.

Unit-driven =
Revenue mainly comes from a repeatable output unit:
- products
- m²
- m³
- tonnes
- transactions
- jobs
- other custom unit

Mixed / Hybrid =
Revenue comes from multiple mechanisms.

Important:
Mode sets defaults and guidance only.
It must not restrict the user from adding other revenue streams.

Rule:
Mode = starting point.
Revenue streams = reality.

---

## 6. UNIT-BASED REVENUE MODEL

Unit-based revenue covers:

- retail products
- café transactions
- concrete laying by m²
- concrete pumping by m³
- gib fixing by m²
- cladding by m²
- any repeatable output unit

Core formula:

Revenue = Output Volume × Rate

Examples:

Retail:
transactions × average sale

Concrete laying:
m²/year × $/m²

Concrete pumping:
m³/year × effective $/m³

Labour:
productive hours × $/hour

The calculation is the same.
Only the unit changes.

Rule:
Change the unit, not the maths.

---

## 7. REQUIRED UNIT FIELD

Business Setup or Revenue Model must define:

primary_output_unit

Examples:
- hr
- unit
- transaction
- m²
- m³
- tonne
- job
- custom

This unit must flow through:
- Revenue / COGS
- Business Summary
- Recovery Summary
- Recovery Outcome

So the system displays:
- $/m²
- m²/year
- required m²
- m² shortfall

instead of generic “units”.

---

## 8. REVENUE / COGS ROLE

Revenue / COGS should remain macro.

It should show:
- total revenue
- total COGS / direct costs
- margin pool
- optional high-level unit driver

It should not become:
- a quoting engine
- a job pricing engine
- a detailed revenue stream builder
- a calculator page

Revenue / COGS may accept:
- total revenue
- output volume
- rate

But the detailed revenue structure belongs in Revenue Model.

---

## 9. REVENUE MODEL INPUT LOGIC

The Revenue Model should allow the user to choose what they know.

For a unit-based stream, the user should not need to enter both volume and rate freely if revenue is already fixed.

Supported input modes:

A. I know my volume

User enters:
- output volume

System derives:
- implied rate = revenue / output volume

B. I know my rate

User enters:
- rate per unit

System derives:
- implied volume = revenue / rate

C. I know both rate and volume

System derives:
- revenue = rate × volume

This mode should be used when the Revenue Model is producing revenue, rather than reconciling to a known P&L revenue total.

Core rule:
Fix one, solve the other.
Do not let all values float freely without showing reconciliation.

---

## 10. CALCULATORS / HELPERS

Calculators are optional helpers.

They should help the user estimate current reality.

They should not own the model.

Rule:
Calculator estimates current reality.
Business Summary calculates required reality.

Calculator examples:

Concrete pump helper:
- hourly setup rate
- pump hourly rate
- m³ rate
- average jobs
- average m³
- estimated annual m³
- effective $/m³

Concrete laying helper:
- average $/m²
- minimum job charge
- typical job size
- jobs per year
- effective annual revenue
- effective $/m²

Café / retail helper:
- average transaction value
- transactions per day
- trading days per year
- annual revenue

These should feed or suggest Revenue Model inputs, but not become the core stored logic unless explicitly saved.

Rule:
Calculators convert real-world thinking into model inputs.
Revenue streams store the model inputs.

---

## 11. REVENUE STREAMS

Revenue Model should support multiple revenue streams.

Each stream should have:

- revenue_stream_id
- revenue_stream_name
- revenue_stream_type
- output_unit
- input_mode
- annual_volume
- rate_per_unit
- annual_revenue
- direct_costs / COGS if applicable
- margin_pool contribution

Revenue stream types:
- labour_rate
- unit_rate
- transaction
- fixed_revenue
- hybrid/manual

Examples:

Concrete business:
Stream 1 — Concrete laying
- unit: m²
- rate: $/m²
- volume: m²/year

Stream 2 — Concrete pumping
- unit: m³
- rate: $/m³
- volume: m³/year

Stream 3 — Concrete supply
- unit: m³
- rate: $/m³
- COGS included

Cafe:
Stream 1 — Store transactions
- unit: transaction
- average sale
- transactions/year

Gib fixing:
Stream 1 — Walls
- unit: m²
- rate: $/m²

Stream 2 — Ceilings
- unit: m²
- rate: $/m²

For Phase 1, avoid forcing detailed streams.
Allow simple blended streams.

---

## 12. SIMPLE VS ADVANCED MODEL

Default should be simple.

Simple mode:
- one blended revenue stream
- one unit
- one rate or total revenue
- one volume

Advanced mode:
- multiple streams
- stream mix
- optional calculators
- optional direct cost allocation

Important:
If the model requires real job data to be accurate, keep it out of Phase 1.

Detailed category-level accuracy belongs later in:
- Quote Engine
- Work Orders
- Job Costing
- Live Data Feedback

---

## 13. RECONCILIATION RULE

Revenue Model should be able to reconcile against Revenue / COGS.

If Revenue / COGS has a known P&L revenue total:

Revenue Model streams should show:

- modelled revenue
- P&L revenue
- variance

This prevents the model from drifting away from known financial reality.

---

## 14. DOWNSTREAM USE

Business Summary should consume Revenue Model outputs once built.

Business Summary should compare:
- total revenue
- total COGS
- margin pool
- total cost burden
- unit-based recovery requirement

For unit-based businesses, Business Summary should show:
- rate per unit
- margin per unit
- required units to break even
- actual / expected units
- unit surplus / shortfall

Recovery Summary should interpret:
- required recovery per hour for labour mode
- required recovery per unit for unit-driven mode
- both where hybrid

Recovery Outcome should only make final viability calls once:
- cost structure is available
- revenue model is available
- cost allocation structure is ready where required

---

## 15. WARNING CLASSIFICATION

Warnings should be split into:

1. Setup / assignment warnings
   - missing links
   - missing units
   - missing stream inputs
   - incomplete allocation

2. Structural warnings
   - incomplete operational groups
   - weak coverage
   - missing assets / labour for selected recovery model

3. Commercial warnings
   - margin too low
   - unit shortfall
   - revenue below cost burden
   - not enough trading margin

Setup warnings should feel like a checklist, not a failure.

Rule:
If the user can fix it by assigning or entering something, it is a setup warning, not a business failure.

---

## 16. KEY DECISIONS LOCKED FROM DISCUSSION

1. Product business, m² business, m³ business, transaction business are all unit-driven businesses.

2. The calculation is the same:
   Revenue = Output × Rate

3. The unit changes:
   units, m², m³, transactions, hours, jobs.

4. Revenue / COGS should stay macro.

5. A new Revenue Model layer is needed before final Business Outcome can be fully trusted.

6. Calculators are optional helpers, not the core model.

7. Cost Allocation defines the delivery/cost unit.

8. Revenue Model defines what is charged for that unit.

9. Business Summary compares cost and revenue.

10. Recovery Outcome should not make hard final judgement until the relevant setup is complete.

---

## 17. FUTURE BUILD TARGET

Build a Revenue Model layer that:

- supports simple blended revenue streams
- supports unit-based output labels
- supports revenue-first or unit/rate-first entry
- reconciles against Revenue / COGS
- feeds Business Summary
- prepares Recovery Outcome for final viability decision

Do not overbuild the calculator layer in Phase 1.

Phase 1 must stay macro and usable.

---

## ONE-LINE DEFINITION

Revenue Model = the layer that defines how the business turns output into revenue, using flexible streams, units, and rates, without becoming a quoting engine.
